# Dominique Ferraro

> [!danger] DM-only
> This note is now spoiler material. To the players, Dominique is [[Bash Whitlock|Bash]]'s cool older cousin and the party's trusted aboveground "heart." She is secretly [[Wesley Crane|Wesley]]'s apprentice/heir and the ritual's bloodline anchor — the most personal betrayal in Act 1. Seed every step of it as *good news;* the reread is what hurts.

**[[Bash Whitlock|Bash]]'s older cousin (~18) — bookish but well-liked, the bridge between the nerds and the popular crowd — established early as the PCs' "woman in the van" aboveground, and revealed late as [[Wesley Crane|Wesley]]'s chosen heir and the [[The Equinox Ritual|ritual]]'s [[Elias Mercer|Mercer]]-bloodline anchor.** *(She absorbs the role formerly held by the placeholder "Sam.")*

## Who she is (the players' view)
~18, a Ferraro (Bash's maternal cousin). **Bookish and scholarly** — she shares [[Bash Whitlock|Bash]]'s curiosity, which is why he idolizes her — but also genuinely *liked:* pretty, easy in any room, welcome among nerds and popular kids alike. She got Bash into computers; she's [[Tank]]'s crush. Early in Act 1 she becomes the party's **aboveground operator** — chasing leads, checking on people, holding the town side together while they delve. The bright, real, outside-the-dungeon thing the cage-carriers orbit. *(This replaces the earlier "social-butterfly free spirit" read: she's a bookish bridge who's liked, not a party girl.)*

## The pressure-point — an absence, not a wound
Dominique isn't broken, so Wesley's cure-the-broken-leg pitch ([[The Cast and the Permanent Cast]]) doesn't work on her. Her crack is **rootlessness:** the universal bridge belongs *everywhere and nowhere* — welcome in every circle, a member of none; many loose ties, no deep one; never rejected, never truly held. [[The Community Center]] is the first place that feels like a **family with her near its center**, and [[Wesley Crane|Wesley]] — who has spent his whole life alone in his clarity — offers her the one thing no one else can: **not healing, but partnership.** He finally meets a young mind that keeps up with his and grooms her as an **apprentice / intellectual heir.** For a girl who has only ever belonged by translating herself into each room, *"here you don't have to translate — and you'll help me run it"* is the perfect key. *(Rhymes against [[Gern]]: both crave real belonging over loose belonging — Gern is offered a* seat*, Dominique a* throne beside the king*. Seducing her is the seduction* of Wesley *mirrored: his deepest unmet hunger isn't to be loved, it's to be understood and joined by an equal.)*

## The fall — gradual, clear-eyed, on-screen
She **becomes**, by degrees, in plain sight: belonging → status → (when she glimpses what Wesley is really doing) rationalization, because by then the group is too much of a family to betray and she's too important in it to leave. She is **not hollowed and not a victim** — she keeps full agency the whole way, which is the horror: she chose it, step by step, while the party wasn't looking. Every step looks like good news in the moment (*"she's been spending a lot of time at the center lately"* reads as wholesome). The design rule: **every step of her drift must look, in the moment, like good news.**

> [!danger] The woman-in-the-van betrayal
> The party hands Dominique the town-side follow-ups — including checking on the Rivals (*"Dominique's been looking out for [[Zander Pruitt|Zander]] since [episode] — glad someone is"*). They think she's helping the Rivals heal and warm toward the party. She's walking each one **further into Wesley's family.** Every act of trust they place in her is a thread she uses to pull a Rival away — they aren't failing to protect the Rivals from Wesley, they're *delivering* them, through the person they trust most. It was visible all along, framed as good news.

## Bash's precognition payoff (true but misread)
The seed in [[Bash Whitlock|Bash]]'s arc — his window showing **"something terrible happening to Dominique"** — pays off here. The window is *accurate* (her at the ritual / aligned with Wesley) but Bash reads it as something being *done to* her and sets out to **save** her. The unbearable turn: it isn't being done to her — **she chose it, in plain sight, while he was reading her as a system to track instead of a person to talk to.** His instrument was never wrong; his interpretation was — the exact failure his flaw sets up (*the unreadable isn't a gap in the instrument; it's the signature of a free choice*). His whole "save Dominique" mission rests on a false premise the campaign eventually detonates.

## Mechanical role (absorbed from the dissolved "Sam")
- **Bloodline anchor:** [[The Equinox Ritual|the ritual]] cannot complete without a [[Elias Mercer|Mercer]]-line participant; she's it. **Exempt from the hollowing qualifier** — the ritual needs her *blood,* not a hollowing (her lack of the "lost-a-week" look is a quiet tell of her status).
- **Continuation-regardless:** if [[Wesley Crane|Wesley]] is killed pre-equinox, **Dominique completes the ritual** — the gate opens whether he lives or dies, *unless she herself is pulled back.* See [[The Other Party (Rival or Evil)]].
- **Reachable — but only just.** She isn't won by Time-Points disposition like the recruits (she has full agency; she chose). She's reachable *only* if a PC refuses the misdirection and **sees her** — confronts the person, not the asset. If reached (Interest 4+), the bloodline anchor refuses and the **ritual cannot complete** (the highest-leverage equinox outcome). [[Wesley Crane|Wesley]] himself stays unreachable; she is the rival party's one reachable heart. If the ritual succeeds, she's **corrupted, not sacrificed** — she continues life as a corrupted heir.
- **Re-homes [[Eddie Park]]'s former "into-the-Mercer-papers" thread** — bookish enough to follow it into the [[The Mercer Mansion|Mercer papers]] herself, which is partly how Wesley spots his heir.
