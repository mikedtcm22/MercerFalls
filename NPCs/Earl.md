# Earl

Up: [[Cast & NPCs]]

**Pawn-shop owner in The Flats — the town's accidental witness, who can't act on what he sees but *catalogues* it, keeping a private ledger of the hollowed count.**

> [!warning] Provisional
> Name confirmed; personality, mannerisms, and the trigger point for the "hollowed count" reveal are still to be designed (bible §20 item 21).

## Profile
Owner of [[Earl's Pawn Shop]] in The Flats. He runs one of the town's cash-conversion channels for dungeon loot — **fast cash, low rates, no questions** (see the money loop, [[Gear and Items]] / [[Time Points]]). Introduced naturally in [[Episode 2 — The Hungry House]] via [[Henry Voss]], who's been pawning things steadily for years.

## Function in the campaign
Earl's specific value: **he sees the town accurately because he's its accidental witness.** Hollowed locals all pawn things eventually — they don't notice they're running through their savings — and Earl has noticed *the pattern*. He doesn't use the word "hollowed," but he keeps a private ledger and can hint that he knows accurate counts of the town's hollowed. He has the witness role but **not the Filter-permission to act** on what he sees (see [[The Reality Filter]], [[Hollowing and Agency-Loss]]); he just catalogues. PCs who invest [[Time Points]] in Earl can gradually surface his ledger as an investigative resource — a quiet, powerful confirmation of the [[The Pattern Recognition Arc]].
