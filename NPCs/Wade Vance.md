# Wade Vance

Up: [[Cast & NPCs]]

**[[Ricky Vance|Ricky]]'s father: active-duty military, almost never home, idolized by the son who's spent his life imitating him — and, unknown to the town or even his own family, a great deal more dangerous and more connected than a small-town absentee dad has any right to be. The PCs meet him at the [[The Hush]]'s den, where he turns out to be the most capable person in the room.**

> [!note] Proposed name
> "Wade" is a placeholder first name, trivially swappable. The surname Vance is fixed (Ricky's family).

## Profile (what the town sees)
40s. Deployed or "away for work" most of the year — the kind of absence the town reads as an absentee father, maybe a deadbeat, and that his family has stopped expecting to end. Whatever cover travels with him is mundane (a reservist; "does security work"; a recruiter two counties over). When he *is* home he's all quiet discipline, and [[Ricky Vance|Ricky]] worships that discipline and emulates it to hold the household together (see Ricky's note). The **military-school threat** hanging over Ricky was Wade's — almost certainly never serious, a disciplinarian's bluff the kid amplified into a sword over his own head. The frazzled, present parent is Ricky's mom; Wade is the idealized, absent one.

## The truth (DM-only)
> [!danger] DM-only — the badass under the absentee
> Wade is a senior operator in a **classified unit** — far more skilled, and far more plugged in, than anyone in Mercer Falls could guess, his family included. The absences were never neglect; they were deployments he could never explain. Ricky built an entire identity emulating a discipline that's a pale shadow of the real thing, idolizing a father whose actual life he doesn't know exists. The love was always real — just buried under operational silence.
>
> **Show it, don't tell it.** At the den the PCs should *clock* it rather than be told: he's already mapped the place before they arrive; he kills the lights and the noise on instinct; he handles his sidearm like breathing; he field-dresses a gash without looking down; he gives the kids calm, exact orders that simply *work*; afterward he makes a call from a payphone in language that isn't quite civilian. Target reaction: *"whoa — Ricky's dad is kind of a badass."*

## Filter resistance — why he found what no one else could
The town is fogged into "wild dog / shipped off to military school." Wade isn't. **Love broke the [[The Reality Filter|Filter]] for him** (the same attachment-rule that cracked [[Russ]]) — but where Russ is merely disquieted, Wade is *trained*, so he could act on it: he didn't just feel something was wrong, he tracked the thing to its den. A Filter-broken adult who is also lethally competent and motivated is rare — and, though he has no idea [[Wesley Crane|Wesley]] exists, exactly the kind of loose variable that could wreck an operation.

## In Episode 2 — the rescue
He converges on the Hush's den alongside the PCs (see [[Episode 2 — The Hush]]) and plays a hand in the kill. Mechanically he's a strong ally; *narratively* most of the **public** glory goes to him ("his dad saved him"), which is why the rescue doesn't ingratiate Ricky with the party in public — but the kids' actions decide the outcome, and that's what Ricky privately clocks. His fate sets Ricky's disposition and trajectory:
- **Clean** (Wade unhurt): he lives — but duty pulls him away again, so Ricky's *true* anchor exists yet stays absent, and Wesley still has an opening (just a less raw one). Wade survives as a latent counterweight and a faction vector.
- **Wounded** (the botch): the strong father becomes a **broken, present** one — laid up, diminished. This collapses Ricky's situation toward [[Tank]]'s (a once-mighty dad who's now a weight) without Tank's coping, and clears Wesley's path. It also removes the faction vector (an operator invalided out).
- **Killed** (egregious failure only): the anchor is gone; the hardest shove into Wesley — and the strongest trigger for the seed below.

> [!note] Encounter-support statline (not a full block — reskin freely)
> Tuned to *enable* a level-1 party against a tough Hush, not to solo it. AC **15** · HP **44** · Speed **30 ft.** · STR 14 (+2) · DEX 16 (+3) · CON 13 (+1) · INT 12 (+1) · WIS 15 (+2) · CHA 11 (+0) · Saves Dex +5, Wis +4 · Skills Stealth +7, Perception +6, Survival +6, Athletics +4 · passive Perception 16.
> - **Noise Discipline.** Wade moves in near silence. Sound-hunters — including [[The Hush]] — have disadvantage to detect, track, or attack him in any round he hasn't fired a gun. He can Hide as a bonus action.
> - **Nerves of Cold Iron.** Can't be frightened; advantage against being surprised.
> - **Read the Field.** Once per round (no action) he murmurs a cue to one ally who can hear him; that ally has advantage on their next attack this round against a creature Wade can see. *(His "strike — now" beat, and mechanically how the kids land the decisive hits while the public story credits Wade.)*
> - **Multiattack:** two attacks. **Combat Knife** +5, 1d6+3 piercing (silent). **Sidearm** +5, range 40/120 ft., 1d10+3 piercing — *loud:* firing forfeits Noise Discipline that round and can draw the Hush, a real tradeoff against a creature that hunts by sound.
> - **Reaction — Cover the Kids.** When an ally he can see within 30 ft. is hit, he can impose disadvantage on that attack.
> Keep the *outcome band* (clean / wounded / killed) hanging on the players' choices, not Wade's dice.

> [!danger] DM-only — Tier-3 faction seed: Wade's unit
> Optional payoff, planted now and deniable. If the players light up at "who *is* this guy," there's a **classified outfit** behind him to bring in around Tier 3 — a mundane, cosmology-blind, extremely capable human faction operating on a totally different axis from [[Order and Chaos]], able to shake the board mid-campaign. Loose entry vectors (pick later, or never): (a) Wade goes missing/KIA and the unit comes looking for one of their own, walking into the anomaly blind; (b) Mercer Falls / the Mercer site is *already* on some old classified watchlist (a decades-long disappearance pattern, a flagged "signature"), making Wade's posting here not wholly coincidental; (c) Wade himself, after the rescue, quietly flags the town up his chain. Don't build it unless it earns its place.
