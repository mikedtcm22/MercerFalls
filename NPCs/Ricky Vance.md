# Ricky Vance

Up: [[Cast & NPCs]]

**17, the dare-night bully whom the Filter couldn't fully clean — something stuck, and [[Wesley Crane]] is feeding his unease a framework that names it "insight."**

## Profile
17. A jock — crewmate of [[Tank]] — and the **one who proposed the prank** on the night of [[Session 1 — The Dare]]: scare [[Bash Whitlock]] and [[Gern]] into leaving the mill within an hour. He came back to the grounds to witness them chickening out, found **no one** (the party had already fled below), and went looking — which left him alone on the grounds when [[The Watcher]] (the Hush) broke off from the hatch and hunted *him* instead. He survived, but [[The Reality Filter]] couldn't fully clean a night that raw — something stuck. He shows up at [[The Community Center]] within a couple weeks of Session 1, fresh and raw. Wesley clocks him fast and reframes his unease as insight. By August Ricky has quit his summer job to help full-time.

## Arc / function
Featured in [[Episode 1 — The Watcher]] (the dare-night hunt is his cold-open POV). The **most plausibly wedge-able** recruit — but also the most likely to escalate to violence on the failure path. The hook is confrontation and recognition: the choice *not* to write him off. Handling the dare-night confrontation with care is a key PC skill. After Episode 1, [[Wesley Crane]] begins counter-cultivating him.

> [!danger] DM-only
> **Negotiation profile (provisional).** Motivations: Justice (he knows he failed; Wesley reframes failure as insight — the PCs can offer a different way), Higher Authority (he's desperate to find one), Vengeance (against whatever made him this way). Pitfalls: Revelry ("lighten up, normal-kid stuff" reads as not seeing him), Discovery (he doesn't want to face the painful truth about what's happening to him). Reachable through: acknowledgment — "*you knew that night was wrong before any of us did*" hits because it's true. **Wesley's counter-cultivation** (after Ep1): any PC confrontation about dare night implicitly pitfalls Ricky with shame; Wesley exploits it — "*they only see who you used to be.*" His hollowing is *instrumental* to [[The Equinox Ritual]]. See [[The Negotiation System]].
>
> Note he sits on the prank truth too: a confrontation that names *him* as the one who set up Bash & Gern is double-loaded (shame + the fracture inside [[The Party]]).
