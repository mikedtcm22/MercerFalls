# Russ

Up: [[Cast & NPCs]]

**Owner of [[Panels]], the campaign's monster-research hub — sardonic-with-warmth horror obsessive, Filter-touched without knowing it, and the strong candidate for the first significant Filter-break.**

## Profile
27 in summer 1996 (born 1969); last name open. Dropped out of OSU in fall '88 at 19 (dropout reason deliberately open). Lean, average height, dark hair overdue for a cut, smudged glasses, band/movie tees under unbuttoned flannel. **Sardonic on the surface, melancholy underneath** — never mean; treats kids as future best customers. Talks in references; runs [[Panels]] like a guy who'd rather have a conversation than make a sale (which is why it barely breaks even). Dialog tics: trailing self-interruption ("*It's like — well, you've seen — anyway*"), "*See, the thing is—*" as his lore-bridge, quiet humming when working alone.

> [!note] Bible date correction
> Bible §12 (line 1204) says Russ "dropped out of OSU in '78." This conflicts with the load-bearing "twenty-two, three years out of his OSU semester [in 1991]" line. Correct reading is **'88**; the '91 / age-22 anchor is canonical (corroborated by bizarro v5 §7 Ep4).

## The 1991 anchor
His older sister **Annie** (24, worked the line) was one of the seven who died in [[The 1991 Mill Incident]]. Russ was 22. Her death is why he stayed in Mercer Falls. A small framed photo of Annie in her mill uniform sits on a back-room shelf, not prominent. This anchors him to the campaign's underlying tragedy: when he finally puts it together, *it explains his sister.*

## Function: monster-research hub
Under [[The Nightmare Engine]], [[Panels]] is the de-facto monster reference library — horror-VHS wall, horror comics, *Famous Monsters* runs, paperback rack, a back-room gaming table. Deepest love: late-70s–mid-80s practical-effects creature features. When a PC describes a monster he often recognizes it from memory; otherwise he points them at the right shelf. **Panels gives an *edge* (canonical weakness, kill condition) but is never required** — per [[The Nightmare Engine]], the vulnerability is the most reliable path, not the only one. A trusted resource, not a checkpoint. Costs a [[Time Points|Time Points]] investment.

**The unlock (magic-item source):** Russ becomes a real source of [[Gear and Items]] only when all three are true — sustained rapport ([[Time Points]] over multiple sessions), he's bought a dungeon antique he's clocked as "real," and he's decided they're his kind of customer. Then acquisition runs Purchase / Trade / Gift (gift = best, via a favor done for him).

## Back-room collection
Curios on the gaming-table shelves: most decor, a meaningful minority real. Named pieces: the **[[The Mercer Mirror]]** (estate-sale buy '89, has started showing figures that aren't there — pays off in Episode 4) and a **brass compass that never points north** (he thinks it's a broken antique; what it points to is open). Some real Mercer-Society-auction antiques mixed in (an 1880s Mercer ledger, a hand-mirror, a pressed-flower paperweight). Full back-room access is rapport-gated.

> [!danger] DM-only
> **Filter-touched, not Filter-broken** (bible §12, §14). He half-hears anomalous info and reframes it — the "too specific" reframe ("*I mean, like a costume at the mall?*") is the tell; fire it exactly once in the Episode 1 first-contact scene. He is the **only adult in town quietly distrustful of [[Wesley Crane]]** — his half-strength Filter picks up what the town's suppresses. Reserved payoff when his break lands: "*I knew that guy was bad news.*" He is the leading candidate for the first significant [[The Reality Filter]] break (bible §20 item 23); the trigger scene is open (candidates: Annie, the Mirror, Wesley-recognition, the cumulative research weight). Delicious irony to hold: he's an expert on visible, killable monsters — the real horror is invisible and unkillable. *He is an expert on the wrong subject.*
