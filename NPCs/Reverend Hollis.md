# Reverend Hollis

Up: [[Cast & NPCs]]

**A hardline small-town preacher losing his flock to [[Wesley Crane|Wesley]]'s [[The Community Center|Community Center]] — the deliberate red herring for the "hooded figure." Sanctimonious, cold to the kids, and (unbearably) right about the one thing no one will hear him on.**

## Profile
Fifties, pastor of a hardline independent congregation (name TBD — e.g. "Grace Tabernacle"). Robed at services — the hood the players will fixate on — rigid, humorless, given to brimstone. Treats the mill kids as delinquents on principle; fixes them with a probing stare and pointed questions ("*What were you children doing out at that mill?*"). Not warm, not kind — but not violent, and **not the hooded man**.

## Function: the red herring (and the discredited prophet)
Players always suspect the priest, and Hollis is built to *reward* that suspicion so it can be spent harmlessly. He's exactly the sort of authority they'll *want* to dislike.

The turn that makes him more than a decoy: **he is right about Wesley.** His congregation is bleeding to the Community Center, and he rails that "*that man is leading this town to ruin*" — the correct read, delivered by the least credible messenger. Because he's insufferable and transparently motivated by lost tithes, the players discount the one true warning in the noise; and because they're busy suspecting *him*, they don't hear it. Mirrors [[Russ]]' dismissed distrust of Wesley and quietly reinforces [[The Reality Filter|the Filter]] theme: the truth-teller discredited by being unpleasant.

**How the players meet him — the microfilm (self-concealing).** In [[Episode 2 — The Hush]] he's found at **[[The Library]]**, hunched over the microfilm reader, quietly digging **dirt on Wesley** for the rivalry — something he'd never explain to a pack of kids. So his cover is honest ("a man doing some reading"), he **clams up when they get close,** and he keeps **turning up wherever they turn up.** His interests overlap theirs *precisely* because Wesley is the hidden hinge between the mill, the Mansion, and the disappearances — so the players draw the damning line themselves, without him feeding it. It's the first time a **second party is shown independently circling Wesley** (a later payoff). **Play him shifty and petty, not sinister-competent,** or the table promotes him from herring to mastermind.

## Where found
- **Core — [[The Library]] microfilm** (above): where the PCs first cross him in [[Episode 2 — The Hush]], self-concealing over the mill/Mercer records.
- **Unlock follow-up — his church:** comes online if they chase the "hooded figure"; an **evening service** (robed, brimstone) is peak red-herring. He may also accost "those mill kids" on the street. Never mandatory.

> [!note] DM-only
> Hollis is **not Filter-touched** in any special way — his alarm about Wesley is ordinary jealousy that happens to land on the truth, not perception of the anomalous. Keep him a mundane human. If the players later realize Wesley is the villain, his "I told you so" should sting *because* they had the warning and threw it away.
