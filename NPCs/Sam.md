# Sam

Up: [[Cast & NPCs]]

**A PC's ordinary older relative who is, unknown to anyone, an attenuated [[Elias Mercer|Mercer]] descendant — the bloodline anchor [[The Equinox Ritual]] cannot complete without, and the campaign's surprise recruit.**

> [!warning] Provisional / locked after PC creation
> Sam's exact relation to the PC, name, residence, ordinary-summer circumstances, and full negotiation profile are **locked after PC creation** (bible §20 item 1). Default: [[Eddie Park]]'s older cousin, 25–35, with an ordinary job (librarian's assistant, town clerk, similar). Sam must be designed to be **plausibly radicalizable** — not a pure sympathetic bystander — because Sam takes over [[Wesley Crane]]'s ritual role if Wesley is killed pre-equinox.

## Profile
A distant-but-real, unaware Mercer descendant, 25–35, bonded to a PC **by family** (the default and the point). Starting disposition **3 (Sympathetic)** — Sam is family. Emerges in the PCs' awareness around [[Episode 5 — Storm-That-Doesn't-Move]] (Mike Tinsmith's hollowing window) as an adult who knew [[Mike and Doug Tinsmith|Mike]] and is quietly looking into his death.

## Arc / function
**Sam is a surprise recruit.** Their connection to [[Wesley Crane]] is not obvious to the PCs and not even to the other recruits until very late — Wesley meets Sam privately, framed as "just a thoughtful older friend." The slow recognition by the PC relative is one of the campaign's signature beats. The bond hook is **family — bonded by default, defended only if someone thinks to defend it.** The asymmetry is the point.

If the ritual succeeds, **Sam is *corrupted*, not sacrificed** — and continues their normal life as a corrupted version of themselves (see [[Aftermath Branches]]).

> [!danger] DM-only
> **Negotiation profile (provisional — finalized at PC creation).** Motivations: Higher Authority (Wesley appears as a thoughtful older friend — that's the manipulation), Discovery (genuinely investigating Mike's death), Benevolence (genuinely cares about the PC family member). Pitfalls: Greed; possibly Power. Sam should *not* be radicalizable by simple appeals; reachability lives in the family relationship.
>
> **Special mechanical status (bible §5, §9):** if **Sam reaches Interest 4+ at any point** in the equinox negotiation, **the ritual cannot complete regardless of the other Rivals' states** — the gate stays sealed (the "Ritual fully prevented" branch). Sam is the bloodline anchor; without her the ritual cannot complete. **Wesley's counter-cultivation** begins only after Sam emerges in PC awareness (Ep5–6): framing the relationship as Sam's adult business — "*she's allowed to have friends outside the family.*" If Wesley is killed pre-equinox, Sam — radicalized by his death and holding bloodline access — attempts to complete the ritual (see [[Wesley Crane]], Killing Wesley Early). See [[The Negotiation System]], [[The Equinox Ritual]].
