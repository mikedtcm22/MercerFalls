# Wendell Pace

Up: [[Cast & NPCs]]

**The janitor [[Wesley Crane|Wesley]] dragged into the dungeon as a hollowing victim — the man the PCs met alive under the juvenile [[The Hush|Hush]] in the [[The Pillared Reach|Pillared Reach]] and watched hollow on the [[The Buzzsaw Room|Buzzsaw]] catwalk. The campaign's first proof, seen on-screen, that "death in the dungeon isn't exactly death."**

## Profile
Middle-aged **school janitor** who also **busses tables at [[Marco's Diner]]** — the identity the party already knew him by (Bash recognized him on sight). Unremarkable; the kind of man a town doesn't miss quickly. Wesley brought him down as labor (and a hollowing seed). The blood trail across the [[The Spiked Corridor|Spiked Corridor]] and into the [[The Buzzsaw Room|Buzzsaw Room]] was his.

> [!note] As played (Episode 1 — Sessions 1–2)
> The party came on him in the [[The Pillared Reach|Pillared Reach]] as the juvenile Hush killed him, **intervened, and saved him** (the narrow-save branch). He tagged along, barely verbal — then, crossing the [[The Buzzsaw Room|Buzzsaw]] catwalk during the boss wave, he was struck and **hollowed on-screen**: dissolved to motes, leaving a nullprint. [[Gern]] recognized the shape from his "Certainology Workbooks." He reconstitutes at the entrance and is **alive topside** — hollowed. See [[Episode 1 — The Dare — Play Log]].

## Function: "death in the dungeon isn't death"
Per the dungeon's death mechanic ([[Death, TPK, and the Lost Day]]), a body that dies below **reconstitutes at the entrance and walks home on autopilot.** So Wendell is **alive** afterward — and, because he didn't leave free-willed, **hollowed:** going through the motions of his routine, flat and *off.* An **Insight** check catches the wrongness (lights on, nobody home); no dramatic reveal, just a quiet, unsettling *"...wasn't that guy—?"*

He's a **seed**, not a plot node: he plants *dying below isn't final* before the players need to understand it. Incidental, never required.

## Where found (topside, Episode 2+)
Now that play has fixed him as the **school janitor / [[Marco's Diner]] busser**, the natural topside re-encounter is at the diner (or around the school, though it's summer) — wherever the kids would clock the flat, *off* version of a man they know. He can also turn up **janitoring [[The Mercer Mansion]]** on a town-history trip (planting both a cordial [[Wesley Crane|Wesley]] and his hollowed victim in one room, connected by neither — see [[Episode 2 — The Hush — Town-Time Investigation Map]]), or shuffling the Flats near [[Jerry's Pawn Shop]].

> [!danger] DM-only
> Wendell is **Wesley's** victim (Wesley brought him down) — a link the players cannot make yet and shouldn't. Hold it for the [[Wesley Crane]] recognition arc. His hollowing also quietly feeds [[Jerry]]'s "who's been acting strange" whisper-count and [[Jerry's Pawn Shop|the pawn shop]]'s hollowed-count ledger.
