# Jerry

Up: [[Cast & NPCs]]

**Pawn-shop owner in The Flats — the town's accidental witness and its human-intel hub. What [[Russ]] is to monster-lore, Jerry is to town-history and goings-on: he sees people when they're desperate, and he hears what the town won't say out loud.** Named by [[Old Man]] in Session 1.

## Profile
Owner of [[Jerry's Pawn Shop]] in The Flats. Runs one of the town's cash-conversion channels for dungeon loot — **fast cash, low rates, no questions** (the money loop, [[Gear and Items]] / [[Time Points]]). The trip to sell still costs Time Points. Comes online **early** — in [[Episode 2 — The Hush]] — because the players took to the idea of a pawn shop in Session 1 and will want somewhere to turn dungeon haul into supply money.

## Function: the town's whisper-hub (and accidental witness)
Two linked roles:
- **Human intel.** Jerry sees the sides of people they don't show anyone — how they act when they're broke, scared, or unloading something they shouldn't. He knows the whispers, the debts, the quiet town secrets. Where Russ answers *what is that monster*, Jerry answers *what's going on with these people* — the Track-A counterpart in [[Episode 2 — The Hush]] (the Vances; who's been acting strange; who's really gone where). A [[Time Points]] investment surfaces a gossip beat (1 pt) or a real, load-bearing whisper (2 pt).
- **The accidental witness.** Hollowed locals all pawn things eventually — they don't notice they're running through their savings — and Jerry has clocked *the pattern.* He doesn't use the word "hollowed," but he keeps a **private ledger** and can hint he knows accurate counts. He has the witness role but **not the Filter-permission to act** on it (see [[The Reality Filter]], [[Hollowing and Agency-Loss]]); he just catalogues. Sustained investment surfaces the ledger as a quiet, powerful confirmation of [[The Pattern Recognition Arc]]. ([[Henry Voss]], who's pawned things steadily for years, deepens this thread in [[Episode — The Hungry House]].)
