# Tank

Barbarian · the popular multi-sport jock (football, wrestling, baseball — anything highly physical; good at throwing people around or hitting things with a bat). Lives in [[The Districts|the Flats]]. Outwardly nonchalant and wild; secretly **incredibly disciplined**. *(Player still building him out — Session 0 read.)*

## The read — Order wearing a Chaos mask
"Big dumb jock" on the outside, immense *self-imposed* [[Order and Chaos|Order]] underneath — a kid who polices himself harder than anyone could. The 5 a.m. workouts, last to leave practice, the discipline no one sees: the wild-jock act is the mask over the machine. The gap between the two is his whole character.
- **The load-bearing detail — his father.** A [[The 1991 Mill Incident|mill worker]] who never recovered from the closing and has relocated all his unspent hope onto Tank's body — Tank as "the only way out." Order in its most intimate, sympathetic form: a cage built of love and desperation, hardest to resist because resisting feels like betrayal. Tank senses the weight and has *chosen* to carry it, because the alternative is watching his dad have nothing.
- **Tank and [[Old Man]] are the same wound from opposite social positions** — both load-bearing kids carrying a parent's collapse (Old Man's invisible/indoor; Tank's public/physical). Run the *"your suffering is load-bearing"* lie against both and watch them respond differently; later, let them recognize each other.
- **Vulnerability:** Chaos's pitch is the locker-room dream of *pure release* — stop carrying it, indulge the wildness he only *performs*. The deeper, sadder trap is **Order's**: anything that promises to make the dream come true and *make Dad okay* — he'd walk into real wrongness for it. His redemption question: can "the only way out" become a thing he chooses *for himself* rather than carries *for his dad*? (Agency vs. inheritance, in cleats.)
- **Craft note:** protect the reveal — let the table (and the players) be fooled by the dumb-jock act for a while, until a quiet scene shows the 5 a.m. kid. Don't make him transparently Secretly Deep from session one.

## At the mill
A **prank**, not a dare — agreed to in a bid to impress his jock crew (the prank was *proposed by* [[Ricky Vance]]); the goal was to scare [[Bash Whitlock]] and [[Gern]] into leaving within an hour. He's the one with *power* in the dynamic and is misusing it mildly — not out of cruelty (he isn't cruel) but out of the same need everyone has: to belong. The gap between his public performance and private decency shows immediately, and sets up his guilt as a redemptive lever once the prank turns into mortal danger.
