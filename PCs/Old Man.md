# Old Man

Human Bard · 16 · the socially-adept party face who can talk anyone — including himself — into nearly anything. Ideal: *say the true thing, gently — precision is a kind of mercy.* Flaw: *carries everyone, lets no one carry him; believes his own eloquent line.* The first PC whose arc was fully built.

## The read — he *is* the Order/Chaos swing
His life is an Order→Chaos swing, mid-motion. The "good son" era was pure [[Order and Chaos|Order]]: the family's load-bearing wall, self erased into function. A year ago it "broke bad" — he lurched toward Chaos (keeps his own hours, refuses any rule he didn't choose). But his rebellion is pure negation (anti-the-stillness, never *pro* anything) — Chaos exactly: a pitch always *against* Order, never *for* something. He fled one cage and mistook the other for open sky.
- **His gift is Chaos's vector.** He believes the well-turned line ("if it comes out eloquent, he takes it for true") — the most seducible character for a Chaos that speaks in gorgeous half-truths, and the most dangerous conduit to the party (he's the face).
- **His Ideal is the escape hatch.** Truth held with tenderness, in service of a value = the agentic third position neither jaw can offer.

## Awakening test — "The Pit and the Spring" (locked)
He's at the bottom of a pit; a rope-ladder hangs out of reach; he and a **stone statue of a thing he loves** sit on a platform on a compression spring. The exit + ember are **below**, in either case. Step off himself (self-sacrifice) → the statue rises to a dead ceiling (stone can't climb — futile) and the exit is at his feet. Unload the statue (save himself) → he rises to a dead-end ladder, then climbs back down to the exit. **Up is always the false promise; the way out was under the weight.** Per-station inscription:
> *You were made the floor, so the rest could stand. / But the world is not a scale, and weight was never love. / Lie down beneath it and it rises only as far as stone. / Or let go — and find what was under you the whole time.*
The tell, cruel for him: the room asks the one question that's defined his life (carry them or save myself) and is equally pleased either way — it rewards even the *futile* sacrifice. The real answer (keep what he loves *with* him) was never on the menu. See [[The Ember Chamber]], [[The Embers]].

## Battle plan
- **Through-line — The Eloquent Trap.** [[The Entity]] never speaks to him; the Chaos framing surfaces inside his own notebooks/lyrics (he "generates the material first, digs meaning out after"). His songs get better and more *wrong* together; the crisis is the session his own best sentence nearly talks the table onto the Entity's side, and he must distrust his most beautiful line in real time.
- **Early/mid lean — The Load-Bearing Wall.** Cast him as the one holding everyone up; let him succeed; then escalate the weight past one kid's capacity. The lie to run: *your suffering is load-bearing* (the statue already refuted it). **Order's seduction** lives here — the ritual offers to *stop the bleeding* by freezing the people he loves into safe, hollowed peace; aimed at his mother ("narrowing year by year under a long run of losses"). He'll hate the means, crave the result.
- **Pressure-point & turn — The Creek Gets a Ledger.** The wrongness reaches the one relationship that never cost him (the creek, the salamander census). Saving it *right* — valuing and acting at cost, not self-erasure — is his agentic turn.
- **Reserve:** the friend who died on his 16th birthday (later: hollowing-adjacent?); the mother (Order's hardest lever — use late).

## Relationships
Fellow [[The Districts|Flats]] kid with [[Tank]] (same street); his recent slacker turn put him near the jock hijinx. **Old Man and Tank are the same wound from opposite social positions** — both load-bearing kids carrying a parent's collapse. At the mill by **coincidence** (brooding in the nearby woods), he volunteered for the "adventuring party" — partly people-watching, partly because he clocked the prank and quietly moved to soften it. He knows it was a setup: a held truth he may have to weigh later. See [[The Party]].
