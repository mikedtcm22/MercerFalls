# The Pattern Recognition Arc

Up: [[Plot & Structure]]

**A DM tracking tool, not a player-facing structure: the soft progression by which the PCs go from "weird town" to "the dungeon is the source" to "it's Wesley."**

> [!danger] DM-only
> Never telegraph the phases. Plant clues densely; have NPC nudges ready. The PCs should feel they're solving real mysteries, not that the DM is being coy or withholding.

## The Two Clicks

The Wesley reveal isn't a single beat — it's two cumulative realizations across 4–6 episodes (see [[The Bizarro Plot]] and [[Episodes]]).

**Phase 1 (Ep1–2) — "weird problems."** Plant subtle visual/material consistency at problem sites. Don't flag it. The PCs probably won't notice; that's fine.

**Phase 2 (Ep3 → Click 1) — "the dungeon is the source."** Make the consistency *unmissable* at the third site ([[Episode 3 — The Click]]). Even an inattentive PC should be able to roll Investigation and connect it. They realize the problems share a source — the dungeon is leaking objects, materials, *itself* — but not yet who, if anyone, is causing it. **Recommended click clue:** dungeon-stone material recurring across [[Episode 1 — The Hush]] (the Hush's not-bone teeth), [[Episode 2 — The Hungry House]] (the basement chamber-stone), and Episode 3's source. (Provisional — could swap to a recurring glyph or creature-anatomy detail; see [[Cosmology Seeds]].)

**Phase 3 (Ep6–8 → Click 2) — "it's that guy at the community center."** [[Wesley Crane]]'s name accumulates: by [[Episode 6 — First Real Wesley Suspicion]] three episodes have surfaced it (the community center deaths — see [[Wesley's Summer Calendar]]); by [[Episode 8 — Wesley Confirmed]] a faceup conversation makes it clear. From here the PCs run their own investigation alongside the monster work, with the equinox approaching as a deadline they don't yet recognize.

## DM Nudges (if the PCs are missing it)

- **For Click 1:** have an NPC say it. [[Marcy Hauer]]: "isn't that weird, that's the same thing my brother had on his work badge in '91." A librarian, small-talking: "Oh, that material? Mercer Steel used to make those — but not for years."
- **For Click 2:** have a recruit slip. [[Eddie Park]], with Wesley nearby: "You know about that Pine Street house, right, Wesley? You said something about it last month." Wesley deflects warmly; the PCs notice.

## Accelerant: The Keystone Theft

The [[The Keystone Theft]] interlude (early–mid August) can pull Click 2 *earlier* than the Ep8 default. Its Wesley-fingerprint level is maximum — the moment the campaign goes from "Wesley is involved somewhere" to "Wesley is actively messing with us by name." If the PCs commit to a recovery op, they put his name to *the antagonist* much sooner. The campaign supports either pacing.
