# The Keystone Theft

Up: [[Plot & Structure]]

**[[Wesley Crane]] reaches the Floor 3 *map* keystone before the PCs (early–mid August), steals it, and chalks a taunt — the beat that confirms he's watching them and accelerates Click 2.**

> [!danger] DM-only
> This is a direct antagonist beat, not a hollowing-cornerstone. It slots as a short side-arc between [[Episode 5 — Storm-That-Doesn't-Move]] and [[Episode 6 — First Real Wesley Suspicion]]. See [[Wesley's Summer Calendar]].

## How It Happens

Once the PCs have used [[The Keystone Tablet]] for a few delves, Wesley notices — observing them from a sigil-bound vantage during one of his own delves (see [[Wesley's Sigils]]). His relic doesn't let *him* use a keystone (they're inert in his hand), but it lets him understand what the PCs have. Around the Floor 3 window he arrives at the Floor 3 map keystone's location first, takes it, leaves a dusty outline in the recess, and chalks a taunt nearby in his sigil-cartography style — *"finders keepers — best of luck out there, kids"* or *"if you wanted it you should have come sooner."*

(Note: the keystone is distinct from **the Weight**, the Floor 3 ritual item — see [[The Three Ritual Items]].)

## What the Beat Does at Once

- **Confirms Wesley has been watching them**, not just operating in parallel.
- **Locks his identity to the chalk-cartography style** the PCs have seen since Session 1 — now it has a face.
- **Marks his tonal shift** from cryptic-grim ("you poor things") to playful-cruel.
- **Creates an above-ground choice:** burn 1–2 days on a keystone-recovery heist, or push Floor 3 compass-only as a permanent state.
- **Accelerates Click 2** (see [[The Pattern Recognition Arc]]). Fingerprint level: **maximum** — the campaign goes from "Wesley is involved somewhere" to "Wesley is actively messing with us by name."

## The Recovery Side-Arc

Optional, 1–2 days of investment — a heist subplot, not its own MOTW. The PCs can:

- Investigate Wesley's likely hiding spot (the [[The Community Center]] back room is the leading candidate; the [[The Mercer Mansion]] is later, once Wesley has access there);
- Attempt a stealth or social incursion to recover it;
- Decide it's not worth the cost and push on compass-only.

**Disposition layer:** PCs with strong bonds to [[Marcy Hauer]] (front desk) or [[Ricky Vance]] (now full-time at the center) have inside access. But if a PC uses a recruit-bond to scout the back room, Wesley may notice, slot it into his "she's being used by them" counter-cultivation framework, and erode the bond (see [[Wesley's Recruits]]).

## Where the Keystone Lives

Above-ground, **never re-introduced to the dungeon**. Community center back room initially; possibly transferred to the Mercer mansion mid-September. **Keystones cannot be destroyed** — the heist's only real failure state is the PCs deciding not to attempt it.
