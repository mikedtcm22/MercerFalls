# Where the PCs Can Disrupt

Up: [[Plot & Structure]]

**The menu of soft spots in [[Wesley Crane]]'s plot. The DM's job is to make them *findable* without flag-waving; the longer summer gives the PCs many opportunities.**

> [!danger] DM-only
> Each lever below either denies Wesley a ritual element, shifts a Rival's equinox disposition, or surfaces information. See [[Aftermath Branches]] for how these cash out.

## Information-Side (campaign-spanning)

- Identify Wesley as the buzzsaw-room cultist (across episodes; faceup conversation by [[Episode 8 — Wesley Confirmed]]).
- Identify the recruits as a coherent group (gradually visible at [[The Community Center]] — see [[Wesley's Recruits]]).
- **Notice [[Dominique Ferraro]] is drifting into Wesley's orbit.** *Deliberately* the hardest lever to see: she reads as the party's trusted helper, so it isn't "identify a target" but realizing the woman-in-the-van is being turned. Episode seeding (and any ember-resonance "tell" near her, since she carries Mercer blood) is **TBD** — she's present from Act 1's start, so the old Sam-style "emerges mid-summer" beat doesn't apply.
- Decode Wesley's chalked glyphs as cartography (see [[Wesley's Sigils]]).
- Read sigil-spawned encounters as evidence of Wesley's location and timing.
- Distinguish real sigils from decoys (achievable by ~Ep5 if invested).
- Read Earl's pawn-shop ledger to estimate the hollowed-townsperson count (see [[Earl's Pawn Shop]]).

## Disposition-Building (the big lever)

The campaign's primary mechanism for converting summer effort into equinox-night advantage — each recruit's starting Interest/Patience in the [[The Negotiation System]] derives from summer disposition.

- **Build a real bond with [[Ricky Vance]], [[Zander Pruitt]], [[Ren Sołtys]], or [[Eddie Park]]** via their featured episode plus sustained [[Time Points]]. Sympathetic (3) is one well-handled episode; Connected (4) needs reinforcement; Bonded (5) needs a key emotional moment.
- **Reach [[Dominique Ferraro]]** — the single highest-leverage lever (her refusal seals the gate), but *not* won by ordinary bond-building: she already trusts the party. The lever is a PC **seeing her drift and confronting her as a person**, refusing the misdirection — she kept full agency, so she can still choose out. How and when this becomes available is **TBD** (deep-dive). See [[Dominique Ferraro]].
- **Research a Rival's motivations/pitfalls** via Time Points (2/3/4+ TP tiers for partial/half/full reveal) — known info becomes equinox toolkit.
- **Fully extract one recruit** before the equinox (hard — an extracted recruit doesn't even attend).
- **Counter Wesley's counter-cultivation** by addressing the specific story he's planting ("Wesley told Eddie you're just using him").

## Relationship-Side (other NPCs)

- Build rapport with [[Russ]] at [[Panels]] — back-room collection access, eventually his Filter-break.
- Build rapport with Earl — unlocks the hollowed-count ledger (see [[Earl's Pawn Shop]]).
- Help [[Mrs. Devereaux]] (board chair, mansion key-holder) with Society work — off-hours library annex access.

## Dungeon-Side

- Reach the Weight chamber on Floor 3 *before* Wesley (plausible for an aggressive Level-3 party — see [[The Three Ritual Items]]).
- Disrupt one of Wesley's mid-delve operations directly.
- Clear Wesley's real sigils before he returns.
- Recover the stolen Floor 3 map keystone (heist subplot — see [[The Keystone Theft]]).
- Witness adherent walks-home from mill stakeouts (town-side channel — see [[Wesley's Adherents]]).

## Equinox-Night

- Direct interruption at [[The Mercer Mansion]] — Wesley has no death protection above ground.
- **Pull [[Dominique Ferraro]] back** — the one move that seals the gate (she's the bloodline anchor and the continuation; see her note).
- Compromise Wesley's community-center sigil-site during the retreat.
- **Most importantly:** show up with high-disposition Rivals, accumulated motivations/pitfalls intel, and items destroyed over the summer — all of which translate to mechanical advantage in [[The Equinox Ritual]] negotiation-as-combat.
