# The Countdown Structure

Up: [[Plot & Structure]]

**Three nested clocks span the whole campaign: the equinox (Levels 1–5), the Act 2 race plus Wesley's break (Levels 6–15), and the Y2K containment-expiration (Levels 16–20).**

> [!danger] DM-only
> The clocks are the campaign's gravity. Per [[The Bizarro Plot]], the PCs aren't defusing a doomsday device that explodes on failure — they're making the difference between a town with a chance and one without. Failure branches; only the level-20 objective is terminal.

## The Three Clocks (bible §15)

| Clock | Window | Spans |
|---|---|---|
| **Small** | Wesley's autumn-equinox ritual — [[The Mercer Gate]] opens | Levels 1–5 (mid-June → Sept 22, 1996) |
| **Medium** | Act 2 race through the Mercer sector and Tier-2; Wesley's deepening; the Mercer-papers reveal; the Wesley break | Levels 6–15 (1996–1999) |
| **Large** | Y2K — Dec 31, 1999. The containment field expires. | Levels 16–20 |

## Small Clock — The Equinox (Act 1)

The whole of Act 1. Covered in detail across [[Wesley's Summer Calendar]], [[The Equinox Ritual]], and [[Aftermath Branches]]. The summer-vacation framing eases players into [[Time Points]]; the **school-year compression to 2 Day / 2 Night** (late August) accelerates the equinox approach. Milestone leveling: Level 1 at start → 5 by equinox.

## Medium Clock — The Act 2 Race

*(No standalone note exists for the Act 2 Race; its content is folded here and into [[The Mercer Gate]].)*

Once the Gate is open (regardless of Wesley's fate — see [[Aftermath Branches]]), Act 2 becomes a **race**. [[Wesley Crane]] (or his successor) and the PCs both descend through the Mercer sector into the unified Tier-2 floors, both hunting *the thing the Gate was guarding* — each assuming it's a powerful object that could make their faction dominant. The race occupies the bulk of Act 2's sessions: both sides make visible progress (readable via forensic-delving signals), both can block or sabotage the other, and the climax triggers when one side reaches the thing and produces a phenomenon felt across the dungeon and the town.

**The thing behind the Gate** is held loose — somewhere in the Tier-2 system, deep from the Mercer sector, the campaign's canonical "Lore that breaks its possessor." What it does to whoever finds it first is the late-Act-2 / early-Act-3 turning point — plausibly the catalyst for **Wesley's Break**, where Order's user becomes Chaos's champion (see [[Wesley Crane]]). The Mercer-papers reveal — [[Elias Mercer]] documenting his own consumption — is the other engine of this clock.

## Large Clock — Y2K

December 31, 1999 is when the containment field, by [[Order and Chaos|Order]]'s design, naturally weakens to nothing — Order was confident the agency-harvest would by then have solved the problem permanently or produced a livable outcome. **The day the bars come down.** Real-world millennium anxiety becomes a recruitment tool for whichever cosmic side wants it: a town that already can't act is the perfect vector for "the world is ending." At Y2K, one of three: Order has won (permanent suppression of Chaos), or Chaos breaks loose, or the PCs have found and held a third way — the campaign's level-20 victory condition. See [[High Concept]] and [[The Entity]].
