# Aftermath Branches

Up: [[Plot & Structure]]

**What kind of rival party walks out of the opened [[The Mercer Gate]] — the Act 1 success/failure fork, resolved by the [[The Equinox Ritual]] negotiation tally.**

> [!danger] DM-only
> The NPC party exists no matter what; the gate opens no matter what (unless the bloodline anchor is pulled back). What the PCs control is *what kind* of party emerges. Failure doesn't end the campaign — it makes the rest of it meaner. See [[The Bizarro Plot]].

## The Tally Table (bible §9)

The total recruits reaching **Interest 4+** in the negotiation determines the shape:

| Recruits at Interest 4+ | Aftermath |
|---|---|
| 3+ recruits | **Rival Party path** (success) |
| 2 recruits | **Compromised toward Rival** |
| 1 recruit | **Compromised toward Evil** |
| 0 recruits | **Evil Party path** (failure) |
| **[[Dominique Ferraro]] reached (Interest 4+)** | **Ritual fully prevented** — gate stays sealed, regardless of all other states |

## The Branches

### Evil Party (failure — clean ritual completion)
All three elements present and intact; gate opens fully; the anti-embers are pure Order. The four recruits get pure-Order anti-embers as Wesley intended — full radicalization-hollowing, no remaining self, functionally Wesley's peers (his successful "human trial"). Fire-vocabulary inflection: *too-bright, fierce, uncontrolled*. Not reachable as allies. They face their own break when Wesley does (late Act 2). See [[The Other Party (Rival or Evil)]].

### Rival Party (success — disrupted completion)
PCs interfere without fully preventing. Wesley (or [[Dominique Ferraro]]) still walks through, but interference forces a **Chaos infusion** into the anti-embers: an **80/20 Order/Chaos** party — the **mirror** of the PCs' 80/20 Chaos/Order (same magnitude, *opposite* dominant pole — they remain Wesley's Order-side kids, but carry enough Chaos/self to stay reachable). This is the cosmological form of the whole foil design: a party that *reflects* the PCs rather than copying them. Inflection: *uneven, guttering, struggling to catch cleanly*. Not aligned with the PCs, pursuing their own agenda, **but reachable** as future allies. See [[The Other Party (Rival or Evil)]].

### Compromised Party (between Rival and Evil)
Intermediate tally (1–2 recruits swayed). Unswayed recruits get full pure-Order anti-embers; swayed ones (or those landing Interest 2–3) get degraded anti-embers or refuse. A *partially recoverable* party — some members reachable, others near Evil-Party dogma.

### Ritual Fully Prevented ([[Dominique Ferraro]] reached)
Dominique is the bloodline anchor; without her the ritual cannot complete regardless of other states. **Gate stays sealed.** Unlike the recruits she isn't won by Time-Points disposition — she kept full agency the whole way (she was offered a wheel of her own, not cured of a broken leg), so she's reachable *only* if a PC refuses the woman-in-the-van misdirection and sees her as a person who chose in and could choose out. The rarest and most earned outcome, and the most disruptive to the planned Act 2 architecture — the [[The Mill Sector|Mill sector]] becomes the standard route deeper, with the Mercer entrance a campaign-long unsolved problem. The opposing entity may still grant embers directly to the four kids through class-flavored experiences, producing a Rival Party regardless.

## Pre-Equinox Item Disruption (still relevant on the night)

Separately from the per-Rival tally, items the PCs denied Wesley over the summer reduce the equinox [[The Equinox Ritual|Ritual Progress Track]]'s starting count (see [[The Three Ritual Items]]):

- 3 items intact → track starts 3/5 (Wesley fills 2)
- 2 items → starts 2/5
- 1 item → starts 1/5
- 0 items → starts 0/5 (essentially impossible for Wesley to finish in time)

The track gate is *separate* from the negotiation outcome — but the longer Wesley takes to fill it, the more rounds the PCs get to build/recover disposition in the moment.

## What About Wesley?

In all branches Wesley **survives** unless the PCs kill him directly (he has no death protection above ground — see [[Wesley Crane]] "Killing Wesley Early"). The equinox decides whether his recruits get anti-embers, not whether he himself lives. If he is killed, [[Dominique Ferraro]] carries the ritual forward unless she too is pulled back.
