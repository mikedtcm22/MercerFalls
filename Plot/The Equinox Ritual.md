# The Equinox Ritual

Up: [[Plot & Structure]]

**[[Wesley Crane]]'s Act 1 endgame: the autumn-equinox ritual (Sunday, September 22, 1996) that opens [[The Mercer Gate]] — staged as the campaign's climactic negotiation-as-combat in the [[The Mercer Mansion]] basement.**

> [!danger] DM-only
> Everything here is the antagonist's plan and the climax structure. The PCs spend the equinox arc figuring it out from scratch.

## Purpose: Open the Mercer Gate

The Gate is a sealed door in the Mercer mansion basement concealing the dungeon's third surface entrance, locked since 1887 by [[Elias Mercer]]. Wesley has studied it two years and cannot open it without the ritual. The gate is **genuinely required** — it's his access to deeper Mercer research materials toward [[Wesley Crane|the Work]].

**The locking spell needs three elements to undo:**

1. **Materials from the dungeon** — the [[The Three Ritual Items]] (Tally Stone, Gathered Dust, Weight); the Weight is the "lock" component.
2. **A Mercer bloodline anchor** — [[Dominique Ferraro]], an attenuated Mercer descendant via the Ferraro line, is the intended bloodline-witness. Unlike the recruits she is **exempt from the hollowing qualifier** — the ritual needs her *blood*, not a hollowing.
3. **Four witnesses** — the recruits [[Ricky Vance]], [[Zander Pruitt]], [[Ren Sołtys]], and [[Eddie Park]]. The qualifier is **"the Gathering Hollows":** the gate's accounting requires participants who have been **hollowed-and-survived** (touched by the dungeon and come back) — not fully volitional ones. Each acquires that qualifying event somewhere in Act 1 (situational — debut episode or a later Wesley expedition). See [[Wesley's Recruits]], [[The Other Party (Rival or Evil)]].

## Anti-Embers: Filed as a Side-Effect, Secretly the Point

At completion the ritual also produces **anti-embers** for the witnesses — the cosmic energy released by the unlock crystallizes around them as dungeon-side protection mirroring Wesley's relic. Structurally he files this as a welcome side-effect (it arms his rival party). **In truth it's the side he cares about most:** he has *modified* the rite to attempt **steered anti-embers** in the four recruits — using a ritual he must run anyway as cover for his first **human trial of the Work at group scale.** See [[Wesley Crane]] → *The Work*.

## The Gate Opens Whether Wesley Lives or Dies

Structurally critical: **the gate opens regardless of Wesley's fate.** Kill Wesley pre-equinox and [[Dominique Ferraro]] (his heir, holding bloodline access) attempts to complete what's left; kill him in the equinox window and she takes over and walks through — *unless she herself is pulled back* (see below). The PCs' climactic question is not "did Wesley succeed?" but "what kind of rival emerges?" See [[Aftermath Branches]] and [[The Other Party (Rival or Evil)]].

## The Negotiation-as-Combat (bible §9)

The confrontation runs on initiative, rounds, and turns, but the PCs are mostly *arguing*, not fighting. See [[The Negotiation System]] for full mechanics.

**Setup.** PCs reach the basement mid-ritual. Wesley officiates; the four recruits stand in their ritual positions and [[Dominique Ferraro]] holds the bloodline anchor; the three items are placed (minus any the PCs disrupted); adherents may defend.

**PC action options per round:** (1) **Argue to a Rival** — power roll vs. their motivations/pitfalls; (2) **Disrupt the ritual** — Athletics/Arcana, each success drops the Progress Track 1 segment; (3) **Fight an adherent**; (4) **Bolster** — give another PC advantage (Help).

**Wesley's action options per round:** (1) **Counter-argue a Rival** — push Interest down or pitfall them; (2) **Progress the ritual** — +1 segment; (3) **Attack a PC** (sigil powers) — used sparingly; he prefers talking.

**The Ritual Progress Track.** A 5-segment track that **starts filled with the number of intact ritual items left in place** (up to 5 if nothing disrupted; fewer per item the PCs denied — see [[The Three Ritual Items]]). Wesley fills remaining segments; PCs Disrupt to reduce them. When full, the ritual completes and outcomes tally per Rival.

**Wesley is not subject to negotiation** — treated as Hostile (Interest 1, Patience 1), emptied of reachable motivations. PC attempts to negotiate with him fail by design. A once-swayed Rival who hits Interest 5 *before* the track fills can become a DM-controlled ally arguing other Rivals over.

## Resolution Per Rival

| Final Interest | Outcome |
|---|---|
| 5 | Breaks publicly with Wesley; may help, walk out, or argue alongside the PCs |
| 4 | Hesitates, refuses to participate fully; no anti-ember forms |
| 2–3 | Conflicted; gets a degraded (Compromised) anti-ember |
| 0–1 | Fully accepts Wesley's framing; full anti-ember; Evil Party member |
| Patience 0 + low Interest | Shuts the PCs out; treated as the Interest 1 result |

The count of recruits at Interest 4+ sets the aftermath fork. **[[Dominique Ferraro]] is the exception to the table:** she isn't won by Time-Points disposition like the recruits (she kept full agency — she was offered a wheel of her own, not cured of a broken leg), and is reachable *only* if a PC refuses the woman-in-the-van misdirection and sees her as a person. If she is reached, **the bloodline anchor refuses and the ritual cannot complete** — the highest-leverage outcome. See [[Aftermath Branches]], [[The Other Party (Rival or Evil)]].
