# Wesley's Summer Calendar

Up: [[Plot & Structure]]

**Week-by-week, what [[Wesley Crane]] does over the summer regardless of the PCs — paced to be unrushed and deniable, from mid-June through equinox night, Sunday September 22, 1996.**

> [!danger] DM-only
> This is the antagonist's bizarro-plot spine (see [[The Bizarro Plot]]). Wesley adjusts: if PCs disrupt one move, he reschedules or substitutes. He is reactive, not on rails — don't pre-plan more than 1–2 sessions past this.

## The Cornerstones

Four moves are **fixed commitments** Wesley will make absent disruption. Each of the three hollowing-cornerstones generates the downstream monster-of-the-week the PCs encounter as their next episode (see [[Episodes]]), each is a **witnessable** reconstitution-and-walk-home at the mill, and each leaves a chamber with fresh fading residue for a day or two.

- **Dust-harvest (late July)** → Mike's hollowing → [[Episode 5 — Storm-That-Doesn't-Move]]. Retrieves the [[The Three Ritual Items|Gathered Dust]].
- **First Weight attempt (mid-Aug)** → Doug's hollowing → [[Episode 6 — First Real Wesley Suspicion]]. **Fails** — no retrieval.
- **Second Weight attempt (early Sept)** → Phyllis's hollowing → [[Episode 8 — Wesley Confirmed]]. **Succeeds** — Weight retrieved.
- **Keystone theft (early–mid Aug)** — a different kind of cornerstone: not a hollowing but a direct antagonist beat, slotted as its own mini-arc between [[Episode 5 — Storm-That-Doesn't-Move]] and [[Episode 6 — First Real Wesley Suspicion]]. See [[The Keystone Theft]].

## Otherwise, Low-Key

Beyond the cornerstones, Wesley's summer is quiet: he runs the community center, has dinners, gives a Sunday sermon, stays exactly as charming as last year. The PCs may not see him for whole stretches. That deniability is the point.
