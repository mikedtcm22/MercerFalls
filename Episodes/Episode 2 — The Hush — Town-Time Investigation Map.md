# Episode 2 — The Hush — Town-Time Investigation Map

Up: [[Episode 2 — The Hush]]

**The [[Time Points|Town-Time]] investigation for [[Episode 2 — The Hush]]: two parallel tracks that converge, bracketed by a staged opening and a night climax. Locks the information inventory, the trimmed core/unlock node set (with day/night availability), the placements, and the casting. Synced to current design; the full beat-by-beat lives in the DM-screen content doc.**

## Casting locks
**[[Tank]]** = the marked / hunted one · **[[Gern]]** = the named scapegoat · **[[Old Man]]** = the party's first monster-tripwire · **[[Bash Whitlock|Bash]]** = [[Dominique Ferraro|Dominique]]'s cousin (the reassembly's blood tie).

## The opening: two dungeon outcomes, staged out of order
Episode 1 ended two ways and the opening refuses to flatten it. **Tank & Old Man fell** (hollowed → they lose Day A to autopilot); **Bash & Gern escaped** via Wesley's portal (free Day A). Played out of order so the lost day is *witnessed,* not described: open on **Day B** with Tank's ([[Dominique Ferraro|Dominique]]) and Old Man's (a scared Flats kid — the monster-in-the-streets + "you were being weird") confrontations → flash back to **Day A** (Bash & Gern's first Time-Points, the accusation, and crossing paths with placid, hollowed Tank & Old Man) → the **Day-B reconvene** (four vectors home).

## The two-track spine
- **Track A — "Where's Ricky?"** Blame, "military school," the scared mother, the missing father ([[Wade Vance|Wade]]). Human drama; **runs in daylight (Day TP).**
- **Track B — "What's hunting me?"** [[Tank]] is pheromone-marked and stalked; [[Old Man]] surfaces the monster-in-town idea early. **Runs after dark (Night TP).**
- **Convergence = C0.** The turn: the thing hunting Tank is what took Ricky, and he's on a clock. Earn it; let them treat the tracks as separate a day or two.

## Design constraints
- **Budget:** 4 Day / 2 Night per PC ([[Time Points]]); threshold pricing 1 / 2 / 3–4; a delve is a flat 3. More nodes than one party can comb → forced split-up.
- **Tracks split across the clock:** A = Day, B = Night. Night is only 2 points and now dangerous, so each night spend is a bet.
- **Availability:** ☀ day-only · 🌙 night-only · ☀🌙 both · *flips* (same node, different scene by time — the woods) · *earned* (rapport unlocks after-hours — Russ's late-knock, Jerry's back door: the marked kid's night moves).
- **Location-intro:** core nodes online early; the rest unlock via leads. Don't hand them a map.
- **Nightmare-Engine redundancy:** [[Russ]] is the cheap kill path; the **[[The Library|Library]] folklore shelf** is the backup, so skipping [[Panels]] no longer dead-ends the kill.
- **Negotiation stays hidden** (Ss 1–4, per [[The Negotiation System]]). Everything = roleplay + check + TP; Ricky's disposition moves invisibly.
- **The clock is mental math:** window (C2) − days-elapsed (C3, from the accusation). No stage-reading.
