# Episode 2 — The Hush — Town-Time Investigation Map

Up: [[Episode 2 — The Hush]]

**The [[Time Points|Town-Time]] investigation for [[Episode 2 — The Hush]], structured as two parallel tracks that converge. Locks the information inventory, the node roster (where each fact lives, why a PC goes there *before* knowing what's there, and how the interaction resolves), and three incidental NPC intros. The roster and structure are locked; individual node contents are skeletal and flagged for fleshing.**

## The two-track spine

The episode runs on two investigations that look unrelated until they click together.

- **Track A — "Where's Ricky?"** Pure human drama, no monster required. [[Ricky Vance|Ricky]] is gone; the PCs get blamed; the town's story is "military school"; his mother is scared; his father [[Wade Vance|Wade]] is also missing. This is where Town-Time and its social/TP pressure are introduced.
- **Track B — "What's hunting me?"** One PC is pheromone-marked and stalked at night; they try to work out what the thing *is*.
- **The convergence = C0.** The turn of the episode is realizing *the thing hunting [PC] is what took Ricky — and he is on a clock.* Before that click, a party can burn a day (or two, if slow) treating these as separate problems. That delay is a feature: it earns the "it's the same thing" moment and keeps the clock quiet until it suddenly isn't. Seed connective tissue so the click is earned, not handed over (both threads trace to the mill / dare night; the sheriff's "wild dog" vs. the thing [PC] hears at night).

## Design constraints

- **Budget:** summer = 4 Day / 2 Night per PC ([[Time Points]]). Across ~2 daytime cycles before the night climax, TP is real but finite. The map deliberately holds **more nodes than any one party can fully comb**, forcing the split-up — which makes the marked-kid-as-liability a live tactics problem.
- **Threshold pricing:** 1 pt = confirm one fact / check-in; 2 pt = a real find or meaningful talk; 3–4 pt = comb through / revelation. A delve is a flat 3.
- **Negotiation is still hidden** (Sessions 1–4, per [[The Negotiation System]]). Nothing here is a formal negotiation; everything resolves as roleplay + a check + a TP spend. Ricky's disposition moves invisibly behind the rescue.
- **Location-intro meta-rule:** don't hand them a map. A few nodes are online at open (pulled into focus by the reassembly + the smear); the rest unlock via leads.
- **Nightmare-Engine redundancy:** [[Russ]] is the *cheap* path to the kill, never the only one. A party that never enters [[Panels]] can still assemble the solution the hard way.
- **The clock is mental math, not forensics.** Deadline = the ripening window (C2) minus days-elapsed-since-taken (C3). There is no "read the ripening stage off physical evidence" step.

## The three incidental intros

- **[[Wesley Crane|Wesley]] at the Mansion.** Cordial, helpful, neck-deep in the Mercer papers (which quietly explains why he's *there*, and dovetails with [[Dominique Ferraro|Dominique]]'s drift into those papers). **Zero suspicion** he is the dungeon figure. Whether he recognizes the PC from the dungeon is a **roll** — a failed roll where he *doesn't* clock them is its own creepy beat in hindsight.
- **[[Reverend Hollis]] — the hooded-man red herring.** A hardline preacher bleeding his flock to Wesley's [[The Community Center|Community Center]]: robed, sanctimonious, cold to the kids, pointed questions ("*what were you children doing at that mill?*"). Players will *want* him to be the hooded man. The twist that makes him more than a decoy: he is **right** that Wesley is dangerous, but so unpleasant and so obviously motivated by lost tithes that the players dismiss the one true signal in the noise — mirroring [[Russ]]' dismissed distrust and reinforcing [[The Reality Filter|the Filter]] theme (the truth-teller discredited by being insufferable).
- **[[Wendell Pace]] — alive and hollowed.** The PCs watched him die and dissolve in the [[The Pillared Reach|Pillared Reach]]. Now he's topside, alive, doing his janitor's routine — visibly *off* (Insight catches it). Seeds **"death in the dungeon isn't exactly death"** in case no one actually died in Session 1. Efficient placement: janitoring the Mansion (one town-history trip plants both Wesley and his hollowed victim, connected by neither); alternatively anywhere the kids would recognize him.

## Open items (flagged, not yet built)

3. **C1 backup path** — if they skip Russ, confirm the fallback (Mansion monograph, or trial-and-error at the den) per the Nightmare-Engine principle.
4. **Strict/Lax parents TP-modifier debut** — fold into the blame beat (grounding literally costs the scapegoat TP)?
5. **Which PC is scapegoated vs. marked** — steer to *different* PCs; DM prep per variant.
