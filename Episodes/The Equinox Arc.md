# The Equinox Arc

Up: [[Episodes]]

**The Act 1 climax: the PCs know Wesley is the antagonist but not his plan. Three-to-four sessions of figuring out the ritual, locating the venue, choosing a disruption strategy — capped by the ritual itself as a multi-character negotiation-as-combat.** Climaxes Sunday night, September 22. The part of the campaign that *can* be planned in detail (fixed deadline, known targets) — plan it when you get there.

## The arc shape
The PCs work toward the equinox:
1. **Figure out the [[The Equinox Ritual|equinox ritual]] is happening**
2. **Locate [[The Mercer Mansion|the mansion]] as the venue**
3. **Identify the recruits** ([[Wesley's Recruits]])
4. **Choose a disruption strategy** — treat as a small **heist-prep + execution** sequence

The ritual itself is the climactic **2-session event**, structured as a multi-character **negotiation-as-combat** (bible §8; see [[The Disposition Tracker|The Negotiation System]]).

## The climax — negotiation-as-combat
The scene runs as combat (initiative, rounds, actions) but is functionally a multi-character negotiation. The PCs argue to sway each Rival away from [[Wesley Crane]]; Wesley counter-argues and progresses the ritual. The number of Rivals swayed determines the aftermath. The summer's disposition-building is **cashed in** here — each recruit's accumulated disposition sets their starting Interest and Patience.

> [!danger] DM-only
> **Tally → aftermath** (full detail in [[Aftermath Branches]]):
> | Recruits at Interest 4+ | Aftermath |
> |---|---|
> | 3+ | Rival Party path |
> | 2 | Compromised toward Rival |
> | 1 | Compromised toward Evil |
> | 0 | Evil Party path |
> | **[[Dominique Ferraro]] reached (Interest 4+)** | **Ritual fully prevented — gate stays sealed** |
>
> **Ritual Progress Track** (separate from per-Rival tally): items the PCs prevented Wesley acquiring over the summer reduce the starting count. All 3 [[The Three Ritual Items|items]] intact = track starts 3/5; 0 items = ~impossible for Wesley to finish in time. The more time before Wesley closes the track, the more disposition the PCs can build in the moment.
>
> **Wesley survives all branches** (assuming the PCs didn't kill him directly). The equinox is about whether his recruits get anti-embers, not whether he lives. **Wesley is not subject to the negotiation system** — attempts to negotiate him will fail. He has no death protection outside the dungeon, so a direct interruption at the mansion is on the table. If killed, [[Dominique Ferraro]] carries the ritual forward unless pulled back.

## Final disposition tally
Going in, each recruit's summer disposition sets their starting Interest/Patience. Recruits the PCs invested in are approachable; the neglected/alienated start Wary or Hostile. [[Dominique Ferraro]] is the special case — she's the bloodline anchor, and reaching her (Interest 4+) means the ritual cannot complete regardless of the others. But she isn't won by summer Time-Points like the recruits: she kept full agency, so she's reachable *only* if a PC refuses the woman-in-the-van misdirection and confronts her as a person (see [[Dominique Ferraro]], [[The Other Party (Rival or Evil)]]).
