# Episode — The Mirror

Up: [[Episodes]]

**A cursed Mercer-family mirror in the back room at [[Panels]] starts showing figures that aren't there — and the episode lands two anchors: [[Eddie Park]] enters the PCs' orbit, and [[Russ]]'s 1991-sister tragedy becomes load-bearing.** Featured Rival: [[Eddie Park]] (starting disposition 2 — Neutral). Wesley fingerprint level: **none** — the Mirror predates him by decades.

## The monster
[[The Mercer Mirror]] — on display in [[The Mercer Mansion]] for decades, sold at an '83 [[Mercer Falls Historical Society|Society]] auction, cycled through estate sales, bought cheap by [[Russ]] in '89. Recently it shows figures not in the room and moves when unwatched. A kid who looked too long has nightmares of the figure walking *out* toward him.

> [!danger] DM-only
> The thing in the mirror is an **Order-touched echo from one of [[Elias Mercer|Mercer]]'s earliest experiments** — older than [[Wesley Crane]] entirely (see [[Order and Chaos]]). A [[Cosmology Seeds|cosmology seed]]: the first material connection between the PCs' weird problems and the Mercer family specifically. Mercer-as-previous-user is months from legibility, but the threads are weaving.

## Town investigation
Russ knows the mirror is weird but hasn't acted. Investigation surfaces the **auction provenance** (a [[Mercer Falls Historical Society]] thread to pull), the nightmare-kid, and the mirror's volatility — and naturally pulls the PCs to the **library annex** inside the old mansion to research Mercer-family materials, where they meet [[Eddie Park]].

## Russ's 1991-sister anchor lands here
This is where Russ becomes more than a friendly shopkeeper. Investigating the mirror's history opens conversations about loss and the town's past. **Russ's older sister Annie was one of the seven who died in the [[The 1991 Mill Incident|1991 mill incident]]** — she worked the line; she was 24, Russ 22. Her death is why he stayed in Mercer Falls. Not secret, just rarely discussed; a PC on the right thread can hear it. Russ is now bonded to the campaign's underlying tragedy — he half-knows what happened — which begins seeding the eventual [[The Reality Filter|Filter]]-break.

## Eddie's disposition impact
A PC who finds Eddie at the library annex while researching Mercer materials:
- **Genuine peer treatment, sustained engagement** → 3 (Sympathetic) or 4 (Connected)
- **Uses Eddie as a research-source without a bond** → stays at 2; Wesley's "they just needed the library access" line lands easily
- **Talks down to him / treats him as a kid** → 1 (Wary)
- **Doesn't engage** → stays at 2

After this episode [[Wesley Crane]] begins counter-cultivating Eddie. See [[The Disposition Tracker|The Negotiation System]]. *(Note: Eddie is unknowingly adjacent to Mercer's sealed papers in the annex — a late-game artifact.)*

## Resolution
**Destroy** the mirror (Russ's preference, easiest), **seal** it (Eddie can research how), or **extract** what's inside back to the dungeon — careful handling lets the PCs keep it as a high-value, dangerous magic item ([[Gear and Items]]).
