# Episode 1 — The Hush

Up: [[Episodes]]

**The campaign's first full MOTW: the dare-night creature comes home to hunt. Session 1 is the dungeon descent ([[Session 1 — The Dare]]); Session 2 opens town-mode, introduces [[Time Points]], and culminates in a night confrontation with [[The Hush]].** Featured Rival: [[Ricky Vance]] (starting disposition 2 — Neutral). Wesley fingerprint level: **none**.

## Session 1
Run [[Session 1 — The Dare]] — the five-block opener ending on the wake-up. The Hush chases on the mill grounds; the PCs escape down the hatch; the adult gets into [[The Mill]] but **breaks off at the hatch** — it hears [[Ricky Vance]] and takes him instead (the cold-open hunt) — and doesn't follow into the dungeon. After the TPK-or-escape, the PCs wake at home — **the Hush is still loose.**

## Session 2 — The Hunt
Town-mode opens; [[Time Points]] are introduced. Something bad has happened in a PC's life (a pet killed, a sibling chased, a body found near the woods). Investigation paths:
- Variants of "I saw something near the mill last week" (dog walker, paperboy, gas-station attendant)
- The sheriff isn't following up — official line "wild dog" (the [[The Reality Filter|Filter]])
- A mom mentions last spring's bicyclist who never came home (the Hush's previous, never-found kill)
- Tracking the woods: a blood trail, deep gouges in tree bark at chest height

The PCs identify the creature's hunting style by mid-investigation, then resolve it their way — bait, ambush, or hunting it at dusk. Research runs through [[Russ]] at [[Panels]] (the [[The Nightmare Engine|Nightmare Engine]]'s first in-play demonstration: walk in with a description, walk out with the kill condition). A tough fight even with the sound-misdirection trick.

> [!danger] DM-only
> **Episode-1 prep layers two concurrent threads onto Session 2.** (1) The *supernatural-personal*: the adult Hush, tracking the spore-marked PC's voice from the Session-1 juvenile, stalks at night. (2) The *social*: **Ricky has disappeared** — actually hollowed, but the PCs don't know that. The [[The Reality Filter|Filter]] closes the case fast ("his dad shipped him to boarding school"); only Ricky's bully clique pushes back, blaming a PC for "telling." This is the campaign's first sustained social-pressure beat, and **Ricky's disappearance is unresolved at episode's end** — the heat carries into [[Episode 2 — The Hungry House]].
>
> Per the prep ref this re-frames the public Bizarro-Plot version (where Ricky is alive and confront-able). Either way his disposition lands on whether the PCs *acted* well in his name — see below.
>
> **Optional second delve** for the *stone of quietude* (Mercer-era Silence artifact; leading candidate location the Pillared Reach); makes the climax easier but is never required. See [[The Keystone Tablet]] / [[Gear and Items]].

## Ricky's disposition outcomes
The dare-night confrontation question lives here (whether handled as a live confrontation or as acting in his name during the disappearance):
- **Recognition / treats him as someone trying to change** → shifts to **3 (Sympathetic)**
- **Confronts angrily, dismissively, or uses dare night as ammo** → **1 (Wary)**, or **0 (Hostile)** if brutal
- **Ignores him entirely** → stays at **2 (Neutral)**; Wesley works him from there
- **Incidental friendly small talk, no commitment** → stays at **2**, slightly favorable

After this episode [[Wesley Crane]] starts paying attention to Ricky and to the engaged PCs; his counter-cultivation begins. See [[The Negotiation System]].

## Aftermath & seeds
Body in dawn light has a **tooth made of something that isn't bone** — dungeon-stone material, a [[Cosmology Seeds|cosmology seed]] to call back in [[Episode 3 — The Click]] (and the recurring-material clue in [[The Pattern Recognition Arc]]). Sheriff's report: "bear." A hooded figure remains an open question (the slow-burn [[Wesley Crane]] recognition arc). The mill-area chalk reads as background ([[Wesley's Sigils|Tally Stone delve]] residue).
