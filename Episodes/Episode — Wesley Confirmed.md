# Episode — Wesley Confirmed

Up: [[Episodes]]

**School starts, the daily budget compresses, [[Phyllis Harrow]] is hollowed (and the Weight retrieved), and the PCs reach **click 2**: it's that guy at the community center.** This is the campaign's pivot into the equinox arc. No new featured Rival. Wesley fingerprint level: PCs are now running their own investigation alongside the monster work. Lightly sketched (design 1–2 ahead).

## Window & budget
**Early September.** School starts; the [[Time Points]] daily budget compresses from summer's 4 Day / 2 Night to **2 Day / 2 Night.**

## The cornerstone
Tied to the **second [[The Three Ritual Items|Weight]] attempt**: **[[Phyllis Harrow]] hollowed early September; Wesley retrieves the Weight** (the first attempt failed in [[Episode — First Real Wesley Suspicion|First Real Wesley Suspicion]]). She dies days later in a household accident. The downstream manifestation can be **stranger or larger** than previous episodes — the dungeon is more excited as it nears whatever Wesley's been building toward.

> [!danger] DM-only
> **Click 2:** *it's that guy at the community center.* By now the PCs' disposition with each Rival is substantially shaped, and this episode escalates the *threat* to those relationships — **Wesley sequesters his recruits in preparation for the equinox** (see [[Wesley's Recruits]], [[Wesley's Summer Calendar]]). From here, the rest of the campaign is the equinox arc ([[The Equinox Arc]]).
>
> The **faceup conversation with [[Wesley Crane]]** happens around here if it hasn't already; he turns the warmth on, and the PCs leave knowing.

## Negotiation system goes explicit
[[The Disposition Tracker|The Negotiation System]] becomes explicit around now (bible §8): the DM proactively flags significant social scenes as negotiations and hands the players a one-page rules reference ([[Negotiation Rules Reference]]). The Wesley faceup may itself be the explicit-reveal moment — though it's an **unsuccessful** negotiation, since **Wesley is not subject to the system** (too far hollowed to be reached by argument).
