## Beat 1 — The Dare

**Summary:** Cold-open here. Locked in, a beat to camp or explore, then the grounds turn wrong and the chase begins.

**Outline**
- **Cold Open: "The Lock"** (read-aloud below) — open the session with this; the gate is already chained, the bullies' last line tossed over a shoulder. The interaction has already happened.
- Give the table a few minutes to roleplay: camp, fortify, or explore. Ask each player WHY their character accepted the dare; settle "do you all know each other?" if needed.
- **Exploring the Grounds** (read-aloud below) — offer if they wander rather than camp. Five years closed (since '91), mid-June; interrupted, not ruined. Exploration is also how they reach the **Grounds Finds** (Treasure below).
- Seed: at least one bully (Ricky) is a future rival/recruit — and Session 2's cold-open POV. Don't say which.
- The Hush is already hunting — circling in passes: each sound the same KIND, from a different quarter, closer in. Don't call a check yet; narrate the passes (Checks below).
- Perception sets WHEN they clock it, not whether: a sharp kid connects it at Pass 2 and can warn the group; everyone gets it by Pass 3. Read **"The Circle Tightens."**
- Then it goes still — it knows it's been noticed. Read **"It Goes Still."** No fight is implied; the only move is to run.

**Read-alouds**

> **The Lock** *(top of session, before any play)*
> The chain goes through the gate in a long metal whisper, link by link, and the padlock closes with a sound like a single knuckle cracking. Someone on the other side gives it a rattle — testing it, or just enjoying it.
> *"We'll be back when it's light. Try not to die or whatever."*
> Then bike tires on gravel. Laughter, already turning into other conversations, already about something else. The sound of them gets smaller and smaller down the access road until the road takes them and there's nothing left of it at all.
> And the quiet comes in to fill the space they left. It isn't empty quiet. The slag heap ticks somewhere off to your right, still letting go of a heat it banked up years ago. Water is running, low, in a place where no water should still be moving. Mercer Falls Steel was built to be loud — built to run hot and never stop, built for hundreds of people and a noise you could feel in your teeth — and it has been holding its breath for five years, and it just heard the last of the noise leave.
> The dark settles over the yard like it's been waiting for the chance.
>
> *DM note: then hand it to the table — "You're locked in. What do you want to do?" Don't telegraph; every off detail has a mundane explanation a kid would reach for.*

> **Exploring the Grounds** *(optional — if they explore rather than fortify/camp)*
> Past the gatehouse, the grounds open into a wide gravel yard with weeds pushing up through it. The main buildings stand around it — square, intact, glass mostly unbroken; five years closed isn't long enough to ruin them. By the time-clock shed, a hard hat still hangs on its nail, faded almost white. A bulletin board behind cracked glass holds a softball schedule and an overtime notice from the summer the mill closed. On a windowsill, a coffee mug someone set down and never came back for. Nothing's been smashed or stripped — it's less a ruin than a place where everyone stood up mid-shift and walked out, and it's been sitting dark ever since.
>
> *DM note: mid-June 1996; closed in '91. Still no threat named — the unease comes from the frozen-in-place detail, not from telling them it's creepy. The out-buildings (machine shop, locker building, office annex) are enterable — that's where the Grounds Finds live.*

> **The Circle Tightens** *(when a PC clocks the pattern — Pass 2 for a sharp kid, Pass 3 for all)*
> It takes a second to understand why your skin's gone tight, and then you have it: the sounds. The first was over by the gate. The next, a while back, came from the other side of you — the cooling house. And that scrape just now was between them, and closer. You've been hearing it for ten minutes, and only now do the dots pull into a line: it's the same sound each time, and each time it's nearer, and it's going *around* you. Whatever's making it isn't wandering. It's pacing a circle, the circle is getting smaller, and you're standing in the middle of it.
>
> *DM note: best delivered BY the player who made the check — let them be the one who says "shut up and listen." Don't name a creature; the pattern is the fear.*

> **It Goes Still** *(the button — right after the realization; the first "sight")*
> The next sound doesn't come. The grounds go flat and silent in a way that's worse than any noise — and out past the slag heap, where the dark is thickest, something that had been moving stops. You don't see it so much as see the place the moonlight won't fill: a tall stillness, holding, angled at you the way you'd angle at something you'd already decided to catch. Nobody has to say it.
>
> *DM note: it went still because it knows they noticed — the stalk is over, the pounce is next. A great Perception check here can confirm the wrong-light cue — earned, not handed. Then the Outside Chase.*

**Checks — The Circling (3 passes)**

- **Pass 1 · far, by the gate** — passive 15. Narrate only: a soft padding sound from one quarter (gravel shifting, a pipe tick). No check called.
  - ✓ *Flags it* — passive Perception 15+ (or a kid actively listening) files it as "not the wind, not the building settling." No alarm; the first dot. → *"Something shifts the gravel over by the gate — soft, padding, there and gone. Probably a stray."*
  - ✗ *Dismisses it* — most write it off. Fine; the pattern needs a second dot before it exists.
- **Pass 2 · nearer, to the side** — DC 12 Perception (or passive 12+). Same KIND of sound, different quarter, closer.
  - ✓ *Connects it → can warn the group* — THE gift beat: let that player say "shut up and listen," hand them "The Circle Tightens." → *"It's the same sound as before — but it came from the other side of you this time. And it was closer."*
  - ✗ *Another night-noise* — no connection yet; they get it at Pass 3.
- **Pass 3 · close, behind** — auto. The third sound lands BETWEEN two earlier ones, near enough to feel. Everyone clocks the tightening ring → read both read-alouds → run. Go to the Outside Chase.

**Treasure — Grounds Finds (optional Easter eggs, found only by exploring the out-buildings)**

*Design rules: each item is mundane-first (a curious kid's find, not an obvious clue), requires curiosity to get (a drawer, a locker, behind something), and never gates anything — pure reward for looking. Describe the object plainly and let the oddness sit; don't editorialize. None of these payoffs need to exist yet — they're specific enough to feel meaningful and vague enough to attach to whatever gets invented later.*

*Tier 1 — hooks into people/threads that already exist:*
- **The crew photo with someone scratched out** — time-clock shed or a locker. A pinned shift-crew photo, late-'80s, everyone squinting in the sun — one face deliberately scratched or burned out, *recently*, not by age. Names in pen on the back; the scratched one's name is smeared. *Payoff menu: the scratched person is someone alive in town now; or Russ's sister Annie (a crew photo from before '91 is a gut-punch waiting); or a future hollowing victim. Decide later — you just need a name you can eventually say "oh, that's who that was."*
- **A second hard hat with a date that's wrong** — a locker. A name stenciled inside and a date written in marker — a date *after* the mill closed in '91. Someone was here, in a closed mill, wearing crew gear, after it shut. *Payoff: whoever's been using the mill since (Wesley's prep), or the dungeon's caretakers. Specific and totally deniable.*
- **A child's drawing taped inside a locker** — a parent's locker, clearly. Crayon: the family, the mill, a dog. Sweet — until one detail lands: the mill has a door in the ground, or a tall figure with no face stands at the edge, or the sun is scribbled out. Kids draw what they see. *Payoff: a mill worker's kid who saw something, now an adult in town (or who disappeared); or a sign the wrongness predates the closing. Research bait for Panels.*

*Tier 2 — seeds for people not yet invented (person-shaped hooks, cash whenever):*
- **The love-note / breakup in a locker** — a folded note, passed-in-class handwriting, initials only ("R." and "M."). Tender; then a second note, angrier, about leaving town. *Payoff: two adults in town now with unfinished history, one of whom never left — an instant NPC relationship with a decade of grief baked in.*
- **The pawn ticket / IOU** — a claim ticket or scrawled IOU for a real sum, dated near the closing, never redeemed. A name; a debt. *Payoff: someone was desperate when the mill closed — a clean motive engine for a future antagonist, hollowing victim, or someone who made a bad bargain.*
- **The union list with names crossed off** — a clipboard or ledger (phone tree, layoff list, seniority roster) with a handful of names crossed off in a way that reads wrong — not "laid off" but *crossed off*. *Payoff: a GENERATOR, not a single clue — a bag of names for the dead/disappeared/hollowed; every time a name is needed, pull one off this list and the Easter egg pays retroactively.*
- **The Polaroid of the locked room** — wedged in a drawer: a flash photo of an interior door, chained and padlocked, a date on the white border. Someone documented a door being sealed. *Payoff: points at a sealed space (mill, town, someone's house) revealable whenever — and pre-justifies a "we've seen that chain before" beat.*

*Tier 3 — atmosphere/cosmology seeds (vaguest, most flexible):*
- **Graffiti that isn't quite a threat** — scratched, not sprayed: effortful. A town saying gone wrong, or a folk-name for something (usable later carved in the dungeon, in a victim's notebook). *Payoff: the recurring-phrase device — deepens every time it reappears. Costs one sentence now.*
- **The wrong-pointing compass / stopped watches** — a foreman's drawer: a compass that points *down* (rhyming with, not repeating, Russ's), or a wall clock and a pocket watch both stopped at the same odd time. *Payoff: the wrongness has a physics; these are the first instruments to register it — and a quiet Bash tee-up (his gear picking up the mill's hash later is the grown-up version).*
- **The full ashtray in the cleanest room** — an otherwise dust-frozen office: one ashtray, recently used, a chair turned to face the window that looks toward the blast furnace. Someone sits here. Regularly. *Payoff: the mill has an occupant — pure unease, attach later.*

*Recommended if only locking a few: the scratched-out crew photo (Tier 1), the union list (Tier 2 — highest-leverage single item), and the compass/watches (Tier 3). A personal thread, a renewable cast resource, and a cosmology seed, with almost no commitment.*

**Monsters — the Hush (adult). Chase only; cannot be meaningfully fought.**
- *What it is:* a sound-hunter built like an ear, not an eye. Wolf-shaped front — head carried forward, but NO eyes and a blank where the face should be; great translucent listening-fans sweep back like ears. Tracks by sound in every direction; its many limbs drive any direction, so getting behind it gains nothing (never flanked). Hide eats moonlight — why it "won't land right." Faster in a line than the kids; tireless.
- *Hunt grammar:* Calm = front-first, mundane (a glimpse can read as a stray dog). Searching = SPINS in place, legs steering independently, head swinging late, slow echolocation clicks — no fix; the window to freeze or misdirect. Hunting = pours sideways/backward without turning. Found you = SNAPS front-to-bearing and charges (clicks speeding up). Run on the snap.
- *Voice-mimicry:* plays back sound it just heard — a friend's voice, a PC's own name, from the wrong place; timbre right, affect wrong. Lead with the voice, then the shape.
- *Defensive spore:* only if cornered/hurt (rare for an adult) — CON save or poisoned, plus a lingering acoustic mark. (The juvenile's bloom in the Pillared Reach is what marks a PC for the Session-2 hunt.)

## Beat 2 — Outside Chase (3 beats)

**Summary:** Funnel them to the blast furnace. Track condition.

**Outline**
- Run as SITUATIONS, not checks — describe the problem, let them propose, name the skill.
- Failure changes the tactical picture (cut off / stumble / scramble); it doesn't just tick a counter.
- Track condition: **CLEAR / STRAINED / MAULED**. All paths reach the door → bar it.
- "Caught" is never lethal here (Caught & Damage below).
- Optional cat-and-mouse: duck into an out-building to shed pursuit (next move at disadvantage unless they throw a noise elsewhere).

**Checks — Outside (3 beats)**
- **Cross the yard** — DC 10 · Athletics (fast/loud), Stealth (slow/quiet), Survival/Investigation 12 (perimeter route).
  - ✓ *Ahead of it* — the Hush behind them; reach the furnace approach with a lead. → *"You break across the open ground and the wrongness stays at your backs — close, patient, but behind you. The furnace door is ahead."*
  - ✗ *Cut off* — it's now between the PCs and the furnace → "Get past it." → *"It's not where it was. Somehow it's ahead of you now, between you and the only door that matters, tilting its head as if to hear which way you'll break."*
- **The approach** *(if ahead)* — DC 10 · Athletics, Acrobatics, Stealth. Debris field / sagging fence / gap between out-buildings.
  - ✓ *Clean · CLEAR* — comfortable lead; full search window inside (all 3 finds).
  - ✗ *Stumble · STRAINED* — a trip, a knocked drum; door reached, it's right behind. 2 finds. → *"Someone goes down over unseen debris; the clang rings out like a struck bell, and the thing surges toward the sound as you haul each other the last few feet."*
- **Get past it** *(if cut off)* — DC 12 Sleight of Hand/Deception (misdirect with sound) or DC 10 Stealth/Athletics (bull past).
  - ✓ *Slipped past · STRAINED* — by it, behind them again as they reach the door. 2 finds. → *"You throw a stone into the dark and it pivots toward the clatter — just long enough for you to slip the gap and run."*
  - ✗ *Bad scramble · MAULED* — past, but someone grabbed and torn free (1d4–1d6), or the party splits. 1 find; start the inside chase hurt. → *"It gets a grip — cold, wrong, too many points of contact — and you tear someone loose by main force, leaving skin and noise behind."*
- **Bar the door** — DC by condition: CLEAR 8 · STRAINED 12 · MAULED 15. Strength or group effort.
  - ✓ *Barred clean* — full search stretch (per condition) before it finds another way in. → *"The bar drops into its bracket and the door shudders once as something heavy settles against it from the far side. For now, it holds."*
  - ✗ *Barred badly* — improvised or late: shortens the stretch, or a 1d4 parting strike as it forces a limb through.
- **Duck into an out-building** *(optional; machine shop / locker building / office annex — can't be sealed or held, only used to shed pursuit)*
  - ✓ *Break its lock* — cancels a "Cut off"/"Scattered" state or resets a failed beat; but it circles, listening — the NEXT move is at DISADVANTAGE. → *"You press into the dark of the shop and go still. Outside, the clicking circles — patient, unhurried — testing the walls for the sound of you."*
  - ✗ *Throw it off* — cancel that disadvantage (or gain ADVANTAGE on a strong success) with noise elsewhere — a thrown object, the radio. → *"Someone lobs a bolt across the yard; it clatters off a hopper car, and the clicking pours away toward the noise. Go. Now."*

**Caught & Damage**
- *Caught (grip):* 1d4–1d6; an ally must spend their action to break the PC free; pushes condition toward MAULED. Never lethal here.
- *MAULED into inside chase:* start with 1d4 wear + a sprained ankle (disadvantage on Athletics) until the Awakening clears it.
- *Serious bungle (nat 1 / run of fails):* a real but non-gating cost — dropped pack, broken flashlight, twisted ankle. Never the last light source.
- *The Awakening:* clears chase wear — HP top-up + removal of chase-inflicted conditions.

## Beat 3 — Bar the Door & Search

**Summary:** The Hush circles. They search the furnace.

**Outline**
- Search window = condition (CLEAR 3 finds / STRAINED 2 / MAULED 1).
- Three finds, three modes: radio (search), Marv's snapshot (read), evac map (orient).
- Radio = the through-line tool; snapshot seeds the stone of quietude; the map = ADV inside.

**Read-aloud**

> **Furnace Interior** *(on entering)*
> Inside it's dark until your eyes adjust to the moonlight coming through the high broken windows. The space is huge: the blast furnace fills the center — a tower of cold iron and pipework — with steel catwalks crossing overhead. Water runs somewhere below the floor (the river, channeled under the building). Along one wall is a glassed-in foreman's office, its windows mostly intact. Near it, a row of lockers, a workbench, and scattered tools. Everything wears years of dust.
>
> *DM note: the search room. Foreman's office → the snapshot; lockers & workbench → radio, flashlight, pry bar, first-aid; the stair / channeled river below → the way down. The heavy main doors are what they bar.*

**Treasure — the furnace finds**
- **Transistor radio** (ITEM) — break area / a locker · Search (Investigation). Batteries still good. A portable **noise source** — throw it to misdirect the Hush (inside chase & the Session-2 climax). The through-line tool.
- **Old snapshot, writing on the back** (INFO) — foreman's office / pinned in a locker · read & attend (Investigation / Perception / History). A small flash-lit snapshot of a low stone room that is plainly *not* part of the mill — older, wrong-angled — a fist-sized grey stone wedged into a niche in the far wall. On the back, in ballpoint:
  > *"MARV'S SPOT. Took the quiet rock home for some peace and got it — whole house went silent as a grave, slept clean through his alarm and half of Tuesday, near got himself canned. Hauled it back down and wedged it here himself so he'd quit 'borrowing' it. Leave it be. (He don't know we kept the picture.)"*
  - *What it is:* a worker took the silence-stone home, it worked far too well, and he sealed it back below where he couldn't be tempted — the photo marks the exact spot → **lead to the stone of quietude.**
  - *Payoff:* if a PC pockets this now, then later enters that room (DM: pick which dungeon room it shows — deliberately NOT a room on tonight's path; the stone sits off Wesley's trail, location TBD), Perception/Investigation → "you've seen this room before"; a strong success → they pull the photo, it's an exact match, and it becomes a **map to the stone's exact hiding spot.**
- **Evac map / stair** (EDGE) — stairwell / maintenance route · orient (Survival / Investigation; follow the draft & river-sound). Knowing the route to the sub-level hatch grants **ADVANTAGE on the inside chase** (or begin already at the stair-head).
- **Flashlight** (MINOR) — workbench / locker · search. Reliable light, a free hand.
- **Pry bar** (MINOR) — tool rack · search. Improvised weapon (club/crowbar); helps force a stuck door later.
- **First-aid tin** (MINOR) — office drawer · search. A few uses — stabilize a sprain or a scrape (narrative healing).

## Beat 4 — Inside Chase (2 beats)

**Summary:** It breaches. Break for the hatch.

**Outline**
- ADV on the breach if they found "the way down."
- The hatch from Scattered is the radio's moment — throw it to split the Hush's tracking.
- Lands at the hatch regardless; condition carries.

**Checks — Inside (2 beats)**
- **The breach** — DC 10 · Athletics, Investigation (route). ADVANTAGE if "the way down" was found. It enters via a high window, the enclosed river channel, or a weak wall.
  - ✓ *Good break* — stairs down ahead of it. → *"You're moving before the glass finishes falling, and the stairwell mouth opens ahead of you, breathing cold from below."*
  - ✗ *Scattered* — its entry point lands between them and the stairs, or the group splits → the harder hatch line. → *"It comes through where you least expected, and suddenly the group is in two halves on the wrong sides of it, everyone's eyes white in the dark."*
- **Hatch (from Good break)** — DC 10 · Strength / Investigation / Acrobatics — open the corroded hatch & descend.
  - ✓ *Clean descent* — down clean; the Hush loses interest at the threshold. → *"The hatch groans open and you go down into the cold, one after another, and the dark above closes like a throat."*
  - ✗ *Hand on the hatch* — down, but it reaches the hatch as the last one descends — a hand on the metal from above, a sound through it (1d4 to the last PC). → *"The last of you is still on the ladder when something closes on the hatch rim from above — a sound through the metal you'll hear again in dreams — and then it's gone behind you."*
- **Hatch (from Scattered)** — DC 12, or DC 10 with the RADIO thrown to split its tracking · Stealth · desperate dash.
  - ✓ *Past it & down* — the radio (if used) is what split its tracking. → *"The radio skitters away spilling static, and the thing turns toward the noise — and in that bought second you're past it and gone down."*
  - ✗ *Down, but hurt / dropped* — hurt, or something left behind (non-gating — never the last light). → *"You make it, barely, leaving something behind in the dark — a pack, a light, a little blood — and the hatch bangs shut on the noise of it."*

**Caught & Damage:** as Beat 2. *(The Awakening, next, clears chase wear.)*

## Beat 5 — Down the Hatch

**Summary:** It's moments from getting in — then it hears something better and breaks off.

**Outline**
- The Hush is throwing itself at the hatch — and then, between one blow and the next, it stops. It heard Ricky out on the grounds (lone, louder, easier) and left.
- A Perception success catches the clicks receding fast across the grounds — toward something else, maybe a thin distant sound. Don't explain it; let it unsettle.
- Pays off in **Session 2's cold open** — a flashback to Ricky's POV, the other side of that sound.

**Read-aloud**

> **Bottom of the Hatch** *(arriving below)*
> The ladder ends. You're standing on stone that was never poured — too smooth, too old, colder than the night above. Above you the hatch booms — once, twice, something heavy throwing itself against the metal, the bolt jumping in its bracket. And then, between one blow and the next, it simply stops. The silence drops so fast your ears ring with it. Down here the quiet has a floor to it — and you're standing on the wrong side of something that just decided you weren't worth the trouble.
>
> *DM note: offer a Perception check — a success hears the clicking recede fast toward something else; maybe, faintly, a voice. (It heard Ricky. Session 2 opens cold on that scene, from his side.) Don't spell it out.*

**Monsters — why it breaks off:** the adult is seconds from breaching when it hears Ricky on the grounds — alone, louder, easier than four kids gone quiet behind metal. It simply leaves. Establishes the cold-open tradition: overture on the threat, away from the PCs.

## Beat 6 — Cornered → The Lying Wall

**Summary:** The way down is caved in. Trapped. One wall is lying — find the seam.

**Outline**
- Hatch above (the Hush). The archway deeper in is COLLAPSED — real rubble, tactile (the entity placed it; not an illusion). Let "we're trapped" land.
- One wall is not honest stone. Cues contradict the cave-in: a draft off the blank wall, dust drifting wrong, the slag-hum failing to come through where it should.
- Any approach finds it — each kid in their own idiom (Bash: the acoustics are wrong; Old Man: the draft, and what it means; Tank: the wall just reads "off" to hands that know contact; Gern: the dead spot in the sound). The seam opens — the wall admits it was never quite closed.
- The same cues that reveal the door prove the cave-in was lying. They won't name that tonight.

**Read-alouds**

> **Cornered** *(at the bottom — the way down is blocked)*
> You're in a small, rough-cut chamber of dungeon stone — low ceiling, no furnishings, no other doorway. The archway that led deeper has collapsed: a tumble of stone and grit, settled and solid, a wall where a way used to be. You put your hands on it and it's real — cold, heavy, immovable. The only other way out is back up the hatch, and whatever's still up there. There's nowhere to go.
>
> *DM note: genuine, touch-testable stone. Don't let them dig through; let the dead-end sit.*

> **The Lying Wall** *(as they search the dead-end room)*
> It's the blank wall that's wrong. There's a draft coming off it — off solid stone — cool and steady, carrying the smell of somewhere else. The dust in the air drifts toward it and thins. The slag-hum that should be bleeding down from the grounds comes from the wrong direction, or doesn't come at all. When you finally find the seam and press, the wall doesn't grind or fall like the rubble did. It just… stops being a wall, as if it had only been waiting for you to notice it wasn't one.
>
> *DM note: the contradiction is the tell — the cave-in lied; this wall was always the door.*

## Beat 7 — The Awakening

**Summary:** Together in the made chamber, then FOUR private stations — each a locked, bespoke set piece built from that character's deepest question. The relic comes for acting; the tell is retrospective.

**Outline**
- TOGETHER first: the made chamber — charged, vital, reinvigorating, NOT calm (read **"Awakening Room"**). The shared inscription rings the room — read it aloud (legible, riddle-cryptic).
- Then FOUR private stations, one kid at a time. Each is *pulled* to a scene that is unmistakably built for them — a recreated place from their life, staged as a machine. The others see them go still and "elsewhere," not what they experience. Order is flexible; follow table energy.
- Each station carries **diegetic written text** (an inscription, posters, a post-it) — hand it over or read it verbatim; the room speaks in writing, never in voice.
- **Universal rules:** NO WRONG ANSWER — the ember comes fully whichever branch they take; the reward is for *acting*. Chaos is equally pleased either way (the indifference is the buried tell — never narrate it; every test's real answer is the option the machine doesn't contain). Heroic Inspiration goes to playing the moment honestly true to the sheet — values OR flaws (Bash's test names its aligned branch explicitly; for the others, reward authentic engagement).
- Read **"Surge → Settle"** per kid, varied, at the moment of claiming. Then each **dissolves into motes** and is gone — WITNESSED by the others (same face as hollowing; primes the Buzzsaw room). The last kid goes alone.
- The four tests below are LOCKED. Full character arcs and the reads behind each test: [[Old Man]], [[Bash Whitlock]], [[Gern]], [[Tank]].

**Read-alouds (shared)**

> **The Inscription** *(rings the chamber — read aloud to the table)*
> *You were made small, so the world could hold you still.*
> *Small was done to you. Still was done to you.*
> *Here, nothing holds — here, you are the one who does.*
> *Reach. The reaching is the only freedom there is.*
>
> *DM note: Chaos's whole thesis in four lines — Order is what's DONE TO you (small, still); Chaos lets YOU DO; the doing IS the freedom (the con). Both jaws equate action with agency — there is no "choose," only "do." Reads tonight as "they kept you down; not anymore." Later: a thing that believes whatever moves is therefore free. Ambiguous benefactor — interested, not kind.*

> **Awakening Room** *(when the wall opens — the Chaos register)*
> The wall doesn't open so much as admit that it was never quite closed. Beyond it the air changes — it's the breath before a storm, cold and bright and a little too much, and you pull it in without meaning to. The light here moves. It has no source you can find and it will not hold still. Under everything there's a thrum you feel in your teeth and your breastbone, low and live, like standing too close to something enormous and asleep. You are exhausted. You have never felt more awake.
>
> *DM note: reinvigoration, NOT calm. Chase wear clears here. The room is MADE — deliberate, addressed to them, responsive — unlike the raw mill-stone behind them.*

> **Surge → Settle** *(read once per PC at the moment of claiming — LOCKED passage, vary lightly)*
> It floods in — sharp, electric, more than you're ready for, like a breath of pure cold air pulled too fast. And then it banks. ### Station 1 — Old Man: "The Pit and the Spring" *(locked)*

**The scene.** He's at the bottom of a pit. A rope-ladder hangs out of reach overhead. He stands on a platform mounted on a great **compression spring** — and beside him on the platform, a **stone statue of a thing he loves**. The exit, and the ember, are **below** — in either case.

**The station inscription** *(diegetic — carve it where he can read it)*:
> *You were made the floor, so the rest could stand.*
> *But the world is not a scale, and weight was never love.*
> *Lie down beneath it and it rises only as far as stone.*
> *Or let go — and find what was under you the whole time.*

**The two branches:**
- **Step off himself** *(self-sacrifice)* — his weight gone, the platform launches the statue up toward the ceiling… which is **dead stone**. A statue can't climb a ladder; the rise is **futile**. And where he now stands, the exit is at his feet — the ember with it.
- **Unload the statue** *(save himself)* — the platform launches HIM up to the ladder… which dead-ends. He climbs back down, and the exit — and the ember — were below the whole time.

**Up is always the false promise; the way out was under the weight.**

*DM-only — the tell:* the room asks the one question that has defined his life (*carry them, or save myself?*) and is **equally pleased either way** — it rewards even the *futile* sacrifice. The real answer — keep what he loves **with** him — was never on the menu. That absence is the point; don't gesture at it.

### Station 2 — Bash: "The Basement: the Box and the Rig" *(locked)*

**The scene.** His own basement, recreated down to the smell. **Two devices on opposite walls; touch one, and the other dissolves** (mote-fade — the same visual he'll see again tonight).
- **The black box:** sealed, seamless, impenetrable — and it *works*. Its screen shows the near future; one slider; no way in. The one system he can't open.
- **The open rig:** wires bare, alive, throwing static — and inside the static, a **voice** (maybe [[Dominique Ferraro|Dom]]'s) surfacing and dropping, surfacing and dropping.

**The post-it** *(diegetic — stuck between them; it survives the choice)*:
> *one of these works.*
> *the other just wants to talk.*
> *you already know which one you've been chasing — go.*

**The two branches:**
- **The rig** — the ember is **wedged in the machinery**; he has to *work* it free with his hands. As he does, the static clears to Dom's voice — *"…you were right, man, you were always—"* — and cuts off.
- **The box** — the screen shows him **finding the ember behind an air vent**, the next few seconds of his own future. He enacts it; it's exactly there; and the box goes **inert** — the miracle spent.

*DM-only — the tell:* **aligned = the rig** (the unreadable, the person — this is the test with an explicit aligned branch; it earns the Inspiration). The cruelty in retrospect: the room *nudged* him — the post-it — toward a sealed system he could never open, and asked him to dissolve a voice that might be Dom's to get it.

### Station 3 — Gern: "The Obelisk and the Two Gates" *(locked)*

**The scene.** A **pulley** sits at his hands. Above the center of a great **balance** hangs a massive, sturdy stone **obelisk** — a relic of his received faith — with a **spinning saw** to either side of it. Two tall gates, each topped by a weighing-pan: one gate fronts a **warm party** (faces turned toward him, a kept seat, laughter through the bars); the other fronts a **dark cave-mouth** (nothing). At decision time the party is the obvious magnet and the cave looks like pure exile.

**Carved at the pulley** *(diegetic)*:
> *This was set upon you before you could answer.*
> *You did not carve it; you were carved to carry it.*
> *Now the cutting is yours.*
> *Bury it in the dark, or raise it to the light.*

**The mechanism:** he hauls the obelisk over one pan; that side's saw cuts the rope; the relic **drops** — far too huge to ever move again — sealing that gate **with the obelisk standing in the doorway**, while the far gate counterweights open. **Irreversible.** No retrieval, no take-back.

**The two branches:**
- **"Raise it to the light"** *(haul it over the PARTY gate)* — the obelisk stands lit in the party's doorway, **displayed to the whole crowd**; that door seals; the cave gate opens. His only way on is **into the dark** — but a little way in, his approach draws **one or two figures who turn toward him** (they have faces, but none he'll be able to place afterward). One steps in and **embraces him** — and the **ember kindles between them.** Connection isn't the reward after the prize; **connection IS the prize.**
- **"Bury it in the dark"** *(haul it over the CAVE gate)* — the obelisk seals the dark, **seen by no one**; the party gate opens; he walks into the warmth. The ember sits in a corner **no one faces** — and as he nears it, the reveal: **the ember is the source of all the room's light and heat**, while every partygoer is turned the other way, transfixed by something banal in the direction of the entrance. They were never welcoming him; they were just **facing his way.** He claims real fire from a room full of people who never once looked at him.

Then Gern dissolves from the scene with his ember (the figures simply remain).

*DM-only — the tell:* **same ember either way; Chaos doesn't care which gate.** In retrospect: "raise it to the light" *sounds* like the brave choice — and it IS the one that leads to being seen — yet it costs the welcome and routes him through apparent exile; "bury it in the dark" *sounds* like cowardly concealment, and buys the warm room that turns out hollow. The room **never advertised** either truth. *(Held latent, never stated: the obelisk is his PARENTS' faith for now — whichever way he hauls it, he acts on an inherited thing; the campaign converts it into a chosen one. Optional callback: the relic's light is a specific color that later reads unmistakably as HIS. The faith itself — [[Certaintism]] — pays off in the crawl at the Pillared Reach; see Beat 13.)*

### Station 4 — Tank: "The Pep Rally" *(locked)*

**The scene.** The **school gym**, dressed for tomorrow's pep rally — posters laid out on the floor overnight. Two exits, each barred by a gate: one opens onto the electric **stadium** (game-night roar, the thrill of the sport); the other onto a large, beautiful, **empty banquet hall** with a **throne on a high dais** visible through it. Two **performance-stations** — each a thing the room *watches him do*, not a control he operates:
- **Friends-station — the trick shot.** An easy, flashy shot at a hoop — the casual flex. Easy check; and on a miss, **the ball snaps back into his hand before the miss can resolve** — the room won't *let* the flex fail (an eerie property of the space, not a dice rescue). Its exposed gearing runs visibly toward the **STADIUM** gate.
- **Dad-station — the power-clean.** A loaded barbell he must clean off the floor and rack on a wall catch. **Tough Athletics check** (suggest DC 13–15); **failure is allowed** — he resets and grinds until it's done. Its exposed gearing runs visibly toward the **THRONE** gate.

**The posters** *(diegetic — found on the floor)*:
> *(center / preamble)* **Big day today, Tank. / They want a show. He wants a champion. / Go give somebody what they came for.**
>
> *(friends-station)* **They love how easy you make it look. / Never let them see it cost you.**
>
> *(dad-station)* **They're counting on you to be the best. / Never let them down.**

A player reads it cleanly going in: flick the easy shot for the stadium, or grind the lift for the throne. He performs one.

**The twist:** whichever station he works, **the same gate lifts — the THRONE**; the stadium gate never moves. The exposed gearing lets him catch, a half-second early, that **both chains always fed the throne axle** — discoverable, not a GM cheat; the room reveals its true shape. On the dais the ember manifests as a **crown**; he picks it up and it resonates as his. Then Tank dissolves from the scene with his ember.

**Staging:** from the throne, the **stadium stays audible and visible through its still-barred gate** — he ends enthroned, elevated, looking at the warmth he never reached. "Be the best / rise above" cashes out as a lonely seat overlooking the crowd he can hear but not join.

*DM-only — the tell:* the **asymmetry IS the characterization** — the dad-station permits visible, repeated failure (and makes him earn it); the friends-station *forbids* failure (the flex can never be seen to miss). One path lets the effort show; the other's whole premise is *don't let them see you try*. And **Chaos doesn't care which station** — to it they're the same move: performance aimed at an audience, agency outsourced; the ember rewards the *doing*, blind to *whose goal he was doing it for*. The **absent third station** — do it because HE wants it, with no one to play to — is the option the machine structurally can't contain, and the posters never offer (every line is an imperative to perform for someone). *(Held latent: a crown is what the dad-dream looks like cashed out — elevated, admired, alone.)*

## Beat 9 — Delve · The Cataloguing Office (Room 2)

**Summary:** The first room of dungeon: a century-old office still on the clock. No combat; loot is real and leaves.

**Outline**
- A long hidden office where an 1880s operation catalogued finds hauled up from below — desks, ledgers, tagged shelves, a plate camera. Century-old, and caught mid-shift.
- The stranger's fresh footprints run straight down the century-old aisle without stopping — to him this room is furniture. His hours-old trail over their hundred-year-old workplace: two visitors, a century apart. (A cold character beat — don't name him.)
- Three dials, no dice: the blind aisle (alternating recesses — reads like an ambush hall, isn't one tonight); the impossibly-recent workplace (wet ink, steaming tea — FRESHER the deeper you go); and the room is still working (**"Still on the Clock"**).
- Loot is REAL and LEAVES the dungeon — teach that placed items are takeable. Every recess is worth a Stretch; nothing is required.
- Nothing here names its owner. The ledgers are a slow-burn seed (the same archive as papers they'll meet much later). Don't push it.

**Read-alouds**

> **The Long Office** *(on entering Room 2)*
> The passage opens into a long, narrow hall, and the first thing that's wrong is the light: lamps, burning, in brackets down both walls. The walls are thick enough to open into recesses, staggered left and right — writing desks, standing lecterns, pigeonhole shelves, everything tagged in a neat brown hand. There's a plate camera on a tripod, and racks of photographic plates drying that have been drying for a hundred years. Ledgers lie open mid-entry. On the nearest desk a chair is pushed back, a pen rests across a half-finished word — the ink still wet — and beside it a cup of tea, still steaming. Whoever works here stepped out thirty seconds ago. The dust on the floor says nobody has worked here in a century. Both of those things are true, and a line of fresh boot-prints walks straight down the middle of it all without stopping once.
>
> *DM note: real first, wrong second. The recesses alternate so the whole row is never in view — let the blind corners do the dread. No combat tonight (pre-cleared); on later delves this aisle wants a static lurker.*

> **Still on the Clock** *(trigger once or twice as they work the room)*
> Somewhere down the row, a ledger page turns itself over with a small papery tick. And as you pass the tripod, the camera's shutter clicks — once, softly — like a door deciding to notice you.
>
> *DM note: the operation files THEM too — a plate exposed tonight, developed later, shows the party standing in the office. (If Gern is attending to the room, this is the optional second [[Certaintism]] rhyme — a century-old operation that Accounts for everything it finds; hold it unless the table reaches for it.)*

**Checks — working the room**
- **Read the ledgers** — DC 12 Investigation or History; a Stretch of reading.
  - ✓ *Concrete intel* — finds-by-floor (a taste of what's deeper), a partial old floor-map fragment, the first sliver of a private archive. Goes in the players' Atlas notes. → *"Column after column in the same neat hand: item, depth, date. The depths go much further down than one floor. Some entries near the end aren't objects at all."*
  - ✗ *Surface skim* — the gist only; another Stretch, another try.

## Beat 10 — Delve · The Octagonal Vestibule (Room 3)

**Summary:** Eight walls, two doors, six blanks. Two dead dog-things on the floor. One way through.

**Outline**
- The floor's hub — but tonight it's already been DECIDED. The stranger committed a route earlier today and the room collapsed to his choice: his onward door stands open, the entry door is theirs, six walls are blank, inert stone.
- Diegetically there is ONE way through — no rail, just the room's own logic. His trail runs through the open door (footprints, a spent torch, drag marks), a chalk glyph struck beside it, a draft from the passage beyond.
- The room's content tonight is the TWO DEAD DOGMEN — his kills. Natives don't dissolve; they're lootable, and HOW they died is a forensic read.
- Don't reveal the door mechanic. They meet the eight-door puzzle on their first post-reset re-entry — a real "wait, there were doors here?" beat. Tonight the blanks are just… blanks that itch.

**Read-aloud**

> **Six Blank Walls** *(on entering Room 3)*
> The hall lets out into an eight-sided chamber — you can count the walls — but only two of them hold doors: the one at your backs, and one standing open across the room, dark beyond it, breathing a faint draft. The other six are blank, seamless stone. Something about the spacing is wrong, the way a smile with missing teeth is wrong: this room was built for more doors than it has. The boot-prints don't care. They cross the octagon straight to the open door — past two bodies on the floor. Dog-like, man-sized, wrong in the joints, and very dead. Whatever walked through here killed them both without breaking stride.

**Checks — reading the room**
- **Examine the dead dogmen** — DC 12 Investigation or Medicine.
  - ✓ *How they died* — clean, economical kills, or a scorch-signature matching nothing natural (DM: pick how Wesley's methods should read). Either way: something came through recently, unbothered, and efficient. → *"Two wounds each, no more. Whoever did this wasn't fighting — they were tidying."*
  - ✗ *Just dead* — dead a matter of hours; person or thing, unclear.
- **What ARE these?** — DC 12 Nature or Survival. ✓ Built like something out of a campfire story about the Dogmen — and built to run in packs. Two dead here means more, somewhere on this floor.
- **The blank walls** — DC 10 Perception / tapping. ✓ Seamless, cold, absolutely solid — but the spacing itches: a door-sized panel of nothing, centered in each wall. File it; don't solve it tonight.

**Treasure**
- **Crude dogman gear** (MINOR) — the corpses. A hide strap, a bone-handled knife, a crude sling. Trophies — and proof, topside, that something wears gear down here.
- **A curio** (MINOR) — a belt-pouch. Something small and old — a smooth token, a bent coin. Earl-fodder, or a table mystery.

**Monsters:** the corpses ARE the encounter — it already happened, hours ago, to someone else's benefit. *(Natives don't hollow: people dissolve into motes; the dungeon's own monsters just die and lie there until reset. A body you can loot was never human — the other half of that lesson lands in the Pillared Reach.)*
*(DM only — the hub, for later: default state 8 doors — 1 entry, 3 real paths, 2 fake-door maw traps, 2 closets: one loot, one sleeping dogmen. Opening a real exit collapses the rest to blank wall until the overnight reset. Tonight it's pre-collapsed to Wesley's spoke.)*

## Beat 11 — Delve · The Expedition Post (Room 11)

**Summary:** The forward camp, and the first FIGHT: a real wandering roll on a battlefield built to be used.

**Outline**
- A staging chamber: crates and barrels in lanes, a timber loft ten feet up, an open freight shaft with a heavy crate STILL HANGING from the winch directly over the drop. One corner is somebody's — cot, writing table, footlocker.
- THE FIRST FIGHT LANDS HERE — as a real, open wandering roll. When they work the room, call the check and roll the d6 (Monsters). Whatever comes wandered in since the stranger passed. (It also pays off the Vestibule: THAT'S what the dead ones were.)
- Fight WITH the room: loft = high ground (ladder & crate-ramp are contestable chokepoints); crates = cover; spilled bearings = a slip-lane; rotten planking around the shaft gives way under weight; oil barrels topple or ignite.
- THE TENSION: the suspended crate is both the best combat drop (release the winch — it plunges, crushing anything in/at the shaft) AND the room's best discovery. Drop it and the discovery is gone down the hole.
- After (or instead of) the fight, three finds: the crate's contents (**"The Failed Warden"**), the desk page (**"Third Descent — Day 41"** — a handout, free to read), the locked footlocker.

**Read-alouds**

> **The Forward Camp** *(on entering Room 11)*
> This room was for working. Crates and lashed barrels break the floor into winding lanes; a timber loft rides one wall ten feet up, reached by a ladder and a ramp of stacked crates; and near the far end a square hole opens black in the floor — a freight shaft, a hand-winch bolted beside it, and a heavy crate still hanging from the rope directly over the drop. Someone was hoisting it up and never finished. Loose steel bearings from a burst crate glitter across one lane. And in the near corner, unmistakably somebody's: a cot with the blanket turned back, a writing table with a page lying open, a footlocker with a good lock. A camp a hundred years old that looks slept-in last night.
>
> *DM note: Wesley's prints cross toward the far door without stopping. Give the room one quiet beat — then, when they start working it, call the wandering check and roll the d6 openly.*

> **The Failed Warden** *(if they land the suspended crate safely and open it)*
> Under tagged oilcloth, a thing the size of a large melon: a slack grey sphere, half-deflated, like a bladder someone let the air out of. Where a face should be, there are eyes — too many and too few at once. One central eye, filmed over, dead white. And stalks from the crown — but only three, and they're wrong: not grown, grafted, sutured on with waxed thread, one snapped off at the root. The lids don't match. Nothing about it looks born. It looks assembled, and then given up on. A brass tag is wired to one stalk, in the same neat hand as the ledgers: a date, a number, and one word underlined twice — FAILED.
>
> *DM note: mechanically inert — a discovery, not a haul-item. Seeds: the old operation was MAKING things, not just cataloguing; something deeper was meant to be guarded; a working one may wait below. The desk page explains it ("the fourth would not take").*

> **Third Descent — Day 41** *(the open page on the writing table — a handout, free to read)*
> *Third Descent — Day 41.* The lower gallery yields as before. Two more specimens crated and sent up on the hoist — one of them the warden, or what I had hoped would be a warden. It will not wake. The eyes I recovered below do not consent to the ones I have given them, and the fourth would not take at all; Halloran marks the crate FAILED in the book and will not meet my eye as he does. No matter. There is a thing beneath the gallery that careless men must never reach, and a sentinel that neither sleeps nor tires nor asks what it guards is the only sure answer. I will manage it — the next attempt, or the one after.
>
> I confess that the deeper one goes, the less the fear seems to belong to oneself. It is as though it were *issued* — handed out at a certain depth like a lamp, and taken back when you climb. The same, I think, is true of the will. I find I climb less.
>
> Halloran asked what I intend to do when the work is complete. I could not answer. The question assumes an *after*, and I am no longer certain the work has one — nor that I wish it to.
>
> We descend again tomorrow. I have told the men it will be the last. This is untrue, but it comforts them, and I find their comfort costs me nothing now.
>
> — E.M.
>
> *DM note: free — no check. Don't gloss "E.M." The fear-is-issued line is the campaign whispering its thesis a century early. If a player connects it to what happens to THEM tonight, say nothing and smile.*

**Checks — combat interactables**
- **Release the winch** — an action (no check, or DC 10 under pressure). The crate plunges: crushes anything in the shaft, or drags a shaft-edge enemy down (Dex save DC 12 to catch the rim). THE room-winning move — and the discovery inside is gone forever. → *"The winch spins free with a scream of old iron and the crate drops like a verdict."*

- **Land the crate SAFELY** — after the fight: Strength DC 12 AND a Dex/tools DC 12 finesse (or a clever rig; help lowers DCs).
  - ✓ *The discovery* — down on solid stone; open it → "The Failed Warden." → *"The rope groans, the crate swings its slow pendulum over the black drop — and then it's down, on stone, intact."*
  - ✗ *Down the hole* — a slip, a fray — the crate drops into the shaft and is gone. Don't soften it; the tension was the point. → *"For one long second the rope holds. Then it doesn't, and the dark below takes the crate with barely a sound."*

**Treasure** *(footlocker: locked — pick DC 12 Sleight of Hand/tools, or pry DC 12 Strength, loud. One-time contents; shelf supplies restock)*

- **The desk page** (INFO) — free handout; see read-aloud.
- **The failed warden** (INFO) — the suspended crate; see Checks. Gone forever if the crate was dropped in combat.

**Monsters — Floor-1 wandering table ("the pack floor"). First guaranteed roll lands HERE — roll the d6 openly; the room is built so any result is winnable at Level 1 if they use it.**

## Beat 12 — Delve · The Spiked Corridor (Room 12)

**Summary:** A traveling wave of spikes, four ways across, and one blood-flecked spike as the only warning they need.

**Outline**
- A straight trapped run (~30–40 ft): spike-holes in the flagstones, torches down both walls, an old hoist-chain with an iron ring hanging near the middle.
- THE TELEGRAPH: one spike stands frozen mid-rise, flecked with fresh blood. (It's the dragged man's — they'll meet what's left of him next room.) That's the whole "this is a trap room" cue — no check, no words.
- The wave has NO all-clear moment — waiting for a gap fails. The pattern looks random and isn't worth timing as a rhythm. Most parties commit and eat a hit before the logic lands; that's fine — spikes are a STING, not a threat.
- Session 1 never REQUIRES a solve: running it and bleeding a little is a legitimate crossing.
- The pattern is FIXED forever: the diagonal, the torch, the ring are constants. Whatever they learn tonight is theirs for the campaign (Atlas notes).

**Read-aloud**

> **The Gauntlet** *(on entering Room 12)*
> A straight corridor, torches burning down both walls, the flagstones pocked with neat round holes. As you watch, spikes rise from a stretch of them — hiss, hold, drop — and rise again somewhere else, a wave rolling down the floor with no rhythm you can catch. Something heavy hangs from the ceiling near the middle: an old hoist-chain ending in an iron ring. And a third of the way down, one spike stands frozen mid-rise, out of step with all the others — and it's flecked dark, wet-dark. That's blood. It isn't old.
>
> *DM note: Wesley threaded the safe line without breaking stride; the man he dragged couldn't, and tripped one. Let the blood do all the warning. The kindest thing you can do is NOT let them wait for an all-clear.*

**Checks — four ways across**
- **Run it** — just go. Roll a d4: that many spike-strikes across the crossing; Dex save DC 12 vs each, 1d4+1 piercing (half on a save). Dashing = one fewer exposure, no finesse. The default, and fine — the room teaches caution, it doesn't threaten an L1 death. → *"You run it like hot sand, and the floor bites at your heels the whole way."*
- **Read the diagonal** — DC 13 Investigation; a Stretch of watching. Some tiles NEVER fire — same spike-holes, but dead — and they form a diagonal line across the floor. Walk it and the wave never touches you. Fixed forever; goes in the Atlas. → *"It isn't random. Watch long enough and there are holes in the pattern — tiles the wave always skips — and they line up, corner to corner, like a path someone left on purpose."*
- **Pull the torch-release** — DC 13 Perception/Investigation, examining the FIXTURES instead of the floor. One wall-torch isn't a torch — its bracket sits wrong, or it doesn't burn. Pull it: a counterweight drops, grinding, and the whole spike-field retracts and stays down for the delve (re-arms on the overnight reset). → *"The torch comes down like a lever, because it is one — and somewhere under the floor something heavy lets go, and every spike in the corridor sighs back into its hole."*
- **Swing the ring** — DC 12 Athletics/Acrobatics; lasso or grapple the hanging ring and swing the spike-field; tie off a line and the rest can follow. A miss drops you mid-span onto a spike (one hit: 1d4+1, Dex DC 12 half) or short of the far side. → *"The rope hums, the chain shrieks on its mount, and for one perfect second you are above all of it."*

**Monsters:** empty tonight. *(Later delves: a wanderer here turns the wave into a THIRD combatant — the party that knows the diagonal fights from safe footing while the monster doesn't.)*

## Beat 13 — Delve · The Pillared Reach (Room 13)

**Summary:** The biggest room on the floor, a nest, and a fight already in progress. How fast they commit IS the scene.

**Outline**
- A vast columned hall swallowed in shadow — the largest space on the floor, sightlines broken everywhere. They HEAR it before they see anything: a man begging, then screaming, and a wet clicking rush, bounced off the pillars so nothing can be placed.
- The thing killing him is a YOUNG version of what hunted them topside — eyeless, sound-hunting, nesting here. The dying man is the dragged man from the corridor (the blood was his). Its voice-mimicry can throw HIS cries — or a PC's own name — from the wrong direction as they cross.
- THE CHOICE (the room's size is the pressure): **RUSH** in — it's far; they likely can't make it in time, and rushing is LOUD. **WATCH** from cover — they witness the kill (their first hollowing), then it turns toward the nearest sound: them. **SLIP** past — a sound-discipline gauntlet, not a shadow one.
- The fight inverts everything: darkness doesn't help (it has no eyes). Hide in SILENCE, not shadow. Control your noise; bait it; SPLIT its attention. This is the fight they'll research topside and re-fight against the ADULT in Session 2.
- The man dies hollowed ON-SCREEN either way (**"He Comes Apart"**) — and leaves a **nullprint**: a very visible, seared human-shaped residue on the stone. ONLY exception: spring the instant they enter AND roll hot on round one → they pull him off it alive — barely — and he dies in the Buzzsaw instead (next beat).
- On ITS death: the defensive spore — CON save, and a lingering acoustic MARK on one PC's voice. Let the fight decide who. That mark is the Session-2 hook: the adult tracks the marked voice into town.
- **Gern seed ([[Certaintism]]):** the nullprint is the one planted touchpoint of Gern's family faith in the crawl — he recognizes the shape ("Figure 9"). Deliver it privately; see the DM note under "He Comes Apart."

**Read-alouds**

> **Heard Before Seen** *(on entering Room 13)*
> The door opens onto dark and scale. Pillars — a forest of them, marching off in every direction until your light gives up trying. And from somewhere inside that forest: a man's voice. Ragged. Begging. Then a scream — and under it a sound like wet clicking, fast, and moving. The pillars take every sound and throw it back at you from three directions at once. You cannot tell where he is. You cannot tell where IT is. You can only tell that one of them is running out of time.
>
> *DM note: they enter FAR from the action — the distance is the dilemma. If they hesitate, let the mimicry work: his cry, or one of their own names, arriving from the wrong quarter, timbre right, affect wrong.*

> **He Comes Apart** *(the hollowing — read when the young Hush finishes him)*
> The screaming stops before you're sure it's stopped. Where the man went down there's a light with no fire in it — his body letting go of itself, coming apart into motes that lift like ash off a bonfire and drift up past the pillar-tops, and gone. But he doesn't leave clean. Seared into the stone where he fell, plain as a hand pressed into wet cement, is the shape of him — a pale, scorched outline, arms a little open, a whole person burned down to a stain on the floor. It sits there and it does not fade. The thing crouched over it lifts its head — a head with no eyes, with great pale fans swept back where ears should be — and goes very, very still. It's listening.
>
> *DM note: no ember-fire — he's no bearer; just the motes, and the mark left behind. That mark is a **nullprint** — the residue a hollowed NPC leaves seared into the ground (it rhymes with the ash-stains ringing the Buzzsaw platform in room 14 — same phenomenon, older). The person reconstitutes at the entrance; the nullprint stays. This is the party's first witnessed hollowing, and the motes are the same dissolve they each did in the Awakening — but note for later that THEY leave almost nothing, where this man leaves a full print.*
> *Gern ([[Certaintism]]): hand him the recognition PRIVATELY — he's seen this exact shape before, "Figure 9" in his family's scripture, the diagram his mother drilled him on. Then let him react in front of the others and explain nothing; the horror is "were my parents right?" (Full script + the low-residue misread in [[Certaintism]].) "Nullprint" is a fine word to let the table start using in-game once Gern names it — it doesn't replace "hollowing," it's just what these kids would call the mark based on what they know.*

**Checks — sound is the terrain**
- **Move quiet** — DC 12 Stealth. Silence, not shadow; darkness is worthless against it. ✓ Unlocated — reposition, flank, line up the far door unheard. ✗ It orients — advantage on its next move against whoever made the sound. → *"The fans spread. The clicking stops. In the new silence you can hear exactly how loud you just were."*
- **Bait / decoy** — DC 10 improvised (a thrown stone, a dropped weapon, the radio). ✓ It commits — a full move wasted; SEVERAL competing sources at once STAGGER it (loses its advantage, hesitates, splits between bearings). The lesson the adult will make them use for keeps. ✗ Half-taken — a partial reposition, still between them and where they wanted it.
- **Rush it (loud)** — no check to run, but rushing is noise: everyone who moved loud grants it advantage against them this round. The trade is real — the only way to reach the man in time is to give it every one of your positions.
- **Death-spore** — CON save DC 12, everyone adjacent when it dies. ✗ Poisoned (1d4, disadvantage on the next hour's checks — clears with rest). Either way: one PC's VOICE takes a lingering acoustic mark (let the fight pick who — whoever killed it, whoever was closest, whoever screamed). They can't hear it. The adult can.

**Treasure** *(the corpse STAYS — a native; the man it killed left nothing but his nullprint)*
- **A tooth made of something that isn't bone** (INFO) — pry it loose. Stone — but not any stone topside, and exactly the stone of these walls. LEAVES the dungeon: a trophy, a sample, and a thread they'll see again this summer. (The same tell will be on the adult's corpse in Session 2.)
- **Nest leavings** (MINOR) — the far reaches; a Stretch to search. Earlier prey's effects — a rusted lunch pail, a wallet gone to felt, a ring of keys to doors that no longer exist. Sad, saleable, and quietly a body count.

**Monsters — BOSS: The Hush (juvenile)** — *the nest's owner, interrupted at dinner*
- AC 13 · HP ~25 · Speed 40 ft. · +5 to hit, 1d8+2 slashing (claw/bite).
- **Eyeless.** Blind beyond its hearing (blindsight ~60 ft by sound); immune to gaze/visual effects; darkness and light mean nothing to it. Hide in SILENCE, not shadow.
- **Sound-keyed.** Advantage on attacks vs. any creature that made sound on its own turn (moved loud, attacked, spoke, screamed). STAGGERED by competing noise: two or more deliberate, separated sound sources in the same round cost it its advantage and its bearings (it hesitates or splits its move).
- **Voice-mimicry.** Plays back fragments it just heard — the dying man's cries, a PC's name — thrown from the wrong quarter. Timbre right, affect wrong.
- **Death-spore.** On death: adjacent creatures CON save DC 12 — fail: poisoned (1d4, disadvantage 1 hour); either way one PC's voice takes the lingering acoustic mark (the Session-2 hook).
- *Tactics:* opens committed to the kill it's finishing; noise pulls it off. It uses pillars the way the party should — cover, angles, thrown sound. It does not flee; this is its nest.

## Beat 14 — Delve · The Buzzsaw Room (Room 14, the Millrace)

**Summary:** The robed figure, the sigil, the wave, and a closing window. Not meant to be won.

**Outline**
- The trail ends at a chamber split by a chasm — 15 ft down to fast black water (a fall = swept away, gone) — crossed only by narrow single-file catwalks. On a raised platform at the far end, a robed figure finishes a chalk glyph. Around the platform: ash-stains in human shapes.
- DC 15 Perception for a clear look at his unremarkable face — lock 2–3 physical tells now (build, hood color, a watch/shoe/smell); the recognition is the table's to make over the coming weeks.
- One line — **"You poor things"** — he steps through the glyph and it OPENS: the wave pours through behind him. Weak pack-creatures first, escalating, ending in the Lieutenant and its wolf.
- If the dragged man was saved last room: he dies HERE — caught on the catwalks, he doesn't make the last span behind the robed man before the Lieutenant reaches him. The demonstration casualty, moments before they run the same gauntlet.
- THE GEOMETRY IS THE FIGHT: 5-ft catwalks string them single-file (no surround, no dogpile); the Lieutenant's reach whip hits the front PC AND the second in line; the wolf works a PARALLEL catwalk into the strung-out middle. The chasm is the party's equalizer — a shove is the best action in the room.
- THE SIGIL ESCAPE WINDOW: the portal is the only out, and it's closing (1–2 minutes; 10–12 rounds at most, tighten as drama demands). Equivalent exchange holds — EACH escape spits another tough monster into the room behind them, very likely to drop a straggler covering the retreat. A brutal, real choice; not a free exit.
- Not meant to be won. TPK is the expected outcome and it is NOT a failure state — the next beat is built for every mix of escaped and fallen. **Cut to black on the last kill — do NOT resolve what death means here.**

**Read-alouds**

> **The Millrace** *(on entering Room 14)*
> The last door opens onto noise: a long chamber split down its length by a chasm, fifteen feet down to black water moving fast enough to shout. Narrow iron catwalks lattice the gap — single file, rails you wouldn't lean on. At one end, a great sealed sluice-gate weeps rust. And on a raised platform at the far end, a robed figure kneels with his back mostly to you, finishing something drawn on the stone in chalk that catches your light wrong. Around the platform's base there are stains. Pale ones. In shapes you don't want to name, because some of them have arms.
>
> *DM note: DC 15 Perception for the face — unremarkable, medium build, lean; deliver your locked tells. He does not turn, does not acknowledge them beyond what comes next. Those pale arm-shaped stains are old nullprints (see Beat 13) — the platform is where a lot of people have come apart; a player who saw Wendell's fresh print will feel the rhyme.*

> **"You Poor Things"** *(the trigger — when they're committed to the catwalks, or call out)*
> The figure stands — unhurried, a man finishing a chore — and for one moment the hood tilts your way. "You poor things," he says, in a voice you'd trust to give you directions. Then he steps onto the chalk, and the chalk takes him: no flash, no thunder, just a man there and then not, like a light switched off. The glyph doesn't go dark. It goes bright. And the first thing through it comes fast and low and wrong — and it is not alone.
>
> *DM note: the wave — 3–4 weak pack-creatures in ones and twos, a round or two apart (they're the clock: the party learns the portal is a DOOR while the window is still open), then the Lieutenant and its wolf through last.*

**Checks — the catwalks & the window**
- **Cross under fire** — DC 10 Dex/Acrobatics to keep footing at speed; cover is a girder, a stanchion, not much. ✗ *A stumble at the rail* — prone on a 5-ft span, or caught at the rail — one step from gone; an ally's help action or a round lost. → *"The iron sings under you and for a heartbeat the black water has your full attention."*
- **Shove one over** — opposed Athletics (or a clever push, a spent ability, bearings underfoot at the rail). THE move the room rewards. ✓ Over the rail and swept away — worth more than any weapon they're holding; the wolf near a rail is the softest target. → *"It goes over almost silently — the water is louder than anything it has time to say."* ✗ It holds the rail — and the front of the line just spent its action.
- **Ride the portal out** — no check; reach the platform and step onto the glyph before the window shuts. The cost IS the check: one more tough creature spawns into the room behind them, on whoever's still fighting. Escaping first can doom the friend covering you. Do not narrate past the threshold — resolve next beat. → *"You step onto the chalk and the room folds shut behind you like an eye closing — on everyone still in it."*
- **Spot the face / tells** — DC 15 Perception. ✓ Unremarkable, medium height, lean build — plus the locked tells. This face WILL come back; when it does, the recognition belongs to the table. ✗ A hood, a voice — still enough to itch, later, in a friendlier room.

**Treasure** *(nothing tonight but survival — DM notes for later)*
- **The far-corner prize (DO NOT PLACE TONIGHT)** — a genuinely good magic weapon or armor piece (one of Act 1's 1–2 rare finds) in the far corner across the catwalks, dropped by someone who fell here long ago. Persists — a fixed placed find, one-time. The room's identity forever after: the room you avoid, and the reward for going back anyway. Exact item TBD.
- **Native corpses (aftermath)** — the Lieutenant and wolf stay (natives): crude gear, a curio. The robed man leaves only the SPENT SIGIL — persistent through resets, a forensic study object for braver delves to come.

**Monsters — the wave, then the pair.** *Equivalent exchange: every body OUT through the portal is paid for by a tough creature IN — that's why the wave exists at all (the robed man just left), and why each PC escape spawns one more on whoever remains.*

**BOSS: Dogman Lieutenant + Wolf** — *the wave's spearpoint, a pack alpha built for single-file killing*
- **Dogman Lieutenant** — a modified Pack Lord: ordinary warrior Strength/Dex, AC 15, HP ~35–40. KEEPS Multiattack. No Incite Rampage.
  - *Whip* (reach 10 ft): +4, 1d4+2 — the reach is the point: at a catwalk chokepoint it hits the FRONT PC and the SECOND IN LINE.
  - *Longbow* (opener): +3, 1d8+1 — starts on the platform, shooting, FORCING the party to cross to it.
  - *Optional rider (crueler table):* on a melee hit, target STR save DC 12 or pushed 5 ft — toward the rail. Include it to make near-misses lethal; drop it to keep the block clean.
- **Wolf** — standard (AC 13, HP 11, +4, 2d4+2, Pack Tactics, knock-prone STR DC 11). Does NOT queue: works a PARALLEL catwalk into the strung-out middle, hunting the squishies — its knock-prone on a 5-ft span next to a rail is one step from gone.
- *Tactics:* deny the surround. The Lieutenant holds a chokepoint and whips two ranks deep; the wolf makes the party defend a second front they can't reach. The chasm is the only equalizer — expect (and reward) the shove.

## Beat 15 — The Split Wake-Up

**Summary:** HOW you left decides WHERE you wake. Grounds at dawn, or your own bed with a hole in the week. Roll pilot credits.

**Outline**
- Everyone lands on the same note — the ordinary world, the warmth still in their chest, no way to explain it — but WHERE and WHEN they wake is set by HOW they left. **Waking in your own bed is the BAD outcome;** home is where it set you down like a package. Waking on the dirt means you got out on your own legs.
- **FREE-WILLED EXITS** (rode the portal): held the full dwell, spat onto the mill grounds JUST AS DAWN COMES UP — they wake on the gravel as if they'd camped, exactly the story they told everyone. They remember EVERYTHING; anyone left in that room is, as far as they know, gone.
- **HOLLOWED** (dropped in the Buzzsaw): cut to black on the dissolve. They reconstitute at the mill mouth AS IF NO TIME PASSED — dead of night, glassy — and autopilot home to bed. The two groups never cross paths at the mill. They keep everything up to the moment they dropped; only the walk and the coming day are blank.
- **THE SHARED CLOSE:** the four of them back in ordinary summer, each alone with the warmth — and the proof on the nightstand: everything they carried out came WITH them (a hollowed kid's gear reconstitutes alongside them). Land on *"what do we do now?"* and CUT TO BLACK.
- Session 2 opens cold on Ricky — the other side of the receding clicks — then Day 1 (mixed/escape) or Day 2 (TPK), Time Points in hand.

**Read-alouds**

> **Grounds at Dawn** *(free-willed exits — the portal riders)*
> Birdsong. That's the first thing — birdsong, and dew soaked through your clothes, and gray light coming up soft over the slag heap. You're on the mill grounds. On the gravel, by the cold ring of stones where somebody talked about building a fire a hundred years ago, exactly where four kids camping on a dare would have slept. The mill stands over you — black, ordinary, locked. One breath ago there was screaming and black water and a light like an eye closing. Your body aches in every place it should. And low in your chest, steady as a pilot light, something is warm that has no business being warm.
>
> *DM note: the dwell released them at first light — the campout made literally true. They remember everything, which is its own burden: if anyone stayed behind, as far as these kids know, they watched a friend die. Don't correct it. Let them walk home through a town waking up like nothing happened.*

> **The Walk Home** *(the hollowed — a wordless montage; narrate over black)*
> Night. The mill's mouth — and the dark giving the kids back, one at a time: re-forming out of drifting motes, same clothes, same gear, standing up glassy-eyed, like sleepwalkers answering a bell nobody rang. They don't look at each other. They walk. Out through the fence, down the access road, through a town of dark windows — route-perfect, fence-climbing, traffic-blind — each one to their own front door, up their own stairs, into their own bed. A porch light clicks off behind one of them. By morning, nobody in any of those houses will remember hearing anyone come in.
>
> *DM note: they surface "instantly" — dead of night, hours before the survivors — so the groups never meet at the mill. The Filter smooths the late return for the parents; the KIDS aren't Filtered, and the wrongness is theirs to keep. They lose the rest of today and all of tomorrow; the puppeted day (if run) is the SAME Day 1 the survivors are freely playing.*

> **The Proof** *(the shared close — the last image before black)*
> The last shot before dark: a bedroom, morning, ordinary. Summer going on outside the window like nothing happened — a mower somewhere, a screen door, a dog. And leaning against the wall, where nothing was yesterday: the gear. The pack. The light. The thing you carried up out of a place that can't exist, still smelling faintly of cold stone. And in your chest, when you think about it — and you can't stop thinking about it — the warmth turns over once, like a coal. It was real. It's still there. It is not going out.
>
> *What do you do now?*
>
> *DM note: don't answer the question. Cut to black. Roll pilot credits. The object-that-shouldn't-exist is each kid's physical reason to go find the others.*

**Outcome handling**
- **Full escape:** everyone wakes on the grounds at dawn. Full agency Day 1. No lever needed. They think nothing died but them, almost.
- **Full TPK:** cut to black on the last dissolve → the wordless montage → each wakes in bed with the gap. Do NOT puppet four boring days at four brand-new players — save the full lever for the first LATER hollowing. They don't clock the missing day until Session 2.
- **Mixed:** survivors first ("Grounds at Dawn"), then flash BACK to the midnight walk as the closing image. Day 1: survivors act; puppet the hollowed through one or two flat, useless beats ALONGSIDE them — the contrast teaches itself. Optional chill: a survivor passes a glassy "dead" friend in daylight who doesn't answer to their name.
- **The lever (when run):** mundane and boring, never nefarious — chores, TV, a shrug where help was needed, a reasonable-sounding deflection. One or two stinging beats, not a play-by-play. The dead player watches; that's the mechanic.
- **The TP receipt:** free-willed exit = the 3-TP dwell, paid (they entered with none banked — that's WHY it's dawn). Hollowed = 0 TP, but the rest of today + all of tomorrow are forfeit. Don't teach the math tonight; let it be eerie. Session 1.5 makes it rules.

**Treasure:** everything carried out persists — gear, loot, the tooth, the plate, the photo. A hollowed kid's things reconstitute WITH them: the proof on the nightstand.

## Appendix A — The Awakening AI panel (LEGACY — guest/late-joining PCs only)

*The four core tests are now locked and bespoke (Beat 7); this generator is retained only in case a guest or replacement PC ever needs an Awakening on short notice, and for the dashboard's AI-panel feature.*

**World context (stable):**
> WORLD: "Embers of the Gathering Hollows" — a D&D 5e cosmic-horror campaign set in Mercer Falls, Ohio, summer 1996. The player characters are kids. Tone: the world reads as REAL first, WRONG second; restraint over shock; understatement over spectacle.
> THE EMBERS: relics that grant the PCs their class abilities; manifestations of CHAOS. NEVER named in-fiction — never use the word "ember" in read-aloud text; fire/warmth vocabulary only (coals, banked stoves, a warmth in the chest, something that catches and does not go out). Acquisition = SURGE then SETTLE.
> THE AWAKENING ROOM: charged, vital, almost too-alive — REINVIGORATION, not calm or safety. Air like the prickle before a storm; moving, over-saturated light with no source; a low live-wire thrum in teeth and breastbone.
> THE TEST: each PC is pulled to a private station — a recreated place from their life, staged as a machine — that poses their deepest question as a physical choice with two branches. Diegetic written text (an inscription, posters, a note) frames it; the room speaks in writing, never in voice. NEITHER branch is wrong: the ember comes fully for acting, and Chaos is equally pleased either way — the tell, in retrospect, is that the machine never contained the real answer. The branch most honestly true to the player's STATED ideals/bond/flaw earns Heroic Inspiration — including honestly playing a FLAW.
> DO NOT: invent new cosmology; name the relic; make the room feel cozy or safe.

**Prompt template (input = one PC's stated top value / motivation / ideal / bond / flaw):**
> Propose ONE awakening test for that character in the style above. Return exactly: **THE STATION** (the recreated place and its machine — two branches as physical actions) / **THE WRITTEN TEXT** (the diegetic inscription/posters/note, verbatim) / **THE TWO BRANCHES** (what each choice does and how the ember manifests in each) / **THE TELL** (DM-only: what the machine structurally left off the menu, and which branch is the honest one). Constraints: established tone (real first, wrong second; reinvigoration not calm); no new cosmology; fire/warmth vocabulary only for the relic; under ~250 words.
