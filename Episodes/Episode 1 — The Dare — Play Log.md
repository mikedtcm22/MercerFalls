**The actual-play record of [[Episode 1 — The Dare]] — what the table did, in the order they did it. The canonical account of Episode 1 as played, and the hub for the canon changes it triggered (see the Reconciliation log at the bottom).** All four PCs present: [[Old Man]], [[Bash Whitlock]], [[Gern]], and [[Tank]] (Brock "Tank" Stonecutter).

> [!note] Scope & a naming trap
> Episode 1 was designed as a standalone origin but **ran long — two real-world sessions**: the mill, the escape, and the Awakening in Session 1; the Floor-1 delve and the Buzzsaw finale in Session 2. Beware: "**Session 2**" in older notes means *the topside adult-Hush payoff* = [[Episode 2 — The Hush]], **not** this second real-world session. The transcriber also labels the adult Hush, the juvenile Hush, and the [[The Dogmen|Dogmen]] all as "the creature" — untangled below.

## Session 1 — the dare, the mill, the descent
Locked in on the mill grounds by a boy in a letterman jacket and his crew (the architect is [[Ricky Vance]], un-named at the table), the four fell in together: [[Old Man]] inserting himself out of the woods, [[Bash Whitlock|Bash]] and [[Gern]] on the dare, [[Tank]] there for the prank. They cased the [[The Mill]] grounds, clocked in at the time clock as a joke, and searched the office annex — turning up the first two clues: a **photo of a sealed, chained stone door** and a **[[Jerry's Pawn Shop|pawn ticket]]**.

A rhythmic clicking in the dark resolved into the **adult [[The Hush|Hush]]**, which killed a stray dog and fed. The party learned by accident that holding still and silent confused it (its search state), crawled clear, then broke for the blast furnace when it charged. Inside they barred the door, scavenged (a pry bar, a first-aid tin, an **evacuation plan**, a **radio**, and a second **photo — "Marv's Spot / the Quiet Rock"**), and — when Bash's radio drew the Hush in through a window — fled down the marked hatch. The adult **scratched at the hatch but did not follow** (as designed: it breaks off here). Below: the [[The Ember Chamber|rough-cut chamber]], a draft, a hidden door, and the four lit braziers of the Awakening.

## The Awakening — the four stations, and each kid's choice
Each PC touched a brazier and dropped into a bespoke test. **All four returned with an ember; none took the "third-position" reading.** Bash and Gern each took the explicitly *misaligned* branch; Old Man and Tank took the only real option the room offered, each shaped as their flaw. Every Awakening confirmed the kid's bias — clean level-1 characterization to escalate from.

- **[[Old Man]] — "The Pit and the Spring."** Choice: **stepped off the platform himself** (self-sacrifice), ignoring the rope-ladder overhead; the statue of his **mother** rose and the ember lay in the door beneath. *Read:* the load-bearing / self-erasing answer — the futile sacrifice the room rewards regardless. The aligned option (keep what he loves *with* him) was never on the menu.
- **[[Bash Whitlock|Bash]] — "The Basement: the Box and the Rig."** Choice: **turned the dial on the sealed Black Box** (the instrument that *works*), dissolving the open rig and the voice that might have been [[Dominique Ferraro|Dom]]; the screen showed the ember and he retrieved it (**under his futon** — the design's air-vent detail changed at the table). *Read:* the on-flaw choice — "glowing beats talking," data over the person.
- **[[Gern]] — "The Obelisk and the Two Gates."** Choice: **dropped the obelisk on the dark side** ("bury it in the dark"), sealing the cave and opening the gate to the gathering. Inside, the crowd was transfixed *past* him and the ember sat where a lamp's bulb should be — he got the full hollow-welcome reveal in-fiction. *Read:* the on-flaw choice — "trades conviction for inclusion"; the warm room that never once looked at him.
- **[[Tank]] — "The Pep Rally."** Choice: **worked the Dad-station power-clean** (the barbell), grinding a lift beyond his best; the throne gate rose (as it does either way) and the ember manifested as a **crown** on the dais. *Read:* the dad-dream / Order path — the crown on the empty dais, elevated and alone.

They reappeared to find the entry portal replaced by an **equipment rack** and the cave-in cleared. They armed up, discovered their new powers (Bash conjured a lantern; Gern cast Light; Old Man Thaumaturgy; Tank simply stronger), and noted **Bash and Tank could see far into the dark; Gern could not** (mechanic to lock — see Reconciliation).

## Session 2 — the Floor-1 delve and the Buzzsaw
The party ran the pre-cleared floor in the exact designed order:

1. **[[The Cataloguing Office]]** — the alcove hall with the **1887 ledger** (dip pen over wet ink) and a **phonograph record** cataloguing "curiosities brought up from below" (a lamp that burns without oil, etc.). Bash pocketed the record.
2. **[[The Octagonal Vestibule]]** — two slain [[The Dogmen|Dogmen]] and fresh blood-spatter; a **pouch of very old coins**. (The room's door-puzzle went untriggered — the party just followed the boot-print trail through.)
3. **[[The Expedition Post]]** — the camp over the chasm. Tank first-struck a lone Dogman (muttering "the others always left me behind"); Bash's poison + Old Man's Tasha's Hideous Laughter + Gern's Toll the Dead put it down. Bash looted the desk: a **journal** (repeated descents; created "specimens"; a "warden" that won't wake; a "sentinel" that won't sleep) and an **oddly-shaped skeleton key**; Tank hauled up the suspended **crate** — a grafted gray mass studded with mismatched eyes on stalks, a failed specimen — which the party recoiled from and left.
4. **[[The Spiked Corridor]]** — the flagstone spike-wave; they read the safe diagonal and Gern shut it off via a wall-torch lever. A **blood streak** (Wendell's) ran on toward the next door.
5. **[[The Pillared Reach]]** — the juvenile Hush's nest. They arrived on the **juvenile [[The Hush|Hush]] killing [[Wendell Pace]]**, the school janitor. Bash split its attention (knocking on far pillars) while the others closed; the juvenile loosed its **defensive spore** — Bash resisted, **Tank inhaled it** (the "strangely echoing" cough). **Brock cut the juvenile down.** They **saved Wendell** alive (the narrow-save branch).
6. **[[The Buzzsaw Room]]** (the Millrace) — the finale. Across the water, a green **[[Wesley's Sigils|sigil]]** and a **hooded, robed figure** ([[Wesley Crane|Wesley]], un-attributed) withdrawing through the far archway. The wave came: the **Dogman Lieutenant** (bone-whip reach) and a **wolf**. **Wendell, pressing across the catwalk, was struck and hollowed on-screen** — Gern recognized the null-print from his "Certainology Workbooks." The party never learned who the robed figure was.

## How the episode ended — the split
- **[[Tank]]** — dropped by the Lieutenant's arrow on the catwalk and **dissolved from the room** (no body). He carries the [[The Hush|Hush]] spore-mark.
- **[[Old Man]]** — leapt at the Lieutenant, shoved it off the catwalk into the river and went over with it; struck his head on debris and **dissolved** the same way.
- **[[Bash Whitlock|Bash]] & [[Gern]]** — the last two standing, they went through the far **destabilizing archway** (the NW portal), Gern following Bash rather than be left alone.

> [!note] Resolved — DM ruling (canon-consistent with [[Death, TPK, and the Lost Day]])
> 1. **The archway was the way out.** Bash & Gern took a **free-willed exit** through the NW portal — the dungeon spat them onto the [[The Mill|mill]] grounds after the fixed **3-TP dwell**; entering at night with no runway, they **woke on the grounds at dawn.** The whole party is topside for [[Episode 2 — The Hush]].
> 2. **Tank & Old Man were hollowed** — the ember-mediated dungeon "death." They **reconstituted at the entrance almost at once** (~1 TP; the hollowed pay in agency, not time), walked home **glassy-eyed on autopilot**, and slept in their own beds. As ember-bearers they **recover after the lost day** (rest of the night + the next day on autopilot); they keep their embers, XP, loot-to-date, and every memory **up to the moment they dropped** — losing only the walk home and the lost day.
> Because the hollowed return "instantly" (dead of night) while the free-willed pair are held the full dwell (dawn), the two groups **didn't cross paths at the mill** — exactly as the system intends.

> [!danger] DM-only — Episode-2 timing wrinkle
> Bash & Gern wake clear-headed at dawn; **Tank & Old Man are on lost-day autopilot the following day** (flat, present-without-will). If Episode 2's town-time opens the day after the dare, the **marked kid is on autopilot while the bereaved adult [[The Hush|Hush]] begins hunting his voice** (Track B). Decide whether to lean into that (eerie — he can't respond) or open Episode 2 once the lost day clears.
