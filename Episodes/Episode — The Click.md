# Episode — The Click

Up: [[Episodes]]

**The episode where the PCs realize the problems aren't independent — and the episode that *creates* the Fourth Recruit rather than featuring an existing one.** Its job is the **first click**: *something is going on, and the dungeon is the source.* Featured Rival: [[The Fourth Recruit]] (starting disposition 2 — Neutral). Wesley fingerprint level: **none yet**.

## The monster (open)
Pick whatever feels good — a residue creature, a sleepwalking outbreak, a corrupted dog pack, a thing in the river. The monster is chosen at prep time; [[The Fourth Recruit]] is connected to it (witness, hunted, or tied to its source).

## The function — click 1
This episode delivers **click 1** in the three-phase [[The Pattern Recognition Arc]]: the PCs notice that the [[Episode 1 — The Dare|Dare]]'s creature, the [[Episode — The Hungry House|Hungry House]]'s basement chamber, and *this* episode's source share something **visual and physically present at all three sites**.
- **Recommended click clue: dungeon-stone material.** The Hush's teeth weren't bone; the Hungry House had a piece in the basement; this week's source has it again. By the third occurrence even an inattentive party clocks "this stuff keeps showing up." The PCs realize the dungeon is leaking — not just monsters, but *objects, materials, itself.*

They don't yet know if anyone is *causing* it. See [[Cosmology Seeds]].

## The Fourth Recruit emerges
Structurally distinct from the episodes that feature an established Rival (the Dare, the Hungry House, the Mirror): those feature a Rival who already exists; **this episode *creates* one.** The Fourth Recruit is whoever is in danger from this episode's monster or the key witness who needs the PCs to listen. They don't have to exist before this episode — they're *defined by it.*

> [!danger] DM-only
> **Design timing:** lock the Fourth Recruit's identity, age, name, personality, and motivations/pitfalls when prepping this episode in detail — *defer until then* and let PC interests shape who this person is. ([[The Fourth Recruit]] is `tbd` until then.)

### Disposition impact
- **Successful rescue + meaningful follow-up bond** → 3 (Sympathetic) or 4 (Connected); they may never reach the community center — Wesley fails to recruit them
- **Successful rescue, no follow-up** → stays at 2; drifts to Wesley over the summer
- **Botched rescue / dismissed witness** → 1 (Wary) or 0 (Hostile); flees to Wesley fast
- **Total failure (person hollowed)** → worst case; goes hard into Wesley's orbit, near-0 at equinox

After this episode [[Wesley Crane]] begins paying attention to the Fourth Recruit and counter-cultivates if there's a PC bond to erode. See [[The Negotiation System]].
