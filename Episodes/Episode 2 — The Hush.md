# Episode 2 — The Hush

Up: [[Episodes]]

**A standalone Monster-of-the-Week: the dare-night creature comes home. This is where [[Time Points|Town-Time]] is introduced, where the town starts blaming the PCs for [[Ricky Vance|Ricky]]'s disappearance, and where the heroic climax is a night rescue — pulling Ricky out of the [[The Hush|Hush]]'s den before he's "ripe." Featured Rival: [[Ricky Vance]] (disposition 2 — Neutral, shaped by the rescue). Wesley fingerprint level: none — the Hush was already loose.**

> [!note] Status: OUTLINE / skeleton
> Committed as the working outline for the second episode (formerly "Session 2" of the [[Episode 1 — The Hush|old two-session pilot]], now canonically its own episode). The spine and the explanatory clocks below are locked enough to build on; the beat-by-beat run-sheet is still to be fleshed out. Follows [[Episode 1 — The Hush|Episode 1]] (the Dare); the PCs enter this episode however Episode 1 ended — some at the mill grounds at dawn, some waking in their own beds a day later (see [[Death, TPK, and the Lost Day]] and the reassembly note below).

## The spine (six pillars)

1. **[[Dominique Ferraro|Dominique]] as the connector.** She is the through-line that pulls a loose, not-yet-bonded party back into one room at the start (see *Reassembly*, below). Warm, helpful, unambiguously good news here — and secretly the first turn of the "woman in the van" betrayal (per [[Dominique Ferraro]], every step of her drift must *look like* good news; this episode is where the players fall for Free-Dominique).
2. **The PCs risk getting blamed for Ricky's disappearance.** One in particular is the named scapegoat, but all four are tarred as "the mill kids." The engine and the fiction are in *Why the PCs get blamed*, below. First sustained social-pressure beat; a personal, no-supernatural-buy-in reason to spend Time Points.
3. **One PC is being stalked by the Hush at night** — the spore-marked voice from the Session-1 juvenile. Low-grade daytime wrongness escalating to an active night stalk; the mark is the supernatural thread that converges on the den. See *The mark*.
4. **Exploration reveals the lore that builds the clock and the world.** History of prior disappearances; Hush lore (the discoverable ripening window — *Ricky's clock*, below); the [[The Stone of Quietude|stone of quietude]] lead (paying off the Session-1 Marv's-Spot photo); and a first, un-explained taste of [[The Reality Filter]] via adults being *oddly accepting* of everything (see *The Filter, introduced by texture*).
5. **A delve is on the table** — an optional second dungeon run for the stone of quietude, which makes the climax materially easier but is never required. Ties to the Marv's-Spot snapshot if a PC pocketed it in [[Session 1 — The Dare|Session 1]]. (Location deliberately off Wesley's Session-1 trail — see [[The Stone of Quietude]] / [[The Keystone Tablet]].)
6. **The climax brings the kids to the woods at night**, where they run into Ricky's father — [[Wade Vance]] — already hunting the thing, and he helps them rescue Ricky. He may get hurt (badly) depending on how the operation goes; that outcome sets Ricky's disposition. See *The climax* and *Ricky's disposition outcomes*.

## The shape of the session (to flesh out)
Cold open (Ricky's capture, his POV — the other side of the receding clicks from Episode 1) → morning-after reassembly + Town-Time / Time Points introduced → daytime investigation (the blame, the lore, the mark's daytime wrongness, the Filter texture) → optional stone-of-quietude delve → night: the woods, the den, Wade, the rescue-fight → aftermath + Ricky's disposition lands.

## Explanatory notes (the "why" behind the pieces)

### Why the PCs get blamed
Ricky's bully clique needs a story for why he vanished, and they build the cruelest available one: **"You snitched to Ricky's dad about the dare — that's why he got shipped off to a military-style camp for the rest of the summer."** It sticks for a structural reason — **both Ricky *and* his father are missing** (Wade is off hunting the Hush; see *The climax*), which reads to the town as *"his dad took him away,"* confirming the fiction. The named scapegoat can't disprove it without either revealing a truth he doesn't have or sounding insane.
- **Who gets named:** a DM fork. **Gern** is the most painful, on-theme target — he took the dare at face value, never knew it was a prank, and being frozen out the one summer he was finally getting "new eyes" is his kid-sized mirror with the stakes cranked. **Bash** is the alternative — accusing him gives [[Dominique Ferraro|Dominique]] (his cousin) a blood reason to step in, hard-wiring the reassembly. Hybrid: the clique settles on Gern (easiest target, no protector), but the smear covers "those mill kids," so Dominique intervenes for family anyway.
- **The party crucible:** [[Tank]] ran the prank and [[Old Man]] clocked it — so whether they let the scapegoat take the fall or stand up for him is the party's first real bonding test and the first turn of Tank's guilt lever.
- **The mechanical bite:** let the heat reach the accused kid's parents so they clamp down — grounding literally costs him **Time Points**, making the scapegoat poorer in the currency the episode runs on (and previewing the strict-vs-lax-parents TP modifier).

### Ricky's clock — a *lore* clock, discoverable by investigation
The players must be able to reconstruct "how long does Ricky have" from **facts about Hushes**, not from anything Ricky is doing (they can't see him). The mechanism:
- **What a Hush does with the taken (species fact, discoverable via Russ / the film / a cryptid monograph):** it does *not* kill and eat. It provisions **live prey for its young to learn to hunt on** — and a juvenile can only catch prey that moves and makes noise, so the adult **"ripens"** the prey over days (wears it down toward a panicked, blundering, catchable state). The ripening is a fixed window — **roughly six to seven days from taking to feeding** — with observable *stages*. That window is the outer bound.
- **The twist the players assemble from the specific situation (not the film):** the pupil is dead — the party killed the juvenile in the Pillared Reach in [[Session 1 — The Dare|Session 1]]. So the ripening completes on schedule but the feeding never comes; the adult keeps returning to a finished larder for a young that will never arrive. The delay isn't a process duration — it's a **stalled instinct with no recipient.** (This resolves the long-flagged dark irony: *the kids killed the very young the adult is keeping Ricky for.*)
- **The spore, reframed:** the acoustic mark on the PC's voice carries the *juvenile's* scent-signature. What looks like the adult **stalking** the marked PC is the adult **searching for its lost young** — following the only trace of its offspring that's left, straight into town, straight at that kid. (This is *why* it ranges away from the den at night — giving the rescue its approach window — and it makes the marked PC the lure that draws the adult to the den for the convergence.)
- **The discoverable timeline (the chain that lets a player say "two days, tops"):** (1) *the window* — Hushes ripen the taken over ~6–7 days (film/monograph via [[Russ]]); (2) *the anchor date* — Ricky was taken on the dare night, and the clique's "gone since the weekend" rumor conveniently fixes it, so they can count elapsed days; (3) *stage markers* — physical evidence of how far a ripening has progressed (the note's chest-height tree gouges, scavenger-bird behavior over the den, a call the adult makes only in the final day or two, the pattern of what it's been taking to sustain the prey) lets them place Ricky at *late-stage — day four of a six-day window* rather than guessing. Math: *taken dare night → ~6-day window → it's day four → two, maybe three, days left.*
- **The horror kicker (assembled only at the end):** because the young is dead, no lore covers what the adult does past the ripening deadline — so the deadline is *uncertain*, not a hard number, and the safe read is "get him out before the window closes." Uncertainty at the deadline is scarier than a countdown and keeps them from cutting it fine.
- **Design line:** keep the *ripening window* a stable, countable species fact; keep Ricky's personal endurance entirely offstage. You adjudicate "how many days into the window are we," never "is Ricky still holding on."

### Reassembly — what pulls a loose group back together
Only bites hard in the all-hollowed case (if anyone escaped, they wake at the mill together and can go looking) — so the engines below all work *regardless* of the escape/hollow mix:
- **[[Dominique Ferraro|Dominique]] convenes them (primary).** Bash's cousin, Tank's crush, the bridge who belongs everywhere. She notices Bash is shaken and holding something he can't explain, hears the rumor, connects that these four were at the mill together the night Ricky vanished, and gathers them out of concern. The party coheres *because of her* — the betrayer midwifes the group's existence, and every warm thing she does here is a thread she'll pull later. **This episode is the answer to the "make them love Free-Dominique first" TBD in her note.**
- **The accusation welds them (pressure).** The smear names "those mill kids," so the town publicly makes them a group; the scapegoat needs allies and the others are implicated by association. They band up partly to clear their names — which *is* the investigation.
- **The shared-secret magnet (engine).** Each wakes with the same impossible object and the same question — *am I insane?* — and exactly three other people can confirm they're not. Not friendship; the need for a witness. Stage near-misses before the reunion.
- Run all three, weighted to the table: Dominique as catalyst, the accusation as pressure, the shared secret as engine.

### The mark
The spore-marked PC (chosen emergently by the Session-1 fight — steer it to a *different* PC than the scapegoat so two players each carry a thread). Daytime: low-grade, deniable wrongness — their own name from an empty room, the neighbor's dog that won't settle, a bird dropped dead on the porch — which drives investigation (*why me?*). Night: the stalk goes active and bridges to the den. Frame the "one PC gets the spotlight" risk as a *party-tactics problem*: the marked kid is bait and liability (can't be the one making noise), so everyone's choices matter. Discovering *which of us is marked* is itself an investigation payoff — the marked kid can't hear it; the others can find it.

### The Filter, introduced by texture (not explanation)
First real taste of [[The Reality Filter]], felt before it's understood: adults are *oddly, wrongly accepting.* The sheriff's official line is "wild dog" and won't follow up; the "military school" story is swallowed whole by everyone including teachers; a witness who plainly saw *something* near the mill last week has rationalized it away by morning. The players should feel the fog as an eerie texture — *why is no adult alarmed?* — without being told what it is yet. Pairs with [[Russ]] and [[Wade Vance]] as the two Filter-*resistant* adults they'll meet, which throws the fog into relief.

### The climax
Night, the woods, the den. Two threads converge: the marked PC's voice (drawing the adult) and the social hunt for Ricky. **[[Wade Vance]] is already there** — his bond to his son broke the Filter's fog (the way grief broke [[Russ]]; he's the second Filter-resistant adult), and his covert-operator training is why he can track the thing when no adult will. He converges with the PCs and plays a hand in the kill/rescue (role flexes to how they run it), revealing he's far more than the absentee dad the town wrote off (see [[Wade Vance]]). The Hush is killable now that they've researched it — sound-misdirection, split its attention, the film's "silence in a confined space" kill, and the optional stone of quietude if they earned it. **Most of the glory goes to Dad** — so the rescue does *not* publicly ingratiate Ricky with the party, and resolving the disappearance here means it does **not** dangle into the next episode (though the clique's resentment can linger). Ricky is **not hollowed** — the den is a monster's cache, not a dungeon pocket; the abduction is pure terror (total helplessness, the thing he's built himself against), and that helplessness is what later drives him to [[Wesley Crane|Wesley]].

## Ricky's disposition outcomes
Set by **how the rescue goes** (and whether the PCs engage it at all). He'll never thank them publicly — admitting the kids he set up saved him would detonate his image:
- **Clean** (PCs help; Ricky unscathed, Dad unhurt) → a small *private* uptick: no public warmth, but later he won't throw a PC under the bus when he easily could. **2 → 3 (Sympathetic).**
- **Messy** (PCs help but it's rough; **Dad gravely wounded** in the chaos) → Ricky blames the party → **1 (Wary)**, or **0 (Hostile)** if it got ugly. Thematically the worst confirmation for him (chaos broke his order-symbol) — see [[Ricky Vance]] (the lever).
- **Disaster** (egregious botch only) → **Dad dies** → **0 (Hostile)**, a hard accelerant into Wesley. *(Reserve the father's death for a later, higher-stakes beat — the players barely know him yet.)*
- **Disengaged** (the PCs never connect the threads, leaving Dad to it) → stays **2 (Neutral)**; no bond, Wesley works him from scratch.

Naming the prank / dare night as ammo at any point is a pitfall (shame, doubly loaded because it also exposes the fracture inside [[The Party]]). After this episode [[Wesley Crane]] starts paying attention to Ricky and to the engaged PCs; his counter-cultivation begins. See [[The Negotiation System]].

## Aftermath & seeds
- The Hush's body in dawn light has a **tooth made of something that isn't bone** — dungeon-stone material, a [[Cosmology Seeds|cosmology seed]] to call back in [[Episode 3 — The Click]] (and the recurring-material clue in [[The Pattern Recognition Arc]]). Sheriff's report: "bear."
- Ricky comes home **changed and withdrawn** — which only makes the "military school" story feel true to the town.
- A **hooded figure** remains an open question (the slow-burn [[Wesley Crane]] recognition arc); mill-area chalk reads as background ([[Wesley's Sigils|Tally Stone delve]] residue).
- **[[Wade Vance]]** now has a compact encounter statline (his silence is the counter to a sound-hunter) and a Tier-3 covert-unit seed — set up here, payable off later only if the players bite.
