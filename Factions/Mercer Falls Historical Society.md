# Mercer Falls Historical Society

Up: [[Factions & Groups]]

**The chronically underfunded volunteer-run nonprofit that operates [[The Mercer Mansion]] as a small house museum — and, unknowingly, the institutional cover for [[Wesley Crane]]'s fifteen-year project to get past [[The Mercer Gate]].**

## What it is
The direct [[Elias Mercer]] line is extinct; the last descendant died in the early 1970s and willed the house and grounds to the Society, which has run it as a museum ever since. Limited hours (weekends plus weekday afternoons by appointment), modest admission, three rooms restored period-correct, the rest closed behind velvet ropes. Run on volunteer labor and short on money.

## People
- **[[Mrs. Devereaux]]** — late 60s, the one full-time curator; sharp and possessive of the collection.
- Two part-time docents; a bookkeeper in on Tuesday mornings; a roster of teen and retiree volunteers who shelve, dust, and staff the front desk.
- **[[Eddie Park]]** is on the volunteer roster and effectively haunts the reading room — placing one of [[Wesley's Recruits]] inside the building.

## The archive and keyholders
The mansion's east-wing **library annex** is publicly accessible (catalogued newspapers, town records, ledgers, family correspondence). Behind a locked door at the back is the **inner archive**: rare books, unindexed papers, and Elias's personal diaries — the bible-canonical sealed Mercer papers whose later passages will eventually break Wesley. Access requires a key, held by exactly three people:

| Keyholder | Note |
|---|---|
| **[[Mrs. Devereaux]]** | the curator |
| The board chair | (unnamed) |
| **[[Wesley Crane]]** | major donor, two years on the board |

> [!danger] DM-only
> Wesley sits on the board purely as cover. From the Society's view he is a kindly preservationist propping up an ailing institution; in fact the museum is his unmonitored access to the mansion basement and the sealed dungeon entrance behind the storage shelving. He discovered the basement door during his early board tenure, has studied it for two years, and cannot open it — opening it is the point of [[The Equinox Ritual]]. The Society's records mislabel the door as a disused root cellar.

## For PCs
A primary investigable site. [[Time Points]] spent here surface Mercer correspondence, donation records (Wesley's escalating involvement), volunteer rolls, and board minutes (eventually his "spiritual retreat" booking). The basement is locked and the sealed door is undiscoverable through normal museum access — reaching it requires being invited into the basement or breaking in.
