# Wesley's Adherents

Up: [[Factions & Groups]]

**Older community-center volunteers [[Wesley Crane]] hollows instrumentally — they are not witnesses to the ritual, they are *ingredients* for it. Three of them, spent across the summer to harvest ritual materials.**

> [!danger] DM-only
> Spoiler material. Publicly these are three trusted, well-liked community-center adults who happen to die in unrelated accidents over six weeks. The reality is that Wesley has each one hollowed in the dungeon to harvest [[The Three Ritual Items]] (specifically the Gathered Dust and the Weight), and the deaths are the downstream consequence (see [[Hollowing and Agency-Loss]]).

## The three
- **[[Mike and Doug Tinsmith]]** (mid-50s, brothers; longtime volunteers).
  - **Mike** is hollowed in the **late-July Gathered Dust harvest** (Floor 2). He drowns four days later on a routine fishing trip Wesley casually suggested — a thirty-year fisherman who "didn't see" the current.
  - **Doug**, present at the harvest but not hollowed, grows more committed. He is hollowed in the **mid-August first Weight attempt** (Floor 3), which fails; Wesley aborts via sigil. Doug dies days later in a household accident (falls off a ladder).
- **[[Phyllis Harrow]]** (50s, widowed; recent recruit, the center filled a hole). Hollowed in the **early-September second Weight attempt**, which succeeds. She dies in the following days — household/bathtub accident.

## Cover and forensics
All three deaths are *accurate* — the drowning, the fall, the household mishap all really happened. [[The Reality Filter]] smooths over the question of why three community-center adults died inside ~6 weeks.

What PCs can find:
- Each adherent's **reconstitution-and-walk-home** is witnessable at [[The Mill]] or along the route on the right night.
- The chamber where each was hollowed carries **fresh, dense residue** for a day or two (civilian residue is heavy — see [[Hollowing and Agency-Loss]], [[Forensic Delving]]).
- The aboveground "accidents" are investigable — the foul play happened upstream.
