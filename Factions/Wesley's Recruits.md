# Wesley's Recruits

Up: [[Factions & Groups]]

**The four troubled-but-talented kids [[Wesley Crane]] has cultivated as the witnesses his ritual requires — each built as the opposite-pole foil of one PC. At the equinox they become the seed of [[The Other Party (Rival or Evil)]].**

> [!danger] DM-only
> Spoiler material. The recruits read to players as ordinary kids in Wesley's orbit; their ritual role and their equinox transformation are hidden until very late.

## The four foils
Each is a real kid with a real wound [[Wesley Crane|Wesley]] reframes as insight, and each mirrors a PC at the opposite pole (build key: [[The Cast and the Permanent Cast]]). All start at **disposition 2 (Neutral)**, each anchored to an Act-1 episode where the PCs meet them. [[Time Points]] build a PC bond; Wesley counter-cultivates after the featured episode, reframing PC kindness as condescension.

- **[[Ricky Vance]]** — foil to **[[Tank]]**. Elite athlete, the cool technical "Iceman" (an Order mask) over a chaotic home life; military-academy upbringing, the dare-night "snitch" grudge. Featured Episode 1 ([[Episode 1 — The Hush]]).
- **[[Zander Pruitt]]** — foil to **[[Bash Whitlock]]**. Small, underestimated, Chaos-coded improviser (Warlock) against Bash's Order-engineering. Episode TBD.
- **[[Ren Sołtys]]** — foil to **[[Old Man]]**. Contemplative, perceptive Wood-Elf Shadow Monk who reads people to *vanish* where Old Man reads them to *manage.* Episode TBD.
- **[[Eddie Park]]** — foil to **[[Gern]]**. A rock-bottom kid "born again" into Wesley's center as his new religion (Paladin); the purest cast/permanent-cast case (his cast genuinely worked, which is why he's the truest believer). Episode TBD (the old Ep4 slot is open).

*(This roster replaces the earlier Marcy / Eddie-as-bookworm / Fourth-Recruit placeholders. [[Marcy Hauer]] is retained as a non-rival community-center NPC — her 1991/eulogy texture still ties the center to the mill grief; the [[The Fourth Recruit]] placeholder is dissolved.)*

## The lieutenant (distinct)
[[Dominique Ferraro]] is **not a recruit** — she's [[Wesley Crane|Wesley]]'s apprentice/heir and the ritual's [[Elias Mercer|Mercer]]-bloodline anchor, established early as the PCs' aboveground "heart." She's handled entirely separately: she's offered a *wheel of her own*, not cured of a broken leg, and her turn is clear-eyed and gradual. See her note and [[The Other Party (Rival or Evil)]]. *(She absorbs the role formerly assigned to the placeholder "Sam.")* The older volunteers Wesley hollows for ritual ingredients are [[Wesley's Adherents]] — a different group entirely.

## The Gathering Hollows (qualifier)
The ritual needs witnesses who have been **hollowed and survived.** Each recruit gets one such event in Act 1 — debut episode or a later [[Wesley Crane|Wesley]] "expedition" — situational, not forced; the hollowed aftermath doubles as Wesley's "proof" that they need him. Exactly one is **manufactured** by Wesley (who, TBD). [[Dominique Ferraro]] is exempt. See [[The Other Party (Rival or Evil)]], [[Hollowing and Agency-Loss]].

## What happens to them
The PCs' summer disposition-building cashes in at the equinox negotiation-as-combat (see [[The Equinox Ritual]], [[The Negotiation System]]). How many recruits reach Interest 4+ sets whether they become a Rival, Evil, or Compromised party — and whether they get **embers like the PCs' or like Wesley's** — see [[Aftermath Branches]] and [[The Other Party (Rival or Evil)]].
