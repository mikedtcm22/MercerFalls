# Hollowed Townspeople

Up: [[Factions & Groups]]

**The walking casualties of agency-loss — people the dungeon "killed" by eating the part of them that decides. They look alive, go through the motions, and are the campaign's most visible, least understood evidence of what Mercer Falls actually is.**

## What they are
When someone is "killed" in the dungeon they don't die there — the dungeon takes their **agency**, the body dissolves and reconstitutes at the entrance, and it walks home on autopilot: glassy-eyed, silent, route-correct, climbing into its own bed. The hollowed wake with no memory and the deciding part gone for a week or more, sometimes permanently. To outsiders this reads as **time-loss**; the mechanism is **agency-loss** (see [[Hollowing and Agency-Loss]]). Without survival instinct, many die later in ordinary "accidents" — how soon depends entirely on how dangerous their daily routine is.

## Named and unnamed
The town's hollowed presence (Town Ref §14):

- **[[Henry Voss]]** — at the Sinclair station counter; the locked, signature hollowed NPC. Fire-vocabulary register: *no warmth, the ember gone out, ash behind the eyes, a stove banked too long.*
- **Pete, Ellen, Carl** — additional hollowed townspeople (lightly sketched, names only).
- **An unnamed hollowed class** — the broader population of agency-loss casualties walking around town; many of Mercer Falls' "missing persons" aren't missing at all. [[The 1991 Mill Incident]]'s official "seven died" is the *aboveground* death toll of a mass hollowing event, not the dungeon casualty count.

> [!danger] DM-only
> [[Wesley's Adherents]] — [[Mike and Doug Tinsmith]] and [[Phyllis Harrow]] — join this group across the summer when Wesley has them hollowed for [[The Three Ritual Items]]. Their reconstitution-and-walk-home is witnessable, and the chamber each was hollowed in carries fresh civilian residue (see [[Forensic Delving]]).

## The walk home
The hollowed reconstitute at the dungeon entrance and **walk home through the mill's south gate** ([[The Mill]]) — a behavioral pattern in [[The Wrongness Layer]]. PCs at the mill on the right night can witness this directly. [[The Reality Filter]] keeps the town from noticing or investigating.

## Why PCs can see it and others can't
The hollowed are walking proof of agency-loss that no one in town can read, because the Filter holds everyone's attention away from it. [[The Party]], carrying [[The Embers]], are among the few who can both perceive and act on what the hollowed represent. The fire-vocabulary contrast — warmth in the bearers, ash in the hollowed — sets up the eventual reveal that it is the *same substance*, present versus depleted.
