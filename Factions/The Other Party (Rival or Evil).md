# The Other Party (Rival or Evil)

Up: [[Factions & Groups]]

> [!note] Filename note
> The canonical title is **The Other Party (Rival / Evil)** but a `/` is illegal in filenames, so this file is named `The Other Party (Rival or Evil).md`. The exact slash-form title is kept in `title:` and `aliases:` so `[[The Other Party (Rival / Evil)]]` links from other notes resolve here.

**A parallel NPC party of dungeon-touched kids that emerges as a counterpart to [[The Party]]. The party exists no matter what the PCs do; what kind of party it is — Rival, Evil, or Compromised — is set by the equinox negotiation.**

> [!danger] DM-only
> Spoiler material. This party is the equinox payoff of [[Wesley's Recruits]] plus possibly [[Sam]]. Players will not know its quality, or that they were determining it all summer, until it materializes.

## The fixed fact and the variable
A parallel ember-bearing party emerges over Act 1, able to survive the dungeon at PC-comparable rates — a real ongoing presence in Acts 2+. **The gate opens and the party emerges regardless.** What the PCs control is *what kind* it is, decided by the equinox negotiation-as-combat (see [[The Equinox Ritual]], [[The Negotiation System]], [[Aftermath Branches]]).

## Quality by negotiation tally
Set by how many of the four [[Wesley's Recruits]] reach Interest 4+:

| Rivals at 4+ | Result | Embers (see [[Order and Chaos]]) | Reachable? |
|---|---|---|---|
| 3+ | **Rival Party** (success) | ~80% Order / 20% Chaos — mirror of PCs | Yes — reachable as future allies |
| 2 | **Compromised toward Rival** | mixed / degraded anti-embers | Partly — some members reachable |
| 1 | **Compromised toward Evil** | mostly anti-embers | Mostly not |
| 0 | **Evil Party** (failure) | ~100% Order anti-embers | No — radicalized, like [[Wesley Crane]] |
| [[Sam]] at 4+ (any time) | Ritual fully prevented | entity may still grant Rival-type embers | Yes |

## Reachability — the throughline
The split is cosmological. The success-path **Rival Party** is mixed-cosmology, retaining enough self to be reachable as future allies (fire-vocabulary: *uneven, guttering, struggling to catch*). The failure-path **Evil Party** has pure-Order anti-embers and is functionally Wesley's peers (*too-bright, fierce, uncontrolled*) — not theoretically reachable, only cooperable if first broken out of their relic by external force, and even then there may not be enough of the original kids left. Compromised parties land in between, recoverable with significant effort.

Neither version is allied with the PCs at the end of Act 1; the fork changes *what kind* of opposing party they face and *whether* alliance is ever possible. Either way they pursue their own agenda into Act 2's race for the thing behind the [[The Mercer Gate]].
