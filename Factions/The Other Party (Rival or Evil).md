# The Other Party (Rival or Evil)

Up: [[Factions & Groups]]

> [!note] Filename note
> The canonical title is **The Other Party (Rival / Evil)** but a `/` is illegal in filenames, so this file is named `The Other Party (Rival or Evil).md`. The exact slash-form title is kept in `title:` and `aliases:` so `[[The Other Party (Rival / Evil)]]` links resolve here.

**A parallel NPC party of dungeon-touched kids that emerges as a counterpart to [[The Party]] — built as the PCs' *inverted mirror*: four recruits who each share a PC's core domain but sit at the opposite pole of it, plus a hidden lieutenant. The party exists no matter what the PCs do; what kind it is — Rival, Evil, or Compromised — is set by the equinox negotiation.**

> [!danger] DM-only
> Spoiler material. This party is the equinox payoff of [[Wesley's Recruits]] and their lieutenant [[Dominique Ferraro]]. Players will not learn its quality — or that they spent all summer determining it, or that Dominique was ever part of it — until it materializes.

## The roster — each a PC's foil
Four recruits, each the opposite-pole mirror of one PC (build key for all of them: [[The Cast and the Permanent Cast]]):
- **[[Ricky Vance]] ↔ [[Tank]]** — both elite athletes hiding a second self; *inverted masks.* Tank wears a chaos mask over a disciplined core; Ricky wears an Order mask (the cool, technical "Iceman," high game-IQ) over a chaotic home life.
- **[[Zander Pruitt]] ↔ [[Bash Whitlock]]** — both small, underestimated kids with hidden power; *Chaos vs. Order.* Bash builds and reads systems and distrusts the unreadable; Zander runs on improvisation, luck, and charm.
- **[[Ren Sołtys]] ↔ [[Old Man]]** — both contemplative and deeply perceptive; *same gift, opposite use.* Old Man reads people to manage and carry them; Ren reads people to vanish and never be handled (Shadow Monk).
- **[[Eddie Park]] ↔ [[Gern]]** — both about conviction and belonging; *opposite directions.* Gern is leaving an inherited faith toward formless rebellion; Eddie hit rock-bottom and is "born again" into Wesley's center as his new religion (Paladin).

And their lieutenant, structurally above the recruits:
- **[[Dominique Ferraro]]** — not a recruit but [[Wesley Crane|Wesley]]'s **apprentice / heir** and the ritual's **bloodline anchor.** Established early as the PCs' aboveground "heart" (their woman-in-the-van); her gradual, clear-eyed turn is the party's most personal betrayal. See her note.

> [!note] Why a mirror party
> At the equinox each PC faces their own inverse. That's the dramatic engine of the negotiation-as-combat, and why the [[Aftermath Branches|outcome]] matters: you aren't fighting strangers, you're fighting the version of yourself that handed over the wheel.

## The fixed fact and the variable
A parallel ember-bearing party emerges over Act 1, able to survive the dungeon at PC-comparable rates — a real ongoing presence in Acts 2+. **The gate opens and the party emerges regardless** — [[Dominique Ferraro]] completes the ritual if [[Wesley Crane]] falls (the continuation-regardless principle), *unless she herself is pulled back* (see below). What the PCs control is *what kind* of party it is, decided by the equinox negotiation-as-combat (see [[The Equinox Ritual]], [[The Negotiation System]], [[Aftermath Branches]]).

## "The Gathering Hollows" — the qualifying ordeal
The ritual requires participants who have been **hollowed and survived** — touched by the dungeon and come back (the title's "Gathering Hollows"). So each recruit needs one such event somewhere in Act 1 — in their debut episode or on a later [[Wesley Crane|Wesley]] "expedition" — but it's *situational*, not forced into every intro (e.g. [[Ricky Vance]] may never enter the dungeon). The hollowed stretch (lost time, a careless aftermath) doubles as a **seduction tool**: proof, in Wesley's framing, that the kid can't run their own life and needs him. **Whoever wasn't hollowed in their debut is a candidate for a later expedition, and exactly one of those is a hollowing Wesley *manufactures*** — engineered "proof" (identity TBD once the episodes shake out). **[[Dominique Ferraro]] is exempt** — the ritual needs her *blood*, not a hollowing; her never having that "lost-a-week" look is itself a quiet tell of her different status. See [[Hollowing and Agency-Loss]].

## What the equinox decides — the embers question
The central question of the equinox encounter is **whether the Rivals receive embers like the PCs' or like [[Wesley Crane|Wesley]]'s.** No one is hollowed *at* the ritual; the hollowing already happened (the qualifier). Set by how many of the four recruits reach Interest 4+:

| Recruits at 4+ | Result | Embers (see [[Order and Chaos]]) | Reachable? |
|---|---|---|---|
| 3+ | **Rival Party** (success) | ~80% **Chaos** / 20% Order — mirror of the PCs | Yes — reachable as future allies |
| 2 | **Compromised toward Rival** | mixed / degraded | Partly — some members reachable |
| 1 | **Compromised toward Evil** | mostly anti-ember | Mostly not |
| 0 | **Evil Party** (failure) | ~100% Order anti-embers (like Wesley) | No — radicalized |
| **[[Dominique Ferraro]] reached (4+)** | **Ritual cannot complete — gate stays sealed** | recruits may still get rival-type embers from the entity | Yes — she's pulled back |

## Reachability — the throughline
The split is cosmological. The success-path **Rival Party** is mixed-cosmology, **chaos-dominant like the PCs**, retaining enough self to be reachable as future allies (fire-vocabulary: *uneven, guttering, struggling to catch*). The failure-path **Evil Party** has pure-Order anti-embers and is functionally [[Wesley Crane|Wesley]]'s peers (*too-bright, fierce, uncontrolled*) — not theoretically reachable, only cooperable if first broken out of their relic by external force, and even then there may not be enough of the original kids left. Compromised parties land in between, recoverable with significant effort.

**[[Dominique Ferraro]] is the exception to the whole table.** She kept full agency (never hollowed, never handed over the wheel — she was offered one of her own), so she isn't won by Time-Points disposition like the recruits; she's reachable *only* if a PC refuses the woman-in-the-van misdirection and **sees her** — confronts the person who chose in and could choose out, not the trusted asset. Reaching her is the single highest-leverage outcome: she's the bloodline anchor *and* the continuation, so pulling her back unseals the gate. She is the rival party's one reachable heart; [[Wesley Crane|Wesley]] himself is not reachable at all. If the ritual succeeds, she's **corrupted, not sacrificed** — she continues life as a corrupted heir.

Neither version is allied with the PCs at the end of Act 1; the fork changes *what kind* of opposing party they face and *whether* alliance is ever possible. Either way they pursue their own agenda into Act 2's race for the thing behind the [[The Mercer Gate]].
