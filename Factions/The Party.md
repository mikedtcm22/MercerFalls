# The Party

Up: [[Factions & Groups]]

**The PCs: dungeon-touched kids who took [[The Embers]] at the moment the entity was ready — the only people in Mercer Falls who can both see what's happening and act on it. Not fated; just the ones who walked in at the right time.** As of Session 0, a **four-kid party that does *not* start as a cohesive friend group** — a chain of asymmetric connections that the mill pulls taut for the first time.

## The roster (locked after Session 0)
- **[[Old Man]]** — Human Bard, 16. The party face; Order he *took on himself* (the family manager), now lurching toward Chaos. [[The Districts|Flats]].
- **[[Bash Whitlock]]** — Rock Gnome Artificer, 13. The brain / research engine; Order as *epistemology* (the readable universe), seduced by data not words. [[The Districts|Flats]].
- **[[Gern]]** — Cleric. The homeschooled rich kid; Order *inherited whole* (a strict religion), trying on new eyes this summer. [[The Districts|the Heights]].
- **[[Tank]]** — Barbarian. The secretly-disciplined jock behind a wild-jock mask; Order *internalized*, carrying his father's broken dream. [[The Districts|Flats]].

**Shared vein:** four kids all carrying or fleeing an *imposed* Order — and three of them ([[Old Man]], [[Tank]], arguably [[Gern]]) are load-bearing kids holding up an adult who should be holding *them*. The party is light on a true free spirit; the world supplies one in NPC form: [[Dominique Ferraro]]. See [[Order and Chaos]].

## How they know each other (the web)
A chain of dyads at different temperatures, not a friend group:
- **[[Gern]] ↔ [[Bash Whitlock]]** — mother-arranged playdates (Bash's mom orchestrated them). Assigned, awkward, not close.
- **[[Bash Whitlock]] ↔ [[Tank]]** — *through [[Dominique Ferraro]]* (Bash's cousin, Tank's crush). Instrumental both ways: Bash recruits Tank as a protector and to **phreak him a free second phone line**; Tank's in the orbit for Dom.
- **[[Tank]] ↔ [[Old Man]]** — fellow Flats kids, same street; Old Man's recent slacker turn put him near the jock hijinx.
- **[[Dominique Ferraro]]** is the hidden hub bending Bash and Tank into the same room. *(Note: the party already knows how to break into systems it shouldn't — Bash's know-how + Tank's nerve/hands on the phone-line splice. A skill signature to pay off all campaign.)*

## Why they were at the mill (four reasons, one night)
The same event means something different to each — characterizing everyone before a die is rolled:
- **[[Bash Whitlock]] — a dare, really ingratiation.** Said yes to bank social capital with Tank.
- **[[Gern]] — a dare, taken gladly at face value.** Being included is the prize; he can't conceive it's a setup.
- **[[Tank]] — a prank.** To impress his crew (proposed by [[Ricky Vance]]); meant to scare Bash & Gern into leaving within an hour. Not cruel — needing to belong.
- **[[Old Man]] — coincidence + quiet mercy.** Brooding in the nearby woods, he volunteered for the "adventuring party," partly people-watching, partly because he clocked the prank and moved to soften it (his Ideal, pre-relic).

> [!danger] DM-only
> The **prank-reveal** is a key early fracture: when Bash & Gern learn the dare was a setup (Tank there to humiliate them; [[Ricky Vance]] the architect), the bonding has to be *earned across it* (~Ep 2–3). [[Old Man]] knew from the start — does the mercy-kid protect Tank with silence, or the little kids with the truth? (His Ideal on trial.) And Ricky returning to witness the chickening-out is what leaves him alone on the grounds for the [[Session 1 — The Dare]] cold-open hunt.

## Not fated — eligible
The PCs are **not uniquely chosen**. The entity has quietly cultivated contact with kids in town touched by the dungeon — near-misses, family lineage (Mercer Falls bloodlines are entangled with the dungeon), other markers. There are **perhaps 6–12 eligible kids**. The PCs are simply the ones who entered the dungeon at the moment the entity was ready to act, and the bullies who locked them in (see [[Ricky Vance]]) could have been the protagonists in another version. Their choices got them here.

The other eligible kids matter structurally: the four who become [[Wesley's Recruits]] are drawn from this same pool, and the parallel [[The Other Party (Rival or Evil)]] emerges from them at the equinox.

## What their embers do
- Absorbed into the bearer, never held in inventory; cannot be removed.
- On dungeon "death" the ember pays the dungeon alternative substance; the PC loses one day instead of being hollowed (see [[Hollowing and Agency-Loss]]).
- Act as **tuning forks** — a small warming/flicker in the chest near the dungeon, near dungeon-stuff, or near other ember-bearers.

> [!danger] DM-only
> PC embers are ~80% Chaos / 20% Order (see [[The Embers]], [[Order and Chaos]]). The 20% Order extracts the lost-day toll and drives a slow, dampened radicalization that mirrors [[Wesley Crane]]'s arc over a 20-level campaign. Their patron, [[The Entity]], is the thing the dungeon was built to contain — the level-20 BBEG. They will not learn this for a long time.
