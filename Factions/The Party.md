# The Party

Up: [[Factions & Groups]]

**The PCs: dungeon-touched kids who took [[The Embers]] at the moment the entity was ready — the only people in Mercer Falls who can both see what's happening and act on it. Not fated; just the ones who walked in at the right time.**

## Who they are
Kids who stumble into the whole thing in the last weeks of summer break, 1996. Through circumstance rather than destiny they become the only townspeople who can perceive the dungeon's work *and* intervene in it — everyone else is held inside [[The Reality Filter]]. They are protected by [[The Embers]], fragments of cosmic substance from [[The Entity]] that lodge inside them, granting their D&D abilities and dampening the dungeon's hollowing-toll (see [[Hollowing and Agency-Loss]]). They experience their patron as a benevolent ally.

## Not fated — eligible
The PCs are **not uniquely chosen**. The entity has quietly cultivated contact with kids in town touched by the dungeon — near-misses, family lineage (Mercer Falls bloodlines are entangled with the dungeon), other markers. There are **perhaps 6–12 eligible kids**. The PCs are simply the ones who entered the dungeon at the moment the entity was ready to act, and the bullies who locked them in (see [[Ricky Vance]]) could have been the protagonists in another version. Their choices got them here.

The other eligible kids matter structurally: the four who become [[Wesley's Recruits]] are drawn from this same pool, and the parallel [[The Other Party (Rival or Evil)]] emerges from them at the equinox.

## What their embers do
- Absorbed into the bearer, never held in inventory; cannot be removed.
- On dungeon "death" the ember pays the dungeon alternative substance; the PC loses one day instead of being hollowed (see [[Hollowing and Agency-Loss]]).
- Act as **tuning forks** — a small warming/flicker in the chest near the dungeon, near dungeon-stuff, or near other ember-bearers.

> [!danger] DM-only
> PC embers are ~80% Chaos / 20% Order (see [[The Embers]], [[Order and Chaos]]). The 20% Order extracts the lost-day toll and drives a slow, dampened radicalization that mirrors [[Wesley Crane]]'s arc over a 20-level campaign. Their patron, [[The Entity]], is the thing the dungeon was built to contain — the level-20 BBEG. They will not learn this for a long time.
