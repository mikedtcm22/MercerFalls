# Mercer Lake

Up: [[Mercer Falls — Setting]]

**The town's daylight social engine — the one place in town where life feels normal and abundant — and the tonal counterweight to [[The Mill]].**

## Description
A glacial lake, roughly a mile north-south and a mile and a half east-west, at the town's high north end. Wooded shores north and west, more developed south and east. It drains south through the outlet that becomes [[The River and the Falls]]; the lake sits ~50 feet above the mill bluff's base, and the falls are the geographic expression of that drop. The lake and the dead mill sit half a mile apart with the river and falls between them.

The shoreline by region:
- **SW corner — the public beach (Mercer Memorial Park).** Trucked-in sand, lifeguard chair, buoyed dive line, Parks & Rec snack shack, a boat house. The bright pole: every bike-range kid, tourists, Camp Mercer field trips. Open Memorial Day to Labor Day.
- **S shore — the Bluff and [[The Mercer Mansion|Mercer mansion]]** (private mansion grounds, not publicly accessible).
- **S shore east of the Bluff — the cold spot.** Runs 8-10° colder than the rest of the lake even in late August. Locals know but don't push on why.
- **NW shore — Camp Mercer** (Scout-style summer camp, ~100 kids per two-week session).
- **N / NE shores — the Heights** (old wealth, wooded frontage, private docks). See [[The Districts]].
- **W shore — Mercer Lake Cabins** (twelve rental cabins in the woods).

## In play
The lake is pure summer-life: swim team, hangouts, fishing, first kisses — the foreground of "what we are protecting." Hold the **dual-pole structure** throughout: the SW beach is abundant and alive; the S-shore cold spot is the latent threat. Same body of water, opposite ends.

> [!danger] DM-only
> The south-shore cold spot is the **third (Lake) dungeon entrance** — submerged, non-native stonework on the lake bottom, exposed at low summer pool, requiring diving and equipment. Dormant until late campaign; bypasses the upper floors and lands a party on a deeper Tier-2 floor. See [[The Dungeon's Surface Footprint]]. The cold spot is a [[The Wrongness Layer|wrongness]] entry; PCs may never find the entrance in a given run, and that's acceptable.
