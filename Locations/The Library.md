# The Library

Up: [[Mercer Falls — Setting]]

**The Mercer Falls public library — the town's memory. A core, daytime investigation node: where the disappearance pattern surfaces, where the Hush can be reverse-engineered without [[Russ]], and where a stern preacher is suspiciously haunting the same records the kids are.**

## Description
A small Carnegie-era public library in town — stacks, a **local-history shelf**, a folklore/legends section, and a back-room **microfilm reader** with the run of the *Mercer Falls Ledger* going back decades. Open daytime hours only (☀ — closed at night). Walk-in; no unlock required. Debuts as a core node in [[Episode 2 — The Hush]].

## In play (Episode 2)
- **The disappearance pattern — key connective tissue.** In the old papers, a small *"[name] missing"* note buried in one issue, then ~a week later a *"[name] found dead — suspected animal attack"* headline — and **none of the articles ever connect the two,** nobody notices the rhythm ([[The Reality Filter|the Filter]], in print). Paired with the Hush's behavior (from Russ or the folklore shelf), *this* is what makes the PCs think **the same thing has [[Ricky Vance|Ricky]]** — missing now, on that same ~week clock. It historically corroborates the ripening window and is a prime convergence driver.
- **The kill, the hard way.** The folklore / local-legends shelf (or a run of old "wild animal" sighting reports) is the backup path to the [[The Hush|Hush]]'s weakness (sound-hunter; split its attention, silence in a confined space) and its ripening window — so a party that skips [[Panels]] can still assemble the solution. (Closes the Nightmare-Engine redundancy gap.)
- **The silence relic (F1).** With the Marv's-Spot photo, the local-geography/history holdings surface the legend of a dungeon relic that (allegedly) *drowns all sound around it.* The useful part is **what it does** — the PCs already assume it's down in the dungeon — which they can intuit is a weapon against a thing that hunts by ear. Points toward the [[The Stone of Quietude|Stone of Quietude]] delve. (Second reader alongside Russ.)
- **Gateway to the Mansion.** Good work here surfaces the Mercer papers / [[Mercer Falls Historical Society|Historical Society]] and **unlocks [[The Mercer Mansion|the Mansion]]** (the big-picture [[Elias Mercer]] / mill-origin lore, and a cordial [[Wesley Crane|Wesley]]).
- **Maybe the graphic novel.** If Russ has sent them for the [[The Hush-Hunting Graphic Novel|Hush-hunting graphic novel]] and the Library is the node they've under-used, it's here — checked out to Russ once, back on the shelf.

> [!danger] DM-only — Hollis in the microfilm
> **[[Reverend Hollis]]** is here, hunched at the microfilm, actually digging **dirt on [[Wesley Crane|Wesley]]** for their rivalry — something he'd never explain to a pack of kids. So his cover is honest ("a man doing some reading"), he **clams up when they get close,** and he keeps **turning up wherever they turn up.** His interests overlap theirs *precisely* because Wesley is the hidden hinge between the mill, the Mansion, and the disappearances — so the players draw the damning line themselves without him feeding it to them. First time a **second party is shown independently circling Wesley** (a later payoff), and the quiet tragedy: the one adult building a real case against the villain gets filed under "suspect." **Play him shifty and petty, not sinister-competent,** or the table promotes him to mastermind. His church ([[Reverend Hollis|the red-herring node]]) unlocks as a follow-up.
