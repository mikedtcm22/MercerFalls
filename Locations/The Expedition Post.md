# The Expedition Post

Up: [[The Mill Sector]]

**Floor 1, Room 11 — [[Elias Mercer]]'s forward expedition post, where the 1880s dig staged its gear, hoisted its finds, and bedded down before pushing deeper. Built as a dynamic battlefield: the party's first fight lands here, and the room is half of it.**

> [!danger] DM-only
> Spoiler room. The second Elias-Mercer expedition installation on Floor 1 (with [[The Cataloguing Office]], room 2). It links to **no other sector** — the Mercer sector's secret entrance is the Down-Stairs (room 10), the Lake sector's is the Buzzsaw Room (room 14). See [[The Mill Sector]].

## What it is
Where [[The Cataloguing Office]] *processed* finds, the Expedition Post is where the dig *worked from*: a staging chamber for assembling gear, raising and lowering finds through a freight shaft, and resting between pushes. One corner is unmistakably Elias's own — a cot, a small writing table, a personal footlocker — the man himself bedded down here a century ago. Like the Office it sits impossibly preserved and quietly restocks (see [[Gear and Items]]). On the Session-1 trail it's the first room past the [[The Octagonal Vestibule|Vestibule]] on Wesley's route to the Buzzsaw, and its deeper door shows where the expedition pushed on from.

## The battle map (built in [[Combatscape Layers]])
Designed so the party's first encounter is a fight *for* and *with* the room, not on a bare grid.

- **Layer 0 — base.** An irregular chamber, ~40×30 ft, with a raised timber **loft / watch-platform** (~10 ft up) along one wall and a **freight shaft** open in the floor near the far end. Doors: in from the Vestibule (3), on toward the Spiked Corridor (12).
- **Layer 1 — movement.** The floor is a working yard cluttered with **crates, barrels, and a mine-cart on a short rail**, breaking it into winding lanes — no straight charge. A burst crate has spilled **loose bearings** across one lane (difficult terrain / slip). The **planking around the freight shaft is rotten** — weight there risks giving way (a drop into the shaft or to a lower ledge).
- **Layer 2 — position.** *High ground:* the loft (ranged advantage), reached by a ladder and a crate-ramp — both contestable chokepoints. *Cover:* crate-stacks and barrels as fighting positions (half / three-quarters). *Perch:* the loft rail overlooks the whole floor, worth holding.
- **Layer 3 — interaction.** Marquee: a **heavy crate suspended from the hand-winch** over the yard — cut the rope or kick the winch-brake and it drops (big damage / prone / pin) on whatever's beneath, telegraphed by its shadow and creak. Plus **oil barrels** to topple (spread → difficult terrain) or ignite (fire → a burst); the **mine-cart** to unbrake and shove down its rail (damage + push) or use as moving cover; and the **rotten shaft-planking** to lure a foe onto.

## Session 1 — the first fight ("wandering" monster)
Play the first encounter here, staged as a **wandering monster** (really planned) to teach the party that the floor repopulates and things roam even where Wesley cleared: a **dogman** (Floor-1 pack theme — reskinned Gnoll, CR 1/2; see [[The Nightmare Engine]]) has wandered into the post since the pre-clear, gnawing the century-old supplies or bedded down in Elias's cot. Add a scrawnier feral beast for a larger party. Tuned gentle for level 1 — and the map does the rest: the point of fight #1 is to hand the party an obvious "use the room" beat (drop the crate, light the oil, shove the cart, take the loft) so they learn to read every room for its Layer-3 options. Pays off the two dead dogmen in the Vestibule — *that's* what those were, and here's a live one.

## Loot (investigate to find)
- **Elias's footlocker** (his nook; locked) — the marquee find: a takeable item plus lore. Candidates: a page of Elias's *personal* journal (a Mercer-thread seed distinct from the Office ledgers), an expedition tool with minor utility, or an early glyph-language / keystone hint. One-time.
- **The crates and cart** — searchable expedition supplies: a never-dry lamp, rope, a curio [[Earl's Pawn Shop|Earl]] will buy. Restocks low-value each delve; loot leaves the dungeon.

## Steady-state
The dynamic map stays; the dogman respawns (static Floor-1). On repeat delves this is a favored spot for wandering-monster fights precisely because it rewards knowing it — the party who remembers where the winch-rope and oil barrels are wins faster.
