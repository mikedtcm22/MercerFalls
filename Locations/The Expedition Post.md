# The Expedition Post

Up: [[The Mill Sector]]

**Floor 1, Room 11 — [[Elias Mercer]]'s forward expedition post, where the 1880s dig staged its gear, hoisted its finds, and bedded down before pushing deeper. Built as a dynamic battlefield: the floor's first guaranteed wandering-monster fight lands here, and the room is half of it.**

> [!danger] DM-only
> Spoiler room. The second Elias-Mercer expedition installation on Floor 1 (with [[The Cataloguing Office]], room 2). It links to **no other sector** — the Mercer sector's secret entrance is the Down-Stairs (room 10), the Lake sector's is the Buzzsaw Room (room 14). See [[The Mill Sector]].

## What it is
Where [[The Cataloguing Office]] *processed* finds, the Expedition Post is where the dig *worked from*: a staging chamber for assembling gear, raising and lowering finds through a freight shaft, and resting between pushes. One corner is unmistakably Elias's own — a cot, a writing table, a personal footlocker — the man himself bedded down here a century ago. Like the Office it sits impossibly preserved and quietly restocks (see [[Gear and Items]]). On the Session-1 trail it's the first room past the [[The Octagonal Vestibule|Vestibule]] on Wesley's route to the Buzzsaw, and its deeper door shows where the expedition pushed on from.

## The battle map (built in [[Combatscape Layers]])
Designed so the party's first encounter is a fight *for* and *with* the room, not on a bare grid.

- **Layer 0 — base.** An irregular chamber, ~40×30 ft, with a raised timber **loft / watch-platform** (~10 ft up) along one wall and an open **freight shaft** near the far end. Doors: in from the Vestibule (3), on toward the Spiked Corridor (12).
- **Layer 1 — movement.** The floor is a working yard cluttered with **crates and lashed barrels**, breaking it into winding lanes — no straight charge. A burst crate has spilled **loose bearings** across one lane (difficult terrain / slip). The **planking around the freight shaft is rotten** — weight there risks giving way (a drop into the shaft or to a lower ledge).
- **Layer 2 — position.** *High ground:* the loft (ranged advantage), reached by a ladder and a crate-ramp — both contestable chokepoints. *Cover:* crate-stacks and barrels as fighting positions (half / three-quarters). *Perch:* the loft rail overlooks the whole floor, worth holding.
- **Layer 3 — interaction.** Marquee: a **heavy freight crate suspended from the hand-winch directly over the open shaft** (a load the dig was hoisting up and never finished). In a fight, release the rope / kick the brake and it plunges down the shaft — crushing anything in the shaft or dragging a shaft-edge foe down with it — **but the crate and its contents are gone.** That's the tension: it's both the best combat drop *and* the room's best discovery (see below). Plus **oil barrels** to topple (spread → difficult terrain) or ignite (fire → a burst), and the **rotten shaft-planking** to lure a foe onto.

## Session 1 — the first fight (a real wandering roll)
This room is where the floor's **first wandering-monster encounter is a certainty** — not a planted enemy, but a guaranteed *roll*: when the party works the room, trigger the check and roll the Floor-1 (pack-floor) table (see [[The Nightmare Engine]]). Whatever comes wandered into the cleared post since Wesley passed. Played this way, the table learns the wandering mechanic as a genuine roll, and learns the pack floor repopulates. The map is built to make almost any pack-floor result a fun fight for level 1 — high ground, cover, oil, and the shaft-crate all let a clever party punch up — and it pays off the two dead dogmen in the Vestibule (*that's* what those were). Note the tension bites hardest here: dropping the shaft-crate on whatever they rolled forfeits the discovery inside.

## Finds (three, escalating effort)
- **The suspended crate — a discovery, if you can land it.** Getting it down *without* dropping it into the shaft is a small challenge (haul/rig it to solid ground — STR plus a DEX/tools finesse, or a clever solution; failure, or using it as a combat drop, sends it down the hole). Inside, under tagged oilcloth: a **failed spectator** — a botched attempt to conjure a guardian. It reads *wrong* to anyone who knows a beholder/spectator: a slack, half-deflated grey sphere; a dead, filmed central eye; only **three** crown-stalks, grafted and sutured on with waxed thread, one snapped at the root; nothing about it born, everything assembled and abandoned; a brass tag wired to a stalk in the ledger-hand — a date, a number, **FAILED** underlined twice. Mechanically inert — a discovery, not a haul-item. What it seeds: Elias was *making* things, not just cataloguing them (the Wesley parallel); there is something deeper he meant to guard; and a *working* guardian — or the thing it guards — may wait below (a plantable future Guardian-type encounter; a real Spectator is CR 3). See the desk page, which explains it.
  > *Read-aloud:* Under tagged oilcloth, a thing the size of a large melon: a slack grey sphere, half-deflated, like a bladder someone let the air out of. Where a face should be, there are eyes — too many and too few at once. One central eye, filmed over, dead white. And stalks from the crown — but only three, and they're wrong: not grown, grafted, sutured on with waxed thread, one snapped off at the root. The lids don't match. Nothing about it looks born. It looks assembled, and then given up on. A brass tag is wired to one stalk, in the same neat hand as the ledgers: a date, a number, and one word underlined twice — FAILED.
- **The writing table — the lore beat (a handout).** A page in Elias's own hand, lying open. Free to read; no check. Seeds the guardian experiment, the "something below to guard," and the first faint rhyme between [[Elias Mercer|Elias]] and [[Wesley Crane|Wesley]] (and the hollowing — "the fear is issued and taken back"). The page and the crate explain each other ("the fourth would not take" = the snapped/missing fourth stalk; "the eyes do not consent to the ones I have given them" = the grafting).
  > **Third Descent — Day 41.** The lower gallery yields as before. Two more specimens crated and sent up on the hoist — one of them the warden, or what I had hoped would be a warden. It will not wake. The eyes I recovered below do not consent to the ones I have given them, and the fourth would not take at all; Halloran marks the crate FAILED in the book and will not meet my eye as he does. No matter. There is a thing beneath the gallery that careless men must never reach, and a sentinel that neither sleeps nor tires nor asks what it guards is the only sure answer. I will manage it — the next attempt, or the one after.
  >
  > I confess that the deeper one goes, the less the fear seems to belong to oneself. It is as though it were *issued* — handed out at a certain depth like a lamp, and taken back when you climb. The same, I think, is true of the will. I find I climb less.
  >
  > Halloran asked what I intend to do when the work is complete. I could not answer. The question assumes an *after*, and I am no longer certain the work has one — nor that I wish it to.
  >
  > We descend again tomorrow. I have told the men it will be the last. This is untrue, but it comforts them, and I find their comfort costs me nothing now.
  >
  > — E.M.
- **The footlocker — the loot.** Locked (a pick / pry beat); Elias's personal effects, all takeable and useful:
  - an **ornate silver hand-mirror** — corner-checking, signal-flashing with reflected light, catching a gaze you'd rather not meet head-on; and sells as silver;
  - a **pair of signal whistles** — coordination across distance, and a two-edged toy on a floor where the pack hunts by sound (and the [[The Hush|Hush]] by nothing else) — a whistle can pull a monster off an ally or bring the wrong thing running;
  - a **box of ball bearings** — instant difficult terrain / knock-'em-down; also the in-world source of the ones already spilled in the room;
  - a **jeweled stickpin** — small, unmistakably precious, the clean "fence this at [[Earl's Pawn Shop|Earl's]]" valuable (money-loop fuel).

All footlocker loot leaves the dungeon and persists ([[Gear and Items]]); the footlocker contents are one-time, the shelved supplies restock. The crate specimen is a one-time discovery.

## Steady-state
The dynamic map stays; wandering rolls keep landing fights here, and the room rewards knowing it — the party who remembers the winch-rope, the oil, and the rotten planking wins faster.
