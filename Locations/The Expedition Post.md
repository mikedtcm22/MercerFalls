# The Expedition Post

Up: [[The Mill Sector]]

**Floor 1, Room 11 — [[Elias Mercer]]'s forward expedition post, where the 1880s dig staged its gear, hoisted its finds, and bedded down before pushing deeper. Built as a dynamic battlefield: the floor's first guaranteed wandering-monster fight lands here, and the room is half of it.**

> [!danger] DM-only
> Spoiler room. The second Elias-Mercer expedition installation on Floor 1 (with [[The Cataloguing Office]], room 2). It links to **no other sector** — the Mercer sector's secret entrance is the Down-Stairs (room 10), the Lake sector's is the Buzzsaw Room (room 14). See [[The Mill Sector]].

## What it is
Where [[The Cataloguing Office]] *processed* finds, the Expedition Post is where the dig *worked from*: a staging chamber for assembling gear, raising and lowering finds through a freight shaft, and resting between pushes. One corner is unmistakably Elias's own — a cot, a writing table, a personal footlocker — the man himself bedded down here a century ago. Like the Office it sits impossibly preserved and quietly restocks (see [[Gear and Items]]). On the Session-1 trail it's the first room past the [[The Octagonal Vestibule|Vestibule]] on Wesley's route to the Buzzsaw, and its deeper door shows where the expedition pushed on from.

## The battle map (built in [[Combatscape Layers]])
Designed so the party's first encounter is a fight *for* and *with* the room, not on a bare grid.

- **Layer 0 — base.** An irregular chamber, ~40×30 ft, with a raised timber **loft / watch-platform** (~10 ft up) along one wall and an open **freight shaft** near the far end. Doors: in from the Vestibule (3), on toward the Spiked Corridor (12).
- **Layer 1 — movement.** The floor is a working yard cluttered with **crates and lashed barrels**, breaking it into winding lanes — no straight charge. A burst crate has spilled **loose bearings** across one lane (difficult terrain / slip). The **planking around the freight shaft is rotten** — weight there risks giving way (a drop into the shaft or to a lower ledge).
- **Layer 2 — position.** *High ground:* the loft (ranged advantage), reached by a ladder and a crate-ramp — both contestable chokepoints. *Cover:* crate-stacks and barrels as fighting positions (half / three-quarters). *Perch:* the loft rail overlooks the whole floor, worth holding.
- **Layer 3 — interaction.** Marquee: a **heavy freight crate suspended from the hand-winch directly over the open shaft** (a load the dig was hoisting up and never finished). In a fight, release the rope / kick the brake and it plunges down the shaft — crushing anything in the shaft or dragging a shaft-edge foe down with it — **but the crate and its contents are gone.** That's the tension: it's both the best combat drop *and* the room's best loot (see below). Plus **oil barrels** to topple (spread → difficult terrain) or ignite (fire → a burst), and the **rotten shaft-planking** to lure a foe onto.

## Session 1 — the first fight (a real wandering roll)
This room is where the floor's **first wandering-monster encounter is a certainty** — not a planted enemy, but a guaranteed *roll*: when the party works the room, trigger the check and roll the Floor-1 (pack-floor) table (see [[The Nightmare Engine]]). Whatever comes wandered into the cleared post since Wesley passed. Played this way, the table learns the wandering mechanic as a genuine roll, and learns the pack floor repopulates. The map is built to make almost any pack-floor result a fun fight for level 1 — high ground, cover, oil, and the shaft-crate all let a clever party punch up — and it pays off the two dead dogmen in the Vestibule (*that's* what those were). Note the loot tension bites hardest here: the party may be tempted to drop the shaft-crate on whatever they rolled, forfeiting what's inside.

## Loot & lore (three finds, escalating effort)
- **The suspended crate — the prize, if you can land it.** Getting it down *without* dropping it into the shaft is a small challenge (haul/rig it to solid ground — STR plus a DEX/tools finesse, or a clever solution; failure, or using it as a combat drop, sends it down the hole). Inside: a **glyph-carved stone slab the expedition hauled up from a deeper floor** — lore (the dig was already studying the glyph-language a century ago) and a real, portable head-start: a **reference that gives an edge on later glyph-locks** (notably the Map-Keystone's, room 6) — a leg up, not a skeleton key. A "weird artifact that leaves the dungeon" per [[Gear and Items]] (the carvings faintly glow topside).
- **The writing table — the lore beat.** A page in Elias's own hand, lying open: it begins as methodical expedition notes and drifts, mid-page, into something about "the deeper structure" / the Gate / *the Work* — the tone (and the handwriting) curdling from scientist into something colder. DM-private: the first whisper of the [[Elias Mercer|Mercer]] → [[Wesley Crane|Wesley]] parallel and the hollowing, complementing the Office's operational ledgers with the *personal* layer. Free to read; no check needed.
- **The footlocker — the loot.** Locked (a pick / pry beat). Elias's personal effects: **period valuables [[Earl's Pawn Shop|Earl]] will buy** (a watch, cufflinks, a little coin — money-loop fuel) plus a useful mundane item (a good oilskin coat / a quality tool). *Optional:* if you want to pull the campaign's first minor magic item forward from Floor 2, Elias's **pocket watch that warms near danger** (a [[Gear and Items]] example item) is the natural pick to live here — your call on the gating.

All loot leaves the dungeon and persists ([[Gear and Items]]); the crate slab and footlocker contents are one-time, the shelved supplies restock.

## Steady-state
The dynamic map stays; wandering rolls keep landing fights here, and the room rewards knowing it — the party who remembers the winch-rope, the oil, and the rotten planking wins faster.
