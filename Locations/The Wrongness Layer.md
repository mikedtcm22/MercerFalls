# The Wrongness Layer

Up: [[Mercer Falls — Setting]]

**The ambient, statistical, accumulated wrongness sitting on top of normal small-town life — five layers in increasing perceptual intensity. The town reads real first, wrong second.**

The campaign's tonal engine is the *cumulative* effect of small things, not the punch of any one. Most entries are surface-perceptible (mixed); their *causes* are DM-only. Treat §14-§16 as growing pools, not a fixed list.

## 1. Ambient phenomena
- **The rumble.** A low, constant, sub-audible drone across town, loudest at night and in quiet rooms. Townspeople have heard it forever and call it the river, the highway, trains (none run anymore), or don't notice. **Source is deliberately indeterminate** — the campaign owes no answer. PCs paying attention realize around session 5 they've been hearing it all along.
- **Dead radio pockets.** The biggest in a quarter-mile ring around the mill bluff; smaller along Pine Street near [[Henry Voss|Voss's]] house, on the lake's south shore, and on Highway 21 east of the strip.
- **Other ambient.** Cheap wristwatches die faster and compasses drift west 2-3° in the mill-bluff ring; the lake's south-shore cold spot; the river running cold at [[The Fishing Dock]]; an AM frequency that pulses every few seconds inside a half-mile mill ring.

## 2. Behavioral patterns
The trail-off (people who can't follow up), the hollowed loop, the institutional version (the paper that won't touch the statistics), and the cumulative version — the town's *stuckness*. See [[Hollowing and Agency-Loss]].

## 3. Hollowed presence
[[Henry Voss]] and other named/unnamed hollowed townspeople — walking proof of agency-loss. Their reconstituted walks home come through the mill's south gate. See [[Hollowed Townspeople]].

## 4. Material leakage — dungeon-stone
Dark, slightly translucent stone with a faint mineral sheen — the Episode 3 "click" clue (see [[The Pattern Recognition Arc]], [[Episode 3 — The Click]]). Pre-seeded surface sites:
- The **keystone of [[The Mill|the mill's]] south gate** (anyone walking past has seen it).
- A chunk in [[The Mercer Mansion|the mansion's]] basement foundation (visible from the east-wall window).
- The cemetery's iron arch and several older headstone bases.
- The 1950s interpretive sign base at [[The River and the Falls|the falls overlook]].

Also: the stranger-objects pool, residue trails, and the [[The Mercer Mirror]] at [[Panels]].

## 5. Localized dead zones
The "thing in the woods east of town," the cemetery's old section, the closed school wing, the boarded-up house at 142 Maple, the Highway 21 east stretch.

## Inventories (renewable pools)
- **§15 Filter-Noticed Inventory** — things that have *always been there*: the south-gate keystone, the cemetery arch keystone, Voss's five-sentence rotation, a different-smelling booth at [[Marco's Diner|Marco's]], a cold pocket in the library genealogy stacks, 142 Maple, the sealed mansion-basement door (findable once a PC hears rumor of it).
- **§16 Hidden-in-Plain-Sight** — the falls as innocuous campaign feature, the omission-laden overlook sign, and the river entering/exiting the mill but invisible inside it.

> [!danger] DM-only
> Every entry traces to [[The Dungeon]] and [[The Reality Filter]]: the cold spot is the Lake entrance, the dungeon-stone is material leakage from the dungeon proper, the rumble and dead zones cluster in [[The Dungeon's Surface Footprint]]. The Filter ensures nobody investigates. PCs with embers ([[The Embers]]) are the exception — they notice.
