# Earl's Pawn Shop

Up: [[Mercer Falls — Setting]]

**A pawn shop in [[The Districts|The Flats]] that asks no questions — and the town's accidental witness, whose private ledger quietly counts the hollowed.**

## Description
A working-class pawn shop in the Flats, run by **[[Earl]]**, introduced in [[Episode 2 — The Hungry House]]. Earl buys dungeon antiques without provenance and occasionally sells weird items, making him a steady-state gear/cash channel for the PCs (bible §12). His real value is investigative: dozens of locals pawn things on a slow drip to keep up with bills, and Earl has noticed the pattern.

## In play
The natural introduction is the Episode 2 [[Henry Voss|Voss]] thread — Voss has been pawning things for years to make mortgage payments, and chasing that leads PCs to Earl. In conversation, **Earl can hint that he knows accurate counts of hollowed townspeople**: they all pawn things eventually because they don't notice they're running through their savings. He doesn't use the word "hollowed" — he's just seen the pattern, and he's been keeping a **private ledger**. PCs who invest Time Points in Earl can gradually surface that ledger as an investigative resource.

His specific value as an NPC: **he sees the town accurately because he's its accidental witness.** He doesn't have the [[The Reality Filter|Filter]]-permission to *act* on what he sees, but he catalogues.

> [!danger] DM-only
> Earl's ledger is a forensic data set — a hollowed-count of [[Hollowed Townspeople]] surfacing as a spending-pattern signature, without Earl (or the town) understanding what it means. It corroborates the [[The Wrongness Layer|wrongness]] and feeds [[Forensic Delving]] / [[The Pattern Recognition Arc]]. His characterization (mannerisms, the trigger point for the count reveal) is still loose (bible §20 item 21); the ledger mechanic is locked.
