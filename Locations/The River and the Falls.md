# The River and the Falls

Up: [[Mercer Falls — Setting]]

**A real, visible 50-foot cascade that gives the town its name and its defining image — water that emerges from beneath [[The Mill]] and falls.**

## Description
The river runs north-to-south, originating in [[Mercer Lake|the lake's]] south outlet (a short audible near-rapid) and exiting town to the southeast. South of the lake it runs about a quarter mile through wooded floodplain — walkable paths on both banks, casually fishable, unremarkable. Then the land rises sharply into the **mill bluff**, a single bedrock outcrop ~50 feet high. The mill sits atop the bluff and **the river runs enclosed through the mill**, entering at the north end and exiting at the south, where it drops over the bluff's south edge as **the falls** — roughly 80 feet across, the main flow concentrated in the central third, picturesque but intimate. From outside the mill, the water visibly emerges from *beneath the structure* and falls.

**The falls overlook** is a small public park on the west side of the falls, off the road connecting downtown to the lake: a wooden bench, a 1950s interpretive sign about [[Elias Mercer]] and the founding, a worn footpath descending toward the base (railed off at the steep section), and a footbridge. The overlook view is the campaign's defining image of Mercer Falls — the cascade in the foreground, the dead mill looming directly above and behind, the blast-furnace stack rising over the cascade's top edge.

South of the falls the river runs along the east side of downtown and the west side of the Flats (paralleled by Pine Street), under three bridges (Main Street concrete, Pine Street steel truss, and the overlook footbridge), past [[The Fishing Dock]] ~two miles southeast, and out of town.

## In play
Mercer Falls is *named* for the falls, and they're a genuine tourist draw: postcards (mill prominent in frame) at Hadley's, the Lakeshore Inn's framed 1920s photo, the Historical Society's geology section. This is the chamber-of-commerce voice — "the lake town with the falls." The falls themselves are **not wrong** — a real, lovely natural feature. What's wrong is the structure standing directly above them, consuming the river before letting it fall.

> [!danger] DM-only
> The falls sit at the *southern edge* of [[The Dungeon's Surface Footprint]] but read as completely normal — evidence the dungeon is a discrete circle whose surface effects attenuate at its edges. Forensic-delving payoffs: the interpretive sign's stone base includes **dungeon-stone fragments** (placed by the same 1880s hands that built the cemetery work) and the sign quietly omits things the inner-archive Mercer papers contain; and PCs who realize the river is *enclosed* through the mill's full length are one inference from realizing what sits beneath the river-channel floor (see [[The Wrongness Layer]]).
