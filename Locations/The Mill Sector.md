# The Mill Sector

Up: [[Mercer Falls — Setting]]

**The Act-1 dungeon: floors 1-5 of [[The Dungeon]] reached through [[The Mill]]'s lower works — the campaign's heaviest dungeon-design investment, dozens of sessions of grinding, glyph-decoding, and cat-and-mouse with [[Wesley Crane]].**

> [!danger] DM-only
> This entire note is a spoiler. The Mill sector is the only entrance the PCs can use through Act 1 (via trespass into the sealed mill grounds).

## Where it sits
One of three fragmented-arc sectors of the dungeon's upper floors (see [[The Dungeon's Surface Footprint]]). The Mill, Mercer, and Lake sectors' floors 1-5 **do not connect**; the system unifies only at floor 6. PCs entering from the mill descend through industrial space, then the bluff's bedrock, then dungeon proper. **Floors 1-5 are heavy content by design** — this is where the Act-1 investment goes. (By contrast the **Mercer sector** is a low-detail transit corridor used in Act 2, and the **Lake entrance** drops straight to a deep Tier-2 floor — both noted here only for orientation; see [[The Dungeon's Surface Footprint]].)

## Design lens for the build-out
Every room is built to the **[[Dungeon Mechanics|Stretch-Worth Principle]]**: it offers at least one thing worth spending a Stretch on (loot, inspect/forensic-read, disarm, decode, fight, trigger) — an *opportunity, not an obligation*. Combat-likely rooms additionally get the **[[Combatscape Layers]]** treatment (elevation, cover, chokepoints, interactables) so a wandering monster turns the room into part of the fight. Monsters follow the **[[The Nightmare Engine|reskin pattern]]** (D&D stat blocks, Mercer Falls flavor) and each floor carries a **lair theme** — Floor 1 is **"the pack floor"** (Dogmen/gnolls organized core, feral beasts around, all beneath [[The Hush]]). No pure pass-through rooms.

## Floor 1 — the sixteen-room map (canonical layout)
**Sixteen numbered rooms in a donut footprint** around a central dark void — the unmapped Mercer/Lake sectors' "bite" out of the ring (see [[The Dungeon's Surface Footprint]]). Entry is at the east; the ring loops up and around to the Floor-2 stairs at the northwest. The **[[The Ember Chamber|Awakening Room]]** (map-marked **S**) sits adjacent to the ring, not in it — the one-time pocket for the Session-1 awakening only.

Connective structure: the **[[The Keystone Tablet|two keystones]]** sit at opposite ends of the loop (Map-Keystone in the Niche, room 6; Compass-Keystone in a hidden enclave, room 16). A **one-way painted door** runs from the Map-Keystone Niche (6) to the Bell Junction (7). The wrapping **Stutter Stairs** (15) tie the Buzzsaw/Wesley-tangent side back to the Counting Room (5) via a secret door, with a **hidden landing** dropping to the Compass enclave (16). The other two sectors link into Floor 1 through **secret entrances that stay dormant until Act 2**: the **[[The Mercer Gate|Mercer sector]]** at the **Down-Stairs (10)**, and the **Lake sector** at the **Buzzsaw Room (14)**.

**The rooms** *(the Session-1 delve path — 2, 3, 11, 12, 13, 14 — is fully designed; the rest carry hooks awaiting build):*
1. **Dungeon Entrance** — the boiler-room hatch down from [[The Mill]]'s lower works. In Session 1 the way onward reads **caved-in** until the PCs engage the Awakening Room. *(Interaction hook TBD beyond the entrance itself.)*
2. **[[The Cataloguing Office]]** — long hidden office where [[Elias Mercer]]'s 1880s operation catalogued finds; century-old yet mid-shift. The players' first room; loot (real, leaves the dungeon), forensic reads, the Mercer-papers seed. *(Fully designed.)*
3. **[[The Octagonal Vestibule]]** — the hub; a door on each of eight walls. Open a real exit and the other doors collapse to blank wall, locking the hub to that spoke until reset. Three real paths, two fake-door maw traps, two closets (loot / sleeping dogmen), one entrance. S1: pre-collapsed to Wesley's door, with two lootable dead dogmen on the floor. *(Fully designed.)*
4. **Switchbacks** — a series of switch-back pathways between the Vestibule and the Counting Room. *(Interaction hook TBD.)*
5. **Counting Room** — secret door up to the Stutter Stairs (15). *(Identity/hook TBD — the name wants a tally/counting motif.)*
6. **Map-Keystone Niche** — holds the **Map-Keystone** behind a **glyph-lock** (puzzle TBD). A **one-way painted door** leads out to the Bell Junction (7).
7. **Bell Junction** — a **bell suspended overhead**; striking it (a projectile or some clever reach) **changes the room** — a reward, not a gate. Doors: the one-way in from 6; a route toward the Floor-2 path (→ 8); and a **portal-trap door** that dumps whoever crosses it back into the Vestibule (3), facing a random one of its eight now-shut doors.
8. **Spiral Stair Room** — a spiral stair up to a **sealed Mill-grounds exit**; unsealing it (means TBD) would open a new, unexplored area of the mill grounds above.
9. **The Pit Room** — Floor 1's **exit-guardian** room (Guardian-type). A central pit: the **wide** crossing is easy but has **2–3 sleeping wolves** (pack theme) in the path; the **narrow ledge** is monster-free but risks a fall. A **hard monster** sits in the pit, unable to climb out on its own — the PCs have a means it can't use.
10. **Down-Stairs** — stairs down to **Floor 2**. *(Also the dormant secret entrance the **[[The Mercer Gate|Mercer sector]]** links into in Act 2.)*
11. **[[The Expedition Post]]** — [[Elias Mercer]]'s forward expedition outpost (a second Mercer installation); a **dynamic [[Combatscape Layers|Combatscape]] battle map** hosting the floor's guaranteed first wandering fight, plus finds: a failed-spectator specimen in the shaft-crate, Elias's "Third Descent" desk page, footlocker loot. Links to no other sector. *(Fully designed.)*
12. **[[The Spiked Corridor]]** — a **traveling-wave** spike gauntlet (looks random; a fixed **diagonal** of dead tiles is the safe read). Four ways across — run it, walk the diagonal, pull the disguised torch-release, or lasso-swing the ceiling fixture. S1: traps un-sprung, one blood-flecked spike from Wendell Pace as the telegraph. *(Fully designed.)*
13. **[[The Pillared Reach]]** — the largest room on the floor; the young [[The Hush]]'s nest. The party arrives on a fight in progress (young Hush killing Wendell Pace) with a rush / watch / slip choice; a **sound-as-terrain** fight; the first witnessed hollowing; the spore-mark → Session 2 hook. *(Fully designed.)*
14. **[[The Buzzsaw Room]]** — the **Session-1 climax** ([[Wesley Crane|Wesley]]'s first real sigil/portal). Physically the **Millrace**: a fast-water chasm crossed by single-file catwalks — an anti-surround stage for the fight vs. a modified Pack Lord + wolf. Permanent identity: a deadly-terrain room plus a **major magic weapon/armor in the far corner** as the come-back reward. Also the dormant **Lake-sector** entrance. *(Fully designed.)*
15. **Stutter Stairs** — the long **wrapping stair** around the bite; connects the Buzzsaw side to the Counting Room (5, secret door), with a **hidden landing** down to the Compass enclave (16).
16. **Compass-Keystone Enclave** — hidden; holds the **Compass-Keystone**, reached via the Stutter Stairs' hidden landing; guarded by a **deep guardian** (TBD).
- **S — [[The Ember Chamber|Awakening Room]]** — the one-time, entity-made pocket adjacent to the ring; the Session-1 awakening only, then it closes forever.

See [[The Keystone Tablet]] for what the two Floor-1 keystones do; [[The Nightmare Engine]] for the Floor-1 "pack floor" bestiary theme.

## Floor 1 in Session 1
The floor is **partially pre-cleared by Wesley** on a recent solo delve; the party follows his footprints, spent torches, drag marks, a cloth scrap (seen again on Wesley), stacked corpses, tally marks, and chalk-struck glyphs — their first encounter with the glyph-language. On the map his trail runs from the Entrance (1) through the Cataloguing Office (2) and Octagonal Vestibule (3), out through the Expedition Post (11), the Spiked Corridor (12), and the Pillared Reach (13), ending at the **Buzzsaw Room (14)** — where Wesley is finalizing **his first real sigil** ([[Wesley's Sigils]]); he says one line (*"You poor things"*), triggers the sigil, and vanishes. The engineered wave that follows is tuned for a likely TPK with a narrow sigil-escape window. See [[Session 1 — The Dare]].

The Spiked Corridor's traps stay **un-sprung** on the S1 delve (Wesley threaded them; the lone blood-flecked sprung spike is the janitor's) — a telegraphed warning in S1, a real obstacle only on later delves. The party's first *combat* is a real **wandering roll** in the Expedition Post (11); their first *witnessed hollowing* is Wendell Pace in the Pillared Reach (13) — or, if a fast, hot-rolling party pulls him off the young Hush alive, moments later in the Buzzsaw Room (14).

## Floors 2-5 and steady-state mechanics
Floors 2-5 await detailed design (built one at a time as each comes online). Steady-state: daily intrinsic **floor reset** (chalk and glow persist; footprints and blood are gone), Stretch-based resource attrition, the glyph-language as a persistent knowledge resource, and the **sigil cat-and-mouse** with Wesley (real vs. decoy sigils). Forensic delving — residue-reading, sigil-reading, tracking Wesley's status via the gradient-shuffle — is the signature mechanic here. See [[Dungeon Mechanics]], [[Floor Reset and the Intelligence Gradient]], [[Forensic Delving]].

The Act-1 climax is partial access to **floor 6**, repulsed before significant exploration. After [[The Mercer Gate]] opens at the equinox, the Mill sector's floors 1-5 stop being campaign-active (explored, mapped, drained).
