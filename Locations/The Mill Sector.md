# The Mill Sector

Up: [[Mercer Falls — Setting]]

**The Act-1 dungeon: floors 1-5 of [[The Dungeon]] reached through [[The Mill]]'s lower works — the campaign's heaviest dungeon-design investment, dozens of sessions of grinding, glyph-decoding, and cat-and-mouse with [[Wesley Crane]].**

> [!danger] DM-only
> This entire note is a spoiler. The Mill sector is the only entrance the PCs can use through Act 1 (via trespass into the sealed mill grounds).

## Where it sits
One of three fragmented-arc sectors of the dungeon's upper floors (see [[The Dungeon's Surface Footprint]]). The Mill, Mercer, and Lake sectors' floors 1-5 **do not connect**; the system unifies only at floor 6. PCs entering from the mill descend through industrial space, then the bluff's bedrock, then dungeon proper. **Floors 1-5 are heavy content by design** — this is where the Act-1 investment goes. (By contrast the **Mercer sector** is a low-detail transit corridor used in Act 2, and the **Lake entrance** drops straight to a deep Tier-2 floor — both noted here only for orientation; see [[The Dungeon's Surface Footprint]].)

## Design principle — every room earns an action
From Floor 1 forward, **every room in the dungeon must give the PCs a concrete reason to spend a dungeon turn there** — something to loot, inspect, disarm, decode, or fight. These can mix and stack (a trap that guards loot; a puzzle that pays out in lore). No pure pass-through rooms: a space that is only a corridor between two encounters is a design gap to fill, not to ship. This is the working lens for the room-by-room build-out.

## Floor 1 — the sixteen-room map (canonical layout)
**Sixteen numbered rooms in a donut footprint** around a central dark void — the unmapped Mercer/Lake sectors' "bite" out of the ring (see [[The Dungeon's Surface Footprint]]). Entry is at the east; the ring loops up and around to the Floor-2 stairs at the northwest. The **[[The Ember Chamber|Awakening Room]]** (map-marked **S**) sits adjacent to the ring, not in it — the one-time pocket for the Session-1 awakening only.

Connective structure: the **[[The Keystone Tablet|two keystones]]** sit at opposite ends of the loop (Map-Keystone in the Niche, room 6; Compass-Keystone in a hidden enclave, room 16). A **one-way painted door** runs from the Map-Keystone Niche (6) to the Bell Junction (7). The wrapping **Stutter Stairs** (15) tie the Buzzsaw/Wesley-tangent side back to the Counting Room (5) via a secret door, with a **hidden landing** dropping to the Compass enclave (16). Rooms **11 (Mercer Antechamber)** and **14 (Buzzsaw Room)** sit against the bite as future hidden-shortcut entrances for when the Mercer and Lake sectors come online.

**The rooms:**
1. **Dungeon Entrance** — the boiler-room hatch down from [[The Mill]]'s lower works. In Session 1 the way onward reads **caved-in** until the PCs engage the Awakening Room. *(Interaction hook TBD beyond the entrance itself.)*
2. **Artifact Hall** — long hallway lined with **artifacts on stone shelves** along both walls (loot / inspection / forensic reading). Runs entrance → vestibule.
3. **Octagonal Vestibule** — the hub; a door on each of the eight walls. **Three** lead onward down various paths; **two are fake-door traps**; **two** open to closet-sized spaces (one **loot**, one **sleeping monsters** — a medium encounter, likely to wake when the door opens); one is the entrance from the Artifact Hall.
4. **Switchbacks** — a series of switch-back pathways between the Vestibule and the Counting Room. *(Interaction hook TBD.)*
5. **Counting Room** — secret door up to the Stutter Stairs (15). *(Identity/hook TBD — the name wants a tally/counting motif.)*
6. **Map-Keystone Niche** — holds the **Map-Keystone** behind a **glyph-lock** (puzzle TBD). A **one-way painted door** leads out to the Bell Junction (7).
7. **Bell Junction** — a **bell suspended overhead**; striking it (a projectile or some clever reach) **changes the room** — a reward, not a gate (proceeding never requires it). Doors: the one-way in from 6; a route toward the Floor-2 path (→ 8); and a **portal-trap door** that dumps whoever crosses it back into the Vestibule (3), standing with their back to a random one of its eight now-shut doors.
8. **Spiral Stair Room** — a spiral stair up to a **sealed Mill-grounds exit**; unsealing it (means TBD) would open a new, unexplored area of the mill grounds above.
9. **The Pit Room** — Floor 1's **exit-guardian** room. A pit in the center: the **wide** crossing is easy but has **2–3 sleeping wolves** in the path; the **narrow ledge** is monster-free but risks a fall. A **hard monster** sits in the pit, unable to climb out on its own — the PCs have a means it can't use (e.g., a ladder too small for it, or a creature that can't climb one well).
10. **Down-Stairs** — stairs down to **Floor 2**.
11. **Mercer Antechamber** — bite-adjacent (future Mercer-sector shortcut). Candidate for **lore/info and/or treasure**.
12. **Spiked Corridor** — a creative but not-too-tough **spike-trap gauntlet** to traverse.
13. **Pillared Reach** — the **young [[The Hush]]** encounter (Session 1: the janitor **Wendell Pace** hollows here).
14. **Buzzsaw Room** — the **Session-1 climax** ([[Wesley Crane|Wesley]]'s first real sigil/portal; see below). Bite-adjacent. **Needs a permanent identity** for repeat visits — it's only "the Buzzsaw Room" once.
15. **Stutter Stairs** — the long **wrapping stair** around the bite; connects the Buzzsaw side to the Counting Room (5, secret door), with a **hidden landing** down to the Compass enclave (16).
16. **Compass-Keystone Enclave** — hidden; holds the **Compass-Keystone**, reached via the Stutter Stairs' hidden landing; guarded by a **deep guardian** (TBD).
- **S — [[The Ember Chamber|Awakening Room]]** — the one-time, entity-made pocket adjacent to the ring; the Session-1 awakening only, then it closes forever.

See [[The Keystone Tablet]] for what the two Floor-1 keystones do.

## Floor 1 in Session 1
The floor is **partially pre-cleared by Wesley** on a recent solo delve; the party follows his footprints, spent torches, drag marks, a cloth scrap (seen again on Wesley), stacked corpses, tally marks, and chalk-struck glyphs — their first encounter with the glyph-language. On the map his trail runs from the Entrance (1) through the Artifact Hall (2) and Octagonal Vestibule (3), out toward the Mercer Antechamber (11), the Spiked Corridor (12), and the Pillared Reach (13), ending at the **Buzzsaw Room (14)** — where Wesley is finalizing **his first real sigil** ([[Wesley's Sigils]]); he says one line (*"You poor things"*), triggers the sigil, and vanishes. The engineered wave that follows is tuned for a likely TPK with a narrow sigil-escape window. See [[Session 1 — The Dare]].

*(Open pacing question: whether the Spiked Corridor's (12) traps are live or pre-sprung by Wesley on the S1 approach, given the party is Level 1.)*

## Floors 2-5 and steady-state mechanics
Floors 2-5 await detailed design (built one at a time as each comes online). Steady-state: daily intrinsic **floor reset** (chalk and glow persist; footprints and blood are gone), Stretch-based resource attrition, the glyph-language as a persistent knowledge resource, and the **sigil cat-and-mouse** with Wesley (real vs. decoy sigils). Forensic delving — residue-reading, sigil-reading, tracking Wesley's status via the gradient-shuffle — is the signature mechanic here. See [[Dungeon Mechanics]], [[Floor Reset and the Intelligence Gradient]], [[Forensic Delving]].

The Act-1 climax is partial access to **floor 6**, repulsed before significant exploration. After [[The Mercer Gate]] opens at the equinox, the Mill sector's floors 1-5 stop being campaign-active (explored, mapped, drained).
