# The Cataloguing Office

Up: [[The Mill Sector]]

**Floor 1, Room 2 — the players' first real room of dungeon: a long hidden office where [[Elias Mercer]]'s operation once catalogued the finds hauled up from below. Century-old and yet mid-shift, as if the clerks stepped out thirty seconds ago. A forensic mirror of what the party is about to do.**

> [!danger] DM-only
> Spoiler room. The relics are [[Elias Mercer]]'s — his 1880s cataloguing operation, kept below and hidden from the mill work above. The ledgers here are the same archive as the sealed Mercer papers in [[The Mercer Mansion]] (split across surface and dungeon). Nothing names Mercer yet; this is a slow-burn seed for the Act-2 papers reveal.

## What it is
A hidden field office pressed into the dungeon's architecture just past the entrance (see [[The Mill Sector]]), where Mercer's people logged, measured, sketched, photographed, tagged, and shelved everything recovered from the deeper floors before sending it up. Keeping the cataloguing below and out of sight of the ordinary mill hands fit the operation ([[The Mill]] was always cover over the south entrance). It is the administrative heart of a century-old delving operation — and it is still, impossibly, on the clock.

## The space
A long straight hall, narrow central aisle, walls thick enough (6–8 ft of stone) to open into recesses at intervals, alternating left and right so the whole row is never in view at once. The recesses are work-stations and pigeonhole shelving: writing desks and standing lecterns with open ledgers, auto-refilling inkwells, pens, blotters, loupes, calipers; a plate camera on a tripod; drying racks for photographic plates; shelves of tagged finds (brass/card numbers); specimen jars and mineral trays. Lit by the office's own lamps, still burning. Warm, close, papery, faintly chemical (developer, lamp oil). A chair pushed back, tea still steaming, a pen resting mid-word with wet ink — a workplace caught mid-task in 1888.

## The tension (no combat in Session 1)
Three dials, no dice:
- **Blind aisle.** Alternating recesses make the corridor a gauntlet of blind corners — it reads exactly like an ambush hall, teaching the body-language of dread. (Where a static lurker will live on repeat delves; see below.)
- **Impossibly-recent workplace.** Wet ink, warm tea, work left mid-motion — the party is intruding on someone's shift. Fresher the deeper you go, not older: a PC who clocks that has clocked the wrongness.
- **Still on the clock.** A ledger page turns itself; the plate camera's shutter clicks once as someone passes — and a plate taken here, developed later, shows the party standing in the office. The operation files them too.

## What's worth a Stretch (loot is real and leaves the dungeon)
Opportunity, not obligation — but the loot genuinely works and genuinely leaves, so players learn to trust that placed items are takeable:
1. **Read the ledgers** (History/Investigation) — concrete intel: finds-by-floor foreshadowing what's deep, a partial old floor-map, early Mercer-papers material. Information persists in the players' Atlas notes (see [[The Keystone Tablet]]).
2. **Take useful gear** that leaves with them — a lamp that never burns down (a minor "weird effect" item topside), fine calipers/dividers (tool advantage on a later glyph-lock), a loupe; tagged curios [[Earl's Pawn Shop|Earl]] will buy — a modest, deliberate faucet of odd artifacts into the topside world.
3. **The plate camera** — take the freshly-exposed plate; developed later it shows the party in the office: an early "the dungeon is doing something to us" clue and a genuinely weird artifact that left the dungeon.
4. **Wind the phonograph** — a dictation cylinder of a clerk's voice reading catalogue entries, flat and by the end not quite human. No mechanical payoff; a big tonal one.

## Reset & persistence
- The office is architecture-class dressing: it **restocks on the daily [[Floor Reset and the Intelligence Gradient|floor reset]]** — supplies refill, plates blank, the "just stepped out" tableau resets. This is why it stays eerily fresh, and why it's a renewable (deliberately low-yield) node.
- **Items carried out to the surface persist normally** (as found keystones do). Loot is real. There is deliberately **no "nothing leaves the dungeon" rule** — some artifacts leaving the dungeon, occasionally with weird topside effects, is a feature the campaign wants (recommend promoting this as a general line in [[Gear and Items]]).
- Governor: generic supplies and curios restock (farmable but low practical value); marquee one-off items (a specific weird artifact) are taken once.

## The Mercer seed (DM-only)
The tools and paperwork map one-to-one onto what the PCs are about to do — map, document, collect. Mercer is the party a century early, and privately he is [[Wesley Crane|Wesley]]'s future too. The ledgers here and the sealed papers in [[The Mercer Mansion]] are one archive split across surface and dungeon; a PC who eventually connects them scores a real forensic win. Who maintains the office now is left open in play — the DM-truth being [[The Dungeon|the dungeon]] itself, an Order-machine that keeps its last user's operation running on a loop forever (agency-loss rendered as an office that never closes). See [[Elias Mercer]], [[Order and Chaos]].

## Session 1 vs steady-state
- **S1 (pre-cleared):** no fight. [[Wesley Crane|Wesley]]'s fresh footprints run straight down the century-old aisle without stopping — to him the office is furniture (a cold character beat). Atmosphere plus the party's first forensic breadcrumbs: his hours-old trail laid over Mercer's century-old workplace, the two users a hundred years apart.
- **Steady-state (repeat delves):** the blind aisle wants a static Floor-1 lurker that hides as one of the exhibits and moves only in the recesses no one is watching — a museum-horror pull from the town's cultural catchment (see [[The Nightmare Engine]]); build with the Floor-1 bestiary.
- **Optional DM texture (later delves):** faint signs a *modern* hand has worked these desks (a newer pencil, a coffee-ring among the 1880s teacups) — a forensic breadcrumb linking Wesley's present to Mercer's past, if you want to spatialize the "Wesley = Mercer's future" thread.
