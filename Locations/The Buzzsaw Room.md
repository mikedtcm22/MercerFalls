# The Buzzsaw Room

Up: [[The Mill Sector]]

**Floor 1, Room 14 — the Session-1 climax, where [[Wesley Crane|Wesley]] opens his first real sigil and the floor tries to kill the party. Physically it's the Millrace: a chamber all but filled by a deep, fast-water channel, crossed only by a winding single-file catwalk — a stage built to strip the party of its action economy. ("Buzzsaw Room" is DM shorthand; there is no literal saw.)**

> [!danger] DM-only
> Spoiler room, and the Act-1 opening set-piece. Full sigil / wave / escape-window mechanics live in [[Wesley's Sigils]] and [[Session 1 — The Dare]]; this note is the room and the fight's tactical shape. Also the dormant secret entrance the **Lake sector** wires into in Act 2.

## Physical layout — the Millrace
Almost the entire floor of the chamber is gone: in its place a **wide channel of fast water runs the room's length — ~15 ft down and reaching nearly wall to wall**, with only a **thin stone ledge along each side wall**. A fall is effectively a hollowing (swept away, gone; the body reconstitutes at the entrance). The only crossing is a **single-file catwalk of narrow metal grating that zigzags out over the water**, segment to segment, from the **south entrance** (in from [[The Pillared Reach]], 13) toward the far end — the whole traversal is single-file over the current, which is what makes the room an anti-surround stage. [[Wesley Crane|Wesley]] works his sigil on the **west ledge, about mid-room**; in the **far northwest corner** beyond it, a plain stone archway becomes the **portal**. A **sealed sluice-gate** feeds the channel — dormant now, it's where the **Lake sector** connects in Act 2. The room's permanent draw sits in a **far corner** across the water (below). *(The catwalks are metal grating; the drawn map's wooden planks are a sprite placeholder.)*

## The Session-1 climax
Canon beats (see [[Wesley's Sigils]], [[Session 1 — The Dare]]): the party follows Wesley's trail out onto the catwalks to find him finishing his **first real sigil** at the circle on the west ledge. One line — *"You poor things"* — he triggers it and is gone. The sigil's equivalent-exchange throws a **wave**, escalating through weak pack-floor creatures and culminating in the **northwest portal** disgorging the **Dogman Lieutenant and a wolf** (below). That portal is also the **only out** — a closing escape window — so the fight and the flight run on the same single-file catwalks over the water.

**Wendell dies here — if he was saved in the Pillared Reach.** Caught out on the catwalks, he doesn't make the last span to the portal before the Lieutenant reaches him, and he's hollowed in front of the party — the demonstration casualty for the escape stakes, moments before they must run the same gauntlet. (If he already hollowed in the Reach, skip.) See [[The Pillared Reach]].

## The fight — a stage that eats action economy
The geometry exists to **deny the party a surround**, so the pack's speed and Multiattack snowball instead of getting dogpiled:
- The **winding single-file catwalks string the party out over the water.** They can't circle the Lieutenant; on a one-person span only the front one or two engage while the rest queue behind.
- The **Dogman Lieutenant** — a modified [[The Nightmare Engine|Gnoll]] Pack Lord: ordinary warrior Str/Dex, but **keeps Multiattack and a reach (whip) weapon** (hits the front PC *and* the second-in-line down a catwalk), **no Incite Rampage.** Opens at range (longbow) from the far ledge / the portal end, forcing the party out onto the spans to reach it.
- A **wolf** works a **parallel catwalk span** to reach the strung-out squishies mid-line, so the party can't answer both threats or bunch to help — this is what finally turns the anti-surround geometry against them. Its knock-prone on a one-person span over open water is a step from gone.
- The **water is the equalizer** — the party's hard-won swing is to put a monster over the edge (a shove, a clever push, a spent ability). *Optional rider* if you want the monsters using it too: on a Lieutenant melee hit, the target makes a STR/Dex save or is pushed 5 ft (toward the water). Include it to make near-misses lethal; drop it to keep the block clean.

Not meant to be *won* — the likely-TPK capstone on an already-bloodied party. The portal is the out; the water is the pressure valve.

## Permanent identity — the room you have to come back to
Once the sigil moment is spent, the Millrace stays a **permanently deadly-terrain room** (wide water + winding single-file catwalks) that makes any later wandering fight here dangerous by geometry alone — a [[Combatscape Layers|Combatscape]] board that's all hazard and chokepoint. Its draw is a **major optional reward: a genuinely good magic weapon or armor piece in the far corner, across the water** — dropped by someone who fell here long ago. It's a fixed placed find (it persists — not delve-loot that resets), one-time, and reaching it means re-crossing the room that may well have killed the party in Session 1. That's the identity: **the room you avoid, and the reward for going back anyway.** Most groups won't return until they're stronger — which is exactly right.

## Aftermath & loot
The Lieutenant and wolf leave **corpses** (natives — they stay, lootable: crude gnoll gear, a curio; see [[Hollowing and Agency-Loss]]). The far-corner magic item is the marquee (above). Wesley leaves only the **spent sigil** on the west ledge — persistent, a forensic study object (see [[Wesley's Sigils]]).
