# The Sinclair Station

Up: [[Mercer Falls — Setting]]

**The downtown gas station where [[Henry Voss]] works the counter — a low-risk routine that keeps a hollowed man alive, and the campaign's quiet daily portrait of agency-loss.**

## Description
A small Sinclair gas station in [[The Districts|downtown]]. Its significance is its counterman: **[[Henry Voss]]**, a 1991 mill survivor who has been hollowed for five years (see [[The 1991 Mill Incident]]). Voss has a fixed, low-risk routine — exactly the kind of safe daily life that lets a hollowed person survive for years (a hollowed mill worker handling molten metal lasts hours; a hollowed clerk shrugs through his days). His **five-sentence rotation** at the counter is one of the renewable [[The Wrongness Layer|Filter-noticed]] details.

## In play
A casual town-mode stop and the place PCs most often encounter Voss in his ordinary affect. Per the Ember vocabulary, Voss reads as a man with *no warmth, the ember gone out, ash behind his eyes — a stove banked too long* (the inverse of the PCs' [[The Embers]]). After the [[The Hungry House|Hungry House]] episode reveals his story, the station becomes the lingering reminder that the 1991 casualties aren't all dead — some are still standing behind a counter.

> [!danger] DM-only
> Voss is one of the named [[Hollowed Townspeople]] — walking proof of [[Hollowing and Agency-Loss]] that players can't yet read as such ([[The Reality Filter]] handles the question of why a man like this just keeps shrugging through his days). Were he ever Filter-pierced (hard at low levels), he'd say something Order-flavored about "the other one, the one that wants us to scream forever" — a cosmology seed for far later. His slow drawl matching other townspeople is a first whisper that 1991 wasn't an isolated accident.
