# The Dungeon's Surface Footprint

Up: [[Mercer Falls — Setting]]

**How [[The Dungeon]] relates to the town's surface: a circle ~half a mile across, three entrances, and upper floors built as fragmented arcs that don't connect.**

> [!danger] DM-only
> This is the campaign's hidden geography. Players learn it incrementally across Acts 1-3.

## The circle
A roughly circular region beneath the town, ~half a mile in diameter, centered between the mill bluff (south) and [[Mercer Lake]] (north). The river runs across its diameter; the falls sit near its southern edge. The surface above the dungeon includes the mill bluff, the river corridor, the lake's south shore, the [[The Mercer Mansion|mansion]] grounds, and the wooded terrain between. Surface effects attenuate at the circle's edges — which is why [[The River and the Falls|the falls]] read as normal.

## Three known surface entrances
- **The Mill entrance** (south edge). The main entrance, within [[The Mill]]'s lower works. Sealed since 1991; the only entrance PCs can use through Act 1, via trespass. Drops into [[The Mill Sector]].
- **The Mansion entrance / [[The Mercer Gate]]** (east-southeast edge). Behind the sealed basement door. Inaccessible until [[The Equinox Ritual]] unlocks it. Drops into the Mercer sector; **becomes the PC's standard Act-2 access point**.
- **The Lake entrance** (north edge). Submerged stonework on the lake's south shore (the cold spot), accessible only at low summer pool with diving gear. Bypasses the upper floors and lands on a deeper Tier-2 floor (likely 8 or 9, deferred). Late-campaign reward; PCs may never find it.

## Fragmented arcs
The upper floors (1-5) are **fragmented arcs by sector** — each entrance leads into its own sector, and the sectors **do not connect** on the upper floors. A party entering through the Mill can fully map the Mill sector's floors 1-5 without ever reaching the Mercer or Lake sectors. The fragmentation is intentional: it keeps the three entrances from collapsing into one space and makes each sector's discovery a real milestone.

**The fragmentation ends at floor 6**, where the lower system unifies. From floor 6 down it's one continuous space reachable from any sector. The Mercer sector's floors 1-5 are a low-conflict *transit corridor* — a shortcut to floor 6 once the Gate opens. The Lake entrance bypasses 1-5 entirely.

## Campaign-architecture implications
- **Act 1** = the Mill sector's grueling floors 1-5 (the heavy dungeon-design investment; see [[The Mill Sector]]), climaxing in partial access to floor 6.
- **Act 2** opens with the Gate unlocking at the equinox; the Mercer shortcut becomes the descent route and the Mill sector's 1-5 stop being campaign-active.
- **Act 3** is the deep, unified Tier-2 system; the Lake entrance, if found, accelerates it.

Narrative weight follows: Mill sector 1-5 = dozens of sessions of heavy content; Mercer sector 1-5 = deliberately low-detail (a few encounter rolls, one set-piece per floor); Tier-2 = heavy again. The final lore-bearing chamber sits deep in Tier-2.
