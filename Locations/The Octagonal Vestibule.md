# The Octagonal Vestibule

Up: [[The Mill Sector]]

**Floor 1, Room 3 — the hub the whole floor routes through, and the campaign's thesis rendered as a room: an Order-machine that offers eight ways out and, the instant you choose one, erases the other seven. The dungeon eats agency; this room does it to your feet.**

> [!danger] DM-only
> Spoiler room. Puzzle-hub; the mechanic and layout below are the DM's to reveal through play.

## Identity
An eight-sided chamber with a door on every wall — the floor's decision node and its clearest single expression of the cosmology (see [[Order and Chaos]], [[The Dungeon]]): choice forecloses alternatives. It teaches that in the body — you pick a door and watch the rest turn to stone — long before anyone explains the cosmology aloud.

## The space
A squat octagonal room, unremarkable but for the doors: one centered in each of the eight walls, identical, plain, closed. Fresh dressing each delve (the two dead dogmen below are the Session-1 exception).

## The door mechanic (the heart of the room)
**Default state** — what the daily [[Floor Reset and the Intelligence Gradient|reset]] restores: all eight doors present and closed. The eight walls, in fixed positions:
- **1 entrance** — whichever door you came in by (in S1, from [[The Cataloguing Office]], room 2).
- **3 real onward paths** — to the rest of the floor: the Switchbacks (4) / Counting Room side; the Mercer Antechamber (11) / Buzzsaw side (Wesley's route); the Bell Junction (7) / floor-2 side.
- **2 fake doors** — traps (below).
- **2 closets** — closet-sized dead ends: one **loot**, one **sleeping pack-beasts** (below).

**Commit & collapse.** The instant the party opens a door that *leads out of the room* (one of the 3 real paths), the room collapses: every other door sinks back into blank wall, leaving only the door you entered by and the one you're leaving through. Fake doors and closets **don't** trigger the collapse (they lead nowhere), so they're free to open and investigate first.

**Path-lock until reset.** The collapsed state **holds until the overnight reset.** Once the party commits a spoke, the hub is locked to it for the rest of the delve — they can shuttle up and down that wing freely, but cannot re-route to a *different* wing back through the Vestibule until tomorrow. Inter-wing travel then has to use the ring's other links (the Stutter Stairs (15) tying the Buzzsaw side to the Counting Room, the one-way painted door 6→7, the secret doors). Two deliberate consequences: picking the "wrong" door **forces exploration** (you're committed to that wing for the day), and a party can **inadvertently upset [[Wesley Crane|Wesley]]'s plans** by committing a spoke that cuts across whatever he's doing that day — without ever knowing they did.

**Memory reward.** The layout is **fixed** — same three paths to the same places, same two fake doors, same two closets, every delve. Learn it once (it goes in the players' Atlas notes, which persist — see [[The Keystone Tablet]]) and the hub becomes instant: step in, open the door you want, gone. The room punishes the first blind visit and rewards knowledge forever after — the right shape for a room the party crosses constantly.

## The fake doors
Opening one springs the machine's rebuke: the wall parts like a door, but it's a stone maw that snaps shut on whoever pulled it (minor bludgeoning, shoved back / knocked prone) before grinding back to blank wall. Low-threat, memorable. The lesson is clean — *never open positions [X] and [Y]* — and the two fakes never move.

## The closets
- **Loot closet.** A dead-end nook with a small, reliable payout that **restocks each delve** (low-value consumables — torches, a ration or two, a minor curio; loot leaves the dungeon per [[Gear and Items]]). The reward for remembering which closet is the good one.
- **Sleeping-beasts closet.** A dead-end nook holding a knot of sleeping **Dogmen** (Floor-1 pack theme — see [[The Nightmare Engine]]; a medium encounter for level) that most likely **wake when the door opens.** Static Floor-1 respawn — back, and asleep, each delve. The reward for remembering which closet is the bad one — or a fight the party can choose to pick.

## Session 1 — the funnel
The party arrives to find the room already **collapsed to Wesley's choice.** He pre-cleared the floor earlier the same day and committed the Mercer-Antechamber spoke (11 → Spiked Corridor → Pillared Reach → Buzzsaw); the reset hasn't intervened, so his onward door stands open, his entry (the Office door) is theirs too, and the other six walls are blank, inert stone. There is genuinely, diegetically, **one way through** — no rail, just the room's own logic — so a curious first-session party can't scatter into full exploration and lose the Buzzsaw / young-Hush climax. Strong cues stack the same way: Wesley's fresh trail runs through the open door (footprints, a spent torch, drag marks), a chalk arrow or glyph struck beside it, a draft and faint sound from the passage beyond. They meet the seven-door puzzle for the first time only on their **first post-reset re-entry** — a real "wait, there were doors here?" beat.

## The two dead dogmen (S1)
With the door choice pre-made, the room's *content* is what it has to say — so it says it with two dead **Dogmen** on the floor, Wesley's pre-clear kills (Floor-1 pack theme). Per the native-death rule they don't dissolve — they lie where they fell until reset and are **lootable** (crude gear, a curio or two) — and *how* they died is a **forensic read**: clean, economical kills, or the scorch-signature of a sigil, telegraphing Wesley's methods and that something walked through here recently and unbothered. See [[Hollowing and Agency-Loss]] (natives don't hollow).

## Cross-room hook
The Bell Junction (7) portal-trap that "dumps whoever crosses it back into the Vestibule with all eight doors shut" lands them in exactly this **sealed default state** — puzzle and all — mid-delve. The rooms already interlock: get portal-trapped, and you're re-solving the hub from scratch, or from memory.

## Deferred / to draw
