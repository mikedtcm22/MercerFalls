# The Mercer Mansion

Up: [[Mercer Falls — Setting]]

**Late-Victorian mansion at the tip of the Bluff on the lake's south shore — home of the [[Mercer Falls Historical Society]], the campaign's primary investigable site, and (secretly) the dungeon's third entrance.**

## Description
Three stories, brick and limestone, wraparound porch, wrought-iron gates. The direct [[Elias Mercer|Mercer]] line is extinct; the last descendant (a great-grandnephew who never lived in town) died in the early 1970s and willed the house and grounds to the [[Mercer Falls Historical Society]], which has run it as a small house museum since. Three rooms restored period-correct (Elias's study, dining room, front parlor); the rest closed off behind velvet ropes and "staff only" signs. Chronically underfunded, volunteer-run.

**Staff.** One full-time curator, **[[Mrs. Devereaux]]** (late 60s, sharp, possessive). Two part-time docents, a Tuesday bookkeeper, and a roster of teen/retiree volunteers — **[[Eddie Park]]** is on it and effectively haunts the reading room.

**Key spaces:**
- **Library annex** (restored east wing, public): catalogued period newspapers, town records, ledgers, family correspondence.
- **The inner archive** (locked door at the back of the reading room): rare books, unindexed papers, Elias's diaries. No public access. Three keyholders: Mrs. Devereaux, the board chair, and **[[Wesley Crane]]** (major donor, two years on the board).
- **Third floor:** the Society office.
- **The basement:** utilities, deeper storage, locked from inside. A chunk of **dungeon-stone** is visible in the foundation through a small ground-level east-wall window.

## In play
The mansion is a primary Time-Points investigable site. Investment surfaces: catalogued Mercer correspondence, donation records (Wesley's escalating involvement), volunteer rolls, board minutes (including Wesley's "spiritual retreat" booking when it lands), and — late — possible access to the inner archive.

> [!danger] DM-only
> The **inner archive holds the sealed Mercer papers** — the documents whose later passages will eventually break Wesley (see [[Forensic Delving]], [[The Pattern Recognition Arc]]). At the back of the basement, behind storage shelving, is a heavy oak, iron-banded **sealed door** with no visible lock. Mrs. Devereaux assumes it's a disused root cellar; the Society's records say so. It isn't. Behind it, after a short stone passage with Mercer family carvings, is **[[The Mercer Gate]]**. Wesley discovered the door during his early board tenure and has studied it for two years; he cannot open it. Opening it is the point of [[The Equinox Ritual]]. The Society is institutional cover for Wesley's fifteen-year project. The door is undiscoverable through normal museum access — finding it requires basement invitation or breaking in.
