# The Spiked Corridor

Up: [[The Mill Sector]]

**Floor 1, Room 12 — a trapped corridor of rising-and-falling spikes between the Expedition Post (11) and the Pillared Reach (13). Not a puzzle so much as a gauntlet: seemingly random, survivable, with several ways across for different kinds of players. On Wesley's Session-1 trail; the janitor's blood is the telegraph.**

> [!danger] DM-only
> Spoiler room.

## The space
A straight run of flagstone (~30–40 ft of trapped floor) pocked with spike-holes, wall-torches down both sides, and something heavy hanging from the ceiling near the middle. Doors: in from [[The Expedition Post]] (11), on to the Pillared Reach (13, the young [[The Hush]]).

## The trap — a traveling wave
Spikes rise and fall across the tiles in a rolling wave with **no all-clear moment** — the instinct to wait for a gap fails, so a careful party usually commits and eats a hit before the logic lands. The pattern **looks random** and isn't worth trying to time as a rhythm.

**The hidden read.** It isn't fully random: **some tiles never fire** — same spike-holes, but they stay dead — and those safe tiles form a **diagonal line** from one side of the floor to the other as you cross. A PC who stops to study the field (Investigation / Int) spots the dead tiles and the diagonal; walk it and the wave never touches you. It's the same every delve, so once learned it's a safe stroll forever after (Floor-1 memory reward). Most parties clock it only after a step or two of watching — and a hit or two.

## Four ways across
- **Run it (nerve/luck).** Just go. The DM rolls how many times the wave catches you over the crossing — suggested: a d4 of spike-hits across the trapped stretch, each modest piercing with a Dex save for half; Dashing trades finesse for fewer exposures. The default, and fine — spikes are a sting.
- **Walk the diagonal (the read).** Spot the never-firing tiles → cross safe. See above.
- **Kill the spikes (the trick).** The wall-torches aren't all torches: one is a disguised release (its bracket sits wrong, or it doesn't burn). Pull it and a counterweight drops — grinding — and the whole spike-field retracts and stays down for the delve (re-arms on reset). A reward for examining the fixtures instead of the floor; instant-safe once known.
- **Swing it (flair).** The thing hanging from the ceiling — an old hoist-chain / heavy iron ring — is a lasso target: a would-be roper lands a line and swings the spike-field. Athletics/Acrobatics to stick it; a miss drops you mid-span onto a spike or short of the far side. Tie off a line and the rest can follow.

## Tuning
Spikes are a **sting, not a threat** — modest damage, Dex save for half — because the room teaches caution, it doesn't threaten a Level-1 death. **Session 1 never requires a solve:** the wave is active, but the party can simply run it and come out the far side a little bloodied.

## Session 1 — the telegraph
Wesley threaded the diagonal (he knows it); the wave is live when the party arrives. The single **blood-flecked, sprung spike** is the janitor **Wendell Pace's** — dragged through on Wesley's leash, he couldn't thread it and tripped one. That fresh blood on a raised spike is the whole "this is a trap room, do not barrel through" cue, delivered without a word or a check. (Wendell goes on to hollow in the Pillared Reach next door — see [[Session 1 — The Dare]].)

## Steady-state
Fixed pattern; the diagonal, the torch-release, and the ceiling fixture are constants to be learned. On repeat delves a **wandering monster** can land here, which turns the wave into a third combatant — a moving hazard across the floor (a [[Combatscape Layers|Combatscape]] Layer-1 hazard in a live fight): the party who knows the diagonal fights from safe footing while the monster doesn't.
