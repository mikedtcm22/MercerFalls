# Jerry's Pawn Shop

Up: [[Mercer Falls — Setting]]

**A pawn shop in [[The Districts|The Flats]] that asks no questions — the PCs' cash channel for dungeon loot, the town's whisper-hub, and its accidental witness, whose private ledger quietly counts the hollowed.**

## Description
A working-class pawn shop in the Flats, run by **[[Jerry]]**. He buys dungeon antiques without provenance and occasionally sells weird items — a steady-state gear/cash channel for the PCs (the money loop; bible §12). Comes online **early, in [[Episode 2 — The Hush]]**: the players took to the idea of a pawn shop in Session 1, so it's available as somewhere to turn dungeon haul into supply money (the trip still costs [[Time Points]]). Jerry's deeper value is as an intel node — see below.

## In play
Two things happen here:
- **Selling loot** — fast cash, low rates, no questions ([[Gear and Items]] / [[Time Points]]).
- **Whispers** — Jerry is the town's human-intel counterpart to [[Russ]]: he sees people at their most desperate and hears what the town won't say. In [[Episode 2 — The Hush]] that's the Track-A investigation — the Vances, who's gone where, who's been acting strange. A Time-Points beat surfaces gossip (1 pt) or a real, load-bearing whisper (2 pt).
- **Maybe the graphic novel.** Russ may have sold Jerry the obscure [[The Hush-Hunting Graphic Novel|Hush graphic-novelization]] a while back; if Jerry's is the node the party has skipped, it's here to buy — the den-tracking lore (F4) that finds the [[The Hush|Hush]]'s lair.

The accidental-witness thread runs underneath: dozens of locals pawn things on a slow drip to keep up with bills, and **Jerry has noticed the pattern.** In conversation he can hint he knows accurate counts of hollowed townspeople — they all pawn things eventually because they don't notice they're running through their savings. He doesn't use the word "hollowed"; he's just seen it, and kept a **private ledger.** He sees the town accurately because he's its accidental witness — but he has no [[The Reality Filter|Filter]]-permission to *act,* so he only catalogues. The [[Henry Voss|Voss]] thread ([[Episode — The Hungry House]]) deepens it: Voss has pawned things for years to make mortgage payments, and chasing that leads PCs back to Jerry.

> [!danger] DM-only
> Jerry's ledger is a forensic data set — a hollowed-count of [[Hollowed Townspeople]] surfacing as a spending-pattern signature, without Jerry (or the town) understanding what it means. It corroborates the [[The Wrongness Layer|wrongness]] and feeds [[Forensic Delving]] / [[The Pattern Recognition Arc]]. His characterization (mannerisms, the trigger point for the count reveal) is still loose (bible §20 item 21); the ledger mechanic is locked.
