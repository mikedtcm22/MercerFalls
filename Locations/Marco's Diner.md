# Marco's Diner

Up: [[Mercer Falls — Setting]]

**The downtown diner on Main Street — the town's casual hangout, the party's likely home base, and a default Time-Points conversation venue.**

## Description
A small-town diner on Main Street, three doors north of [[Panels]]. Counter seating and booths, the everyday social crossroads of [[The Districts|downtown]]. Lightly sketched in the town reference (one of the §10 downtown roster); texture filled in lazily as it becomes focal. Russ's tepid Marco's coffee cup turning up in the Episode 1 Panels scene situates the two as neighbors on the strip.

## In play
A natural venue for town-mode investigation — a diner conversation is one of the canonical Time-Points actions in an episode's town phase. Good for overhearing gossip, catching an NPC off-shift, or a low-key meeting. Most NPCs, sub-rooms, and history here are deliberately left to fill in when the location becomes focal in play.

## In play — Episode 2 (The Hush)
A **core** node, available **day and into the evening**. Two roles:
- **The hangout / reconvene stage.** Front-runner for the party's regular spot — the evening booth is where the Day-B reconvene naturally lands, and "meet me at Marco's" is a line anyone can say. *These kids have a place,* which sells the unit fast.
- **The Wendell reveal ("death in the dungeon isn't death").** [[Wendell Pace]] — the janitor the PCs watched hollow on the Buzzsaw catwalk — **busses tables here over the summer,** so a near-guaranteed stop plants the reveal reliably: he's alive, flat, and *off,* going through dull table-work (which already looks like going-through-the-motions, so the wrongness hides until a PC actually looks — Insight catches it). Rhymes with any placid, hollowed party members from the Episode-1 fall. See [[Episode 2 — The Hush — Town-Time Investigation Map]].
- **Filter texture.** The town's "military school" story about [[Ricky Vance|Ricky]] gets repeated here without alarm — a first taste of [[The Reality Filter|the Filter]]'s fog.

> [!danger] DM-only
> A [[The Wrongness Layer|Filter-noticed]] entry: there's **a booth at Marco's where the air smells faintly different** — one of the renewable "always been there" wrongness details (§15). Nothing more is pinned down; let it stay ambient.
