# Mercer Falls, Ohio

Up: [[Mercer Falls — Setting]]

**A dying rust-belt lake town of ~18,000 in northern Ohio, summer 1996 — real first, wrong second.**

## Description
A glacial lake sits at the town's north end and drains south through a river that runs through woods, enters [[The Mill]] atop the mill bluff, and emerges as [[The River and the Falls]] — a real, picturesque 50-foot cascade. South of the falls the river runs along the east edge of downtown and curves out of town to the southeast. The town is laid out across two elevations divided by the mill bluff: the lake on relatively high ground to the north, [[The Districts|downtown]] on flat ground south of and below the bluff. The blast-furnace stack of the dead mill is the tallest structure in town and dominates every long view from the south.

Social geography wraps this spine — see [[The Districts]] for the Heights (old wealth, north/east lake shores), the Flats (working class, southeast lake + river corridor along Pine Street), Sycamore (cheap post-1991 subdivision), and Downtown. The Bluff and the [[The Mercer Mansion|Mercer mansion]] sit on the lake's south shore, their own thing, separate from the Heights. The cemetery is east of downtown; the quarries northwest past the lake; Highway 21 and "the strip" run across the north.

Founded **1887 by [[Elias Mercer]]**, an industrialist with a private occult library and a small persistent gentleman's club ([[The Mercer Gentleman's Club]]) whose attenuated descendants still live in town.

## In play
The town reads as a real Ohio rust-belt lake town in 1996 (pagers, dial-up, no cell phones). It is one of the campaign's two modes of play — the daylight investigation half. Anomalies surface incrementally: missing persons, suicides, and "accidents" run ~4x the national average, statistics the paper won't touch; a "spiritual community center" ([[The Community Center]]) opened in 1996 under [[Wesley Crane]]; and the town feels *stuck* in a way economic decline alone doesn't explain. The wrongness is statistical, historical, and ambient — see [[The Wrongness Layer]].

> [!danger] DM-only
> Mercer chose this site because [[The Dungeon]] was already here; everything in the town's geography is residue of decisions made around that hidden fact. The dungeon occupies a roughly circular region beneath the town between the mill bluff and the lake — see [[The Dungeon's Surface Footprint]]. The citywide apathy is the containment system working as designed ([[Hollowing and Agency-Loss]], [[The Reality Filter]]).
