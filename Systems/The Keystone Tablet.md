# The Keystone Tablet

Up: [[Game Systems]]

**The PCs' navigation tool — the patron's parallel gift, a different gift from the same source as the [[The Embers|embers]]. A hand-sized stone tablet with two slots that take keystones; filling them reveals a floor's layout and points to its down-stairs.**

## Physical Description

A flat stone tablet roughly hand-sized, with **two recessed slots** sized to receive **keystones** — small flat stones of the same dungeon-material as the tablet. A filled slot warms faintly (same fire vocabulary as the embers); when the party is near another keystone, the tablet vibrates softly. It feels alive in the bearer's hand.

> [!danger] DM-only — a cosmology breadcrumb
> Late-campaign, an attentive PC may notice the tablet "feels like the embers feel." Both are extruded [[The Entity|entity]]-substance — the same patron, two gifts. This is the seed of the Order/Chaos forensic payoff.

## The Slots

- **Map slot.** A floor's **map keystone** reveals that floor's *layout* — pathways, intersections, dead-ends, room outlines. It does **not** reveal contents (what's in any room — learned by exploring or decoding door glyphs; see [[Forensic Delving]]).
- **Compass slot.** A floor's **compass keystone** points the bearer toward the current floor's **down-stairs.**

## Distribution

- **Each floor has its own pair** (one map, one compass) hidden on that floor.
- Acquisition varies by floor — guarded, puzzle/trap reward, or deep exploration. This is why speedrunning isn't free (see [[Dungeon Mechanics]]).
- **Floor 1's keystones are NOT pre-loaded.** The *tablet itself* is found during a delve (likely late [[Episode 1 — The Hush]] or in [[Episode 2 — The Hungry House]]), and the PCs hunt down Floor 1's keystones across early delves.

## Player-Facing Map Handouts

When the PCs acquire keystones, the DM hands a physical map handout to all players. Three versions:

- **Map only** (no compass yet) — see the layout, find the stairs by exploration.
- **Stairs only** (no map yet, or map stolen) — know the stairs' direction, navigate blind.
- **Map + stairs** (both keystones) — the full overview.

## Wesley and the Tablet

- [[Wesley Crane|Wesley]] **cannot use** a keystone — inert in his hands (same asymmetry as the sigils and embers).
- Wesley **cannot destroy** keystones — indestructible by his means.
- Wesley **CAN steal** keystones if he discovers them and reaches one first. The planned Floor-3 beat is [[The Keystone Theft]] (he keeps the stolen keystone **above-ground** — community center back room, possibly later the [[The Mercer Mansion|mansion]] — never in the dungeon). The only meaningful failure state is the PCs deciding not to attempt recovery.

## The Players' Atlas Notes

Separate from the tablet: the players keep their own freeform notes — room contents, glyph translations, wandering-monster sightings, sigil locations, residue readings, environmental storytelling. Purely a player artifact, no DM template. **Their accumulated intel persists across all delves and survives TPK** (see [[Death, TPK, and the Lost Day]]).

This is the load-bearing thing that makes "you died, but you learned" tangible: after a wipe, the players point at their notes and the tablet — "we lost the run, but we got two new glyph translations and confirmed the right-side passage on Floor 2." That's the slim crystalline silver-lining the roguelike pillar requires.
