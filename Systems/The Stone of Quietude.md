# The Stone of Quietude

Up: [[Gear and Items]]

**A dungeon-forged magic stone that swallows sound — a hand-held pocket of *Silence* the kids can carry, drop, or throw. The optional prize that makes the [[The Hush|Hush]] climax materially easier: a sound-hunter can't hunt a place that has no sound. Never required; earned toward.**

## What it looks like
About the size of a softball — an oblate, river-smoothed lump that sits heavy in one palm, denser than a rock that size has any business being. Its surface is a matte, lightless charcoal-black — the same not-quite-stone the Hush's tooth is made of, the stuff that lines the dungeon's deepest architecture; light seems to *sink* into it rather than glance off. Worked into the face are faint concentric rings, like the still-water rings after a dropped pebble frozen mid-spread, tightening to a smooth central dimple.

It is cool to the touch and stays cool. Even dormant it has a *tell:* hold it and the room feels a half-second quieter, as if it's always faintly muffling the air at arm's length. Three of the incised rings hold a dim inner glow — **one lit ring per stored charge** — guttering out as the stone is spent and rekindling on a long rest. Tapped against another stone to wake it, it makes no honest *clack:* the strike lands as a flat, dead *tok* that's eaten before it finishes — and then the quiet drops like a held breath.

## How it works
> [!info] Player-facing mechanics
> **Effect — *Silence*.** On activation, for the duration no sound can be created within or pass through a **20-foot-radius sphere** centered on the stone. Anything entirely inside is **immune to thunder damage**; creatures entirely inside are **deafened**; casting a spell with a verbal component inside is impossible.
> - **The stone is always the center.** The sphere follows the stone. Held, it's a moving bubble of silence that travels with you; set down or **thrown**, it anchors the bubble where the stone lands.
> - **Throwing it** is how you put the silence somewhere other than yourself — a chokepoint, or over a monster. But a thrown stone is a loose stone: **anyone can pick it up, kick it, or carry it off,** and nothing stops the bubble walking away with it.
> - **Activation:** an action — tap it against any other stone (a wall, the floor, a rock, even a stone you're about to throw).
> - **Charges:** **3**, starts full. Each activation spends **1**. Regains **1d4** on a long rest (up to the max of 3).
> - **Duration:** 10 minutes, **no concentration** — the stone sustains it. Whoever activated it can end it early (no action) *while holding it;* a thrown or dropped stone runs its full duration, or until someone else picks it up and dismisses it.
> - **No attunement.** Any hand that taps it can use it — which is exactly the risk the moment you throw it.

## Against the Hush
This is the tool the Session-1 Marv's-Spot photo points toward, and the reason it's worth earning. The [[The Hush|Hush]] hunts by sound; inside the bubble it is **deafened — effectively blind,** its echolocation and its voice-mimicry both dead. The film's stated kill is *total silence in a confined space,* and the stone **manufactures that on command:** corner the adult, drop the bubble, and its whole hunt-grammar collapses.

It cuts both ways, and that's the point. Inside the sphere **the party is silenced too** — no shouted warnings, no verbal spells, everyone deafened, no one able to hear the thing they're fighting. A great equalizer against a sound-hunter and a disaster if used carelessly; reward the table that plans hand-signals and picks its moment.
