# Forensic Delving

Up: [[Game Systems]]

**Reading a floor's history through the traces left in it is the campaign's signature investigation skill. Because the daily reset (see [[Floor Reset and the Intelligence Gradient]]) wipes most traces but spares a persistent layer, every delve room tells a continuous story across delves — and both the PCs and [[Wesley Crane]] read each other through these rooms.**

The dungeon is not a vacuum: Wesley delves on his own schedule, the rival/evil party eventually does too, and occasional unfortunates wander in. Players develop forensic delving as a campaign skill — reading both layers below.

## Residue (the load-bearing signal)

When a person is hollowed in a chamber, **residue** is left — a quasi-physical leaving of agency the dungeon hasn't finished consuming. It looks like a thin, unstable shimmer over floor and walls, and **persists through daily reset**, fading on its own hours-to-days timeline (this is what keeps residue-reading viable as the signature mechanic).

| Residue | Reading |
|---|---|
| **Fresh, dense** | Someone hollowed here recently — likely within a day or two |
| **Faint, fading** | Someone hollowed here, but it's been a while |
| **Absent** in an apparent kill-chamber | Nothing happened, or long enough that it's all gone |

**Intensity decodes who:** civilian residue is significant (full agency of an unprotected person, days to dissipate) — a thick chamber is unambiguously where a *civilian* got hollowed.

> [!danger] DM-only — what residue gives away (and hides)
> [[The Embers|Ember]]-bearer residue is **minimal** (the ember intercepts almost the whole transaction; a faint shimmer fading within hours). [[Wesley Crane|Wesley]], with his anti-ember relic, leaves **no residue at all**. So Wesley's adherent-deaths leave clear forensic traces, but the PCs' own hollowings and Wesley's own do not. The Gathered Dust ritual ingredient (see [[The Three Ritual Items]]) is literally harvested civilian residue, collected fresh from a chamber where Wesley just had an adherent hollowed.

## Persistent Signals (the antagonist-activity layer)

The primary environmental-storytelling channel; survives the nightly reset.

- **Sigil-spawn evidence** — sigils freshly inscribed, freshly *spent* (Wesley exited here), freshly *returned-to* (Wesley re-entered here), or cleared by other delvers. See [[Wesley's Sigils]].
- **Chalk-cartography** — Wesley's struck-through glyphs marking his route choices, sometimes annotated.
- **Glyphs in the architecture** — the dungeon's own language, decoded over time (DC 15 Investigation reveals door/wall glyphs; matching glyphs to outcomes builds a private dungeon-language that persists across delves and TPKs). A cultist's notebook with translations is a major score.
- **Residue** — fresh / faint / absent (above).

## Single-Delve Signals (gone by tomorrow)

Visible only during the active delve — wiped by the reset:

- Spent supplies (extinguished torches, used flares, ration wrappers)
- Combat aftermath (drag marks, blood trails, ash-stains in human shapes)
- Personal effects (cloth scraps, dropped tools, identifiable items)
- Monster corpses

**Reading both layers:** single-delve signals show what's happening *today*; the persistent layer shows accumulated antagonist activity. The PCs find sigil-spawned monsters and learn Wesley was here; Wesley returns to a sigil-room, finds it cleared, and learns the PCs were here. The cat-and-mouse is mutual.

Forensic delving connects to two other tracking handles: the **Wesley-status signal** (gradient-shuffle stopping when Wesley is incapacitated — see [[Floor Reset and the Intelligence Gradient]]) and the aboveground **dungeon walk of shame** (see [[Death, TPK, and the Lost Day]]).
