# Floor Reset and the Intelligence Gradient

Up: [[Game Systems]]

**The dungeon resets daily between delves — and *appears* to get smarter the deeper you go. The reset is intrinsic to the machine; the "intelligence" is not. It's [[Wesley Crane]]'s depth-scaled manipulation, the single biggest hidden fact about [[The Dungeon]].**

## Daily Reset (intrinsic)

The dungeon resets daily, between delves. This is intrinsic — [[Order and Chaos|Order]] built the machine with an automatic nightly refresh that runs **whether Wesley is active or not.** PCs entering on Day N find every floor fresh: monsters re-spawned, traps re-armed.

**What persists** (rule of thumb: *chalk and glow persist; footprints and blood are gone*):

- [[Wesley's Sigils|Wesley's sigils]] (real and decoy)
- Wesley's chalk-cartography
- Glyphs on the architecture itself
- Residue (semi-physical agency-substance; fades on its own hours-to-days timeline regardless of reset — see [[Forensic Delving]])
- Found keystones (carried by PCs — see [[The Keystone Tablet]])

**What does NOT persist:** drag marks, blood trails, ash-stains; spent supplies (torches, flares, wrappers); dropped personal effects and tools; monster corpses (and loot on them — **loot during the delve**).

The daily reset operates independently of TPK — every morning is a fresh dungeon, never compounded (see [[Death, TPK, and the Lost Day]]).

## The Floor Intelligence Gradient

Layered on top of the reset. The dungeon seems to get smarter deeper down.

> [!danger] DM-only — it isn't getting smarter; it's Wesley
> The dungeon is a machine, not a mind. **Wesley has more sigil-class manipulation tools available at depth, where he is more deeply integrated with the dungeon.** The gradient describes the *style* of daily reset at each depth — what shape the reset takes — and each tier maps to an ability Wesley has unlocked.

| Floors | Apparent behavior | Underlying mechanic (Wesley) |
|---|---|---|
| 1–2 | Static. Identical layout + monster placements on reset. | Can re-summon monsters but not rearrange them. |
| 3–5 | Monster shuffling. Layout same; which monsters spawn varies. | Can re-summon monsters of his choosing into chambers. |
| 6–9 | Room contents shuffle. A trap room may be empty, monstered, or treasured next time; layout stable. | Can rearrange chamber contents — move monsters, add traps, hide loot. |
| 10+ | Full reshuffles. The layout itself changes; mapping starts fresh each delve. | Deeply integrated — can move walls, restructure pathways. By now he's closer to the dungeon than to himself. |

Floor 1 is a knowable place; Floor 12 is a different dungeon every time — and **both facts are about Wesley, not the dungeon.** When the PCs work this out (late Act 2 at earliest), the entire dungeon retroactively becomes an extension of their antagonist: they've been crawling around inside Wesley all along.

## The Wesley-Status Forensic Signal

> [!danger] DM-only — tracking Wesley without seeing him
> When Wesley is **active**, the gradient operates as designed (shuffling on 3+, contents on 6+, layouts on 10+). When Wesley is **incapacitated** (injured, away, captured), the gradient-shuffle **stops** — the intrinsic daily reset continues, but deep floors come back *static*. Attentive PCs may notice: "Floor 5 is the same configuration today as yesterday — that shouldn't happen." This is the **late-Act-1 forensic handle** for tracking Wesley's status indirectly (pairs with the residue and sigil signals in [[Forensic Delving]]).
