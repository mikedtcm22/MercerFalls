# Death, TPK, and the Lost Day

Up: [[Game Systems]]

**In the dungeon, "death" is hollowing, not death (see [[Hollowing and Agency-Loss]]). The [[The Embers|ember]] intervenes, the body dissolves and walks home on autopilot, and the cost lands as a *lost day* — narrated at the table with the player watching. Outside the dungeon, death is permanent.**

## What Costs What

| Outcome | Cost |
|---|---|
| Survive a delve | Rest of today (no further [[Time Points]]) |
| One+ PCs die, others survive | Dead PCs lose tomorrow; survivors lose only today |
| TPK | All PCs lose tomorrow |
| **Die outside the dungeon** | **Permanent death** |

("Die" is genre shorthand; the underlying event is hollowing.)

## The Dissolve Moment

When a PC drops, their ember intervenes visibly — fire vocabulary throughout. The warmth in their chest *catches*, flares, light pours upward and disperses; the body becomes translucent, dissolves into motes that drift to the ceiling and vanish; a faint shimmer (residue) lingers on the floor and fades over minutes.

> [!danger] DM-only — what the dissolve actually is
> The light is [[The Entity|the entity]]'s substance being spent. The upward motes are the PC's *agency* being routed to the dungeon. The shimmer is residue, and an ember-bearer leaves only a faint trace because the ember absorbs nearly the whole transaction (see [[Forensic Delving]]). Each PC hollowing also ticks the hidden **Surfeit** counter (see [[The Embers]] and [[Wesley's Sigils]]).

**Don't pause the encounter.** The other PCs see the warmth catch and the dissolve, and have to keep fighting — they process while losing.

## Reconstitution and the Walk Home

Minutes-to-an-hour later, the hollowed PC's body re-coalesces at the dungeon's entrance — same clothes, same gear, glassy-eyed, silent, unresponsive to its own name; the warmth in their chest is *cold ash, banked too long*. It walks home on autopilot — route-correct, climbing fences, ignoring traffic, weather, and people — lets itself in, climbs into bed, sleeps.

**The dungeon walk of shame** is *witnessable* — by townspeople (the [[The Reality Filter|Filter]] handles civilians: they perceive the strangeness, don't follow up, rationalize it by morning) and, crucially, by other ember-bearers and PCs who didn't delve and are along the route. **PCs and ember-bearers are not Filtered — they remember.** Staking out [[The Mill]] or a route home lets PCs witness adherent walks, recruit walks, and eventually each other's — a major forensic channel that doesn't require entering the dungeon (pairs with [[Forensic Delving]]).

## What a Lost Day Looks Like

The PC wakes in their own bed next morning with no memory of the walk or the dungeon. The body is intact; the world is normal; parents didn't notice the late return (the Filter covered it). Then they go on **auto-pilot** — flat affect, default behaviors, *present without will.* They show up to scheduled things and say almost nothing; they don't push, ask, or insist. Anything time-locked (Wesley's countdown, NPC plotlines) advances unimpeded.

## How a Lost Day Plays at the Table

Lost days are **not skipped — they are narrated, with the player watching.** This is the campaign's most teeth-bearing dramatic lever.

- **Single PC death:** the DM narrates the dead PC's day in real time alongside the survivors. When the dead PC's "turn" comes, they do — pointedly — nothing: a shrug, a flat half-smile, a reasonable-sounding deflection where intervention was needed. The dead player stays at the table as **pure witness** — they don't get to say "my character wouldn't," because the horror is that today their character does. Aim for **one or two stinging beats**, not a play-by-play.
- **TPK:** a compressed montage — three or four images that sting most (the rival party moving uncontested, an NPC plotline resolving badly, a small broken promise), then cut to the morning. On a TPK, all four kids reconstitute at the mill, possibly together, and walk off in different directions in the dawn light — the campaign's signature image.
- **Partial wipe — temporally fractured party:** survivors have agency tomorrow; the dead don't. Survivors can investigate, confront a cultist, warn someone, sit on their hands, or run a short-handed delve — while the dead PC watches their own body move through the day not acting.

## What Persists Through Hollowing / TPK

- XP earned before the killing blow (kept)
- Loot found before the killing blow (kept)
- Map knowledge, glyph translations, NPC info (kept — see the players' Atlas notes in [[The Keystone Tablet]])
- The [[The Keystone Tablet]] and any acquired keystones (kept — bonded to bearer + entity, not the dungeon)
- Spell slots reset (effective long rest); HP full
- Consumables spent during the run are gone

The daily reset (see [[Floor Reset and the Intelligence Gradient]]) operates independently — it never compounds on a TPK; every morning is a fresh dungeon.
