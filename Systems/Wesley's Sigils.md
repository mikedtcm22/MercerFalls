# Wesley's Sigils

Up: [[Game Systems]]

**[[Wesley Crane|Wesley]]'s anti-ember relic grants a class of inscribed sigil abilities that scale as he integrates further with [[The Dungeon|the dungeon]] — i.e., as he is consumed by it. To the PCs these read as Wesley *mastering* the dungeon; the truth is the boundary between Wesley and dungeon thinning.** Everything here is DM-only.

Each sigil requires **inscription** (drawn with dungeon-extracted chalk/ink-equivalents — provisional; takes time; visible once placed). **PCs cannot use sigils** — the relic is the prerequisite, and even capturing Wesley's notebook leaves the inscriptions inert in PC hands.

## Level 1 — Real Sigils (active from Session 1)

Wesley pre-inscribes a sigil in a chamber; activation is fast (seconds).

- **Exit:** Wesley walks through any door in the room and wakes at home, fully restored. The exit-side stays active **1–2 minutes** — long enough that an astute PC can ride the same door out (lands them in their own bed normally).
- **Cost:** monsters spawn in the room and **persist** — they don't dissipate on their own; they stay bound to the sigil until defeated, at which point the sigil dissipates.
- **Re-entry:** Wesley can return to the sigil's location **once**, from a paired private site (a back room at the community center locked to the public). Once used for re-entry, the sigil is spent and can be redrawn elsewhere for a future delve.

**Cat-and-mouse:** PCs who find a real sigil can clear it — defeating the bound monsters dissipates it and forces Wesley to redraw, costing him significant delve progress. Each delve is a strategic choice: push deeper, or hunt sigils to deny Wesley his fast-travel network. Sigil-hunting is high-risk (every active sigil is a tough encounter) but a successful clear is one of the highest-leverage things the PCs can do. For Wesley, every sigil is a hostage to fortune — he rations placement and sites them deep, in chambers without obvious approaches, misdirected away by his chalk-cartography.

## Level 2 — Decoy Sigils (unlocked mid-Act 1, Week 2–3)

A *different spell*; visually similar but subtly distinct on close reading.

- **Activation:** proximity-triggered (not pre-emptive); summons monsters as a real sigil does.
- **No exit, no re-entry.** The decoy only baits PCs into burning resources fighting to a "sigil" that accomplishes nothing — Wesley loses no progress; the PCs lose HP, slots, consumables.

The first decoy-clear is a significant beat: it tells the PCs Wesley is *gaming the meta*, not just defending positions.

## Detection Paths (distinguishing decoy from real)

Should be hard, especially at Levels 2–3:

- **Visual pattern recognition** over many encounters (unreliable until ~4–5 mixed-type sigils cleared)
- **Glyph-language decoding** — enough decoded dungeon-language makes the inscriptions read clearly different (see [[Forensic Delving]])
- **Forensic deduction** — Wesley's chalk-cartography around real sigils differs from around decoys
- **A captured notebook** resolves the question instantly — highly-prized loot

## Anti-Embers as an Item Type

Sigils flow from the **anti-ember relic** — the Order-side mirror of the PCs' embers. Wesley's relic is ~100% Order (see [[The Embers]] / [[Order and Chaos]]). The equinox produces anti-embers for the [[Wesley's Recruits|Rivals]]: failure-path Evil Party anti-embers are pure-Order (functionally Wesley); success-path Rival Party embers are forced 80/20 Order/Chaos by PC interference. Future Acts may let other anti-ember bearers use Wesley's sigils (the *Shared Sigil* ability below).

## Banked Abilities (provisional — Acts 2–3, not committed)

Pattern: early = *drawing* (sigils, marks) → middle = *mapping/shaping* → late = *summoning* → endgame = *being*.

- **Trapdoor Sigil** — exit lands in a different room than inscription.
- **Shared Sigil** — other anti-ember bearers can use his sigils (coordinated Evil/Rival dungeon force).
- **Silent Sigil** — exit doesn't summon monsters (dungeon stops charging the toll).
- **Bound Sentinel** — binds one specific monster as a guard instead of a wave.
- **Floor Sculpting** — rearrange room contents / move walls on cleared floors (the depth-gradient in [[Floor Reset and the Intelligence Gradient]]).
- **Outflow** — extend dungeon effects (apathy waves, Filter intensification) into specific places in town.

## The Surfeit Counter

> [!danger] DM-only — the hidden cost of saving the PCs
> Every ember-bearer hollowing ticks a behind-the-scenes counter (working name **Surfeit**). [[The Entity|The entity]] pays the dungeon from its own substance to spare the PCs, and that payment accrues. **At thresholds the dungeon becomes incrementally stronger — Wesley unlocks new sigil-class abilities, the [[The Reality Filter|Filter]] strengthens around specific events, the entity's interventions strain.** The PCs don't see the counter; they may notice that bad streaks of delves make the dungeon push back harder. This ties the PCs' own deaths (see [[Death, TPK, and the Lost Day]]) to Wesley's escalating power.
