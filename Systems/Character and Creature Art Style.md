# Character & Creature Art Style

Up: [[Game Systems]]

**The locked look for characters and creatures — the counterpart to the `sketch-it-out` location skill. Same house style as the location plates, but driven by the prompt text, NOT by an attached reference image.**

## The style (house style, character-tuned)
> modern full-color graphic-novel / comic-book illustration; clean, confident ink linework and hatching modeling the anatomy, under loose, atmospheric painted rendering; muted cel-style coloring; a single strong dominant light source (dramatic, not flat) — filmic, not dark-noir murk; deep atmospheric perspective; nostalgic 1980s–90s Amblin / Stranger Things / Paper Girls sensibility; grounded Rust-Belt Americana; muted, desaturated palette; figure reads clearly against a dark ground (legible in shadow, not lost in black).

Visual exemplar: **`the_hush.png`** (the Hush plate) — the target for linework, desaturated palette, single-light drama, and pale-figure-against-dark legibility. Describe *toward* that look in words. (See the next rule about not attaching it.)

## Key rule — drive it with the prompt; don't attach a scene reference
For characters and creatures, **rely on the prompt text; do NOT attach a prior full-scene art piece as a reference image.** Tested: attaching a creature-in-environment plate (`the_hush.png`) onto a new creature prompt **locked the reference's background/environment** onto the result and flattened the subject's uniqueness — the opposite of what a distinct new figure needs. The text prompt alone produced the stronger, more unique image.

This is the deliberate **inverse** of the `sketch-it-out` location practice note, where attaching 1–2 references *helps* lock palette and rendering for environments:
- **Locations →** attach references.
- **Characters / creatures →** prompt only.

## Prompt structure (fill fresh each time)
- **SUBJECT** — the figure: scale (state it explicitly — e.g. "human-scaled / lean, not hulking" — when it matters), the defining wrongness or signature, key features, pose, condition/gear. Grounding specifics over adjective piles.
- **COMPOSITION** — single figure as focus; state pose and vantage; a suggested moody setting in words is fine (the text renders it — no reference needed).
- **LIGHTING & PALETTE** — one dominant light source + direction; muted desaturated palette + at most one accent; figure legible against the ground, not lost in murk.
- **MOOD** — the emotional register.
- **ART STYLE** — the house-style block above, verbatim.
- **FRAMING** — 16:9 landscape for a creature-in-environment plate, or portrait for a tighter figure; eye-level unless there's a reason otherwise.
- **AVOID** — text/logos/words/watermark; anachronisms (1996 Rust-Belt Ohio); plus scene-specific negatives (e.g. Dogmen: no demonic/Abyssal features, no hulking proportions, no dark-noir murk).

## Worked example
The rank-and-file **[[The Dogmen|Dogman]]** prompt (design conversation, 2026-07) is the first creature built to this convention — human-scaled, human/dog clash, single cool light shaft, desaturated greys, prompt-only (no attached reference), and the result the group preferred.
