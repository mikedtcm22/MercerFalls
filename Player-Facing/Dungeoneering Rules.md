# Dungeoneering Rules

Up: [[Player-Facing Materials]]

**The player-facing summary of the 10-minute Stretch system — how time becomes a managed resource when you cross a dungeon. The DM-side application lives in [[Dungeon Mechanics]].**

This is the rules reference for the first of [[The Two Modes of Play]]: dungeon mode, where dungeons are encounters that pay attention.

## Two Types of Rounds

- **Combat** uses 6-second rounds.
- **Crawls** use **10-minute Stretches**.

A dungeon here is any dangerous location with several encounters and challenges to face.

## Exploring: Crawl Strategy

On entry, the party sets a standing strategy so things stay fair when they go sideways:

- **Marching Order** — who's first, who's last (single-file in 5-ft corridors, double in 10-ft).
- **Default Initiative** — agreed turn order for minor combat, to save time.
- **Visibility** — who carries which light sources (tracked for duration), who has darkvision and at what range.
- **Passive Perception** — noted per character; matters against stealthy monsters (hiding is DC 15).

The DM shares the dungeon's **General Features** up front: construction material, ceiling height, lighting.

## Running a Stretch

Each Stretch, every character picks **one Activity** in a given area. Go around the table (no strict initiative). At the end: resolve choices, **mark off the Stretch**, check for **wandering monsters**, resolve any combat, repeat. Real-time table dialogue ≈ in-fiction time; over ~10 minutes of talk, mark a Stretch. No fractional Stretches.

## 10-Minute Activities

Pick one per Stretch (list is open — invent more at the table):

- **Casting a Spell** — a 10-min or ritual cast; chain Stretches for longer casts.
- **Helping Out** — grant a creature in reach advantage on checks (need a relevant proficiency).
- **Interacting** — read notes/tablets, clear rubble, work a device (may need checks).
- **Keeping Watch** — party can't be surprised by creatures you see; you get a reaction before your first turn if combat starts.
- **Loot Everything** — collect already-discovered gear and valuables.
- **Move Quietly** — (Stealth proficiency) creatures need a Perception check vs. DC 8 + Dex + prof (double prof with expertise) to find you.
- **Search Area** — search a room/area ≤50 ft; Perception or Investigation finds anything hidden at ≤ your result.
- **Take Your Time** — auto-20 on the first d20 test of an action (+5 if it had advantage); can replace a failed action check.

## Wandering Monsters

Tuned with three dials: **Frequency** (Constant = d6 every Stretch / Medium = every 3rd / Hourly = every 6th), **Likelihood** (Unlikely = 6 / Possible = 5–6 / Inevitable = 4–6), and **Difficulty** (Trivial / Challenging / Deadly). Tables are built to fit the environment and party level.

**Unconventional use:** Stretches can run a ticking clock or haunted-house event list instead of wandering monsters — relevant to how Mercer Falls dungeons behave (see [[Dungeon Mechanics]], [[Floor Reset and the Intelligence Gradient]]).
