# Character Creation Guide

Up: [[Player-Facing Materials]]

**How to build a 1996 Mercer Falls kid: full 5e rules with nothing restricted, but every race, class, and background re-flavored into a *type of kid*. Build the mechanics or the kid first — just build the whole thing.**

## The Setting (player-facing version)

Mercer Falls, Ohio, summer 1996, a dying rust-belt town of ~18,000. You play a kid **13–17**. It's effectively the real world: no dragons, elves, or wizards, and *magic isn't real* as far as anyone knows. Strange things happen, but nobody talks about them and nobody quite remembers them the next day (the seed for [[The Reality Filter]], unnamed here). 1996 tech baseline: pagers, rare cell phones, dial-up, Walkmans, VHS, X-Files. You'll play in [[The Two Modes of Play]].

## The Core Translation

- **You start as a normal kid.** Your sheet's racial features, spells, etc. are *dormant* until Session 1, when something wakes them up. Mechanically a tiefling, half-orc, or elf, you still look like a human teenager. (The awakening is the embers — [[The Embers]] — but the guide never names them.)
- **Your "race" is a type of kid.** Everyone is human, so race choice = clique/vibe. Mechanical features stay identical; only the *fictional presentation* changes.
- **All classes are open.** Pre-awakening, class is just who the kid already is (athletic, bookish, sneaky, devout, creative). Spellcasters lose **nothing** mechanically; the fictional presentation is worked out at the table.
- **Backgrounds re-flavor to 1996 small-town life** (noble = rich kid, urchin = latchkey kid, soldier = JROTC/military family, sage = the kid who reads at lunch, folk hero = known around town for a save or incident).

## Race-to-Clique Inspirations Table

Inspirations, **not prescriptions** — mechanical features stay; flavor adapts.

| Race | Type of Kid |
|---|---|
| Tiefling | Goth kid (eyeliner, candle, talks to crows) |
| Half-Orc | Jock (letterman jacket, weight room) |
| Mountain Dwarf | Auto shop kid / gearhead |
| Hill Dwarf | Country / farm kid |
| High Elf | Theater kid / honors student |
| Wood Elf | Outdoorsy / cross-country runner |
| Drow | New kid / the outsider (new to *town*, not othered) |
| Lightfoot Halfling | Skater |
| Stout Halfling | Wrestler / gymnast |
| Rock Gnome | AV club / computer kid |
| Forest Gnome | Naturalist / bio nerd |
| Half-Elf | Popular kid / charismatic in-between |
| Dragonborn | Punk rocker / band frontperson |
| Variant Human | Anyone else (most flexible) |

**Extended (off-PHB):** Goliath = big country kid/lineman; Aasimar = choir/devout kid; Tabaxi = dance squad/gymnast; Firbolg = Wiccan/*The Craft* kid; Genasi by element = runner / wrestler / metalhead / swim team.

## The "Wrong" Clique Is Fine

A tiefling cheerleader, a half-orc honors student, an elf jock, a dwarf theater kid — all great. The mechanics stay; the flavor bends to the kid you want.

## Races We're Not Doing

**Aarakocra** (literal flight), **Centaurs** (four legs), and any race whose mechanical advantage needs a literal physical impossibility for a human. Pitches heard, but flight really doesn't fit.

## Your Kid Has a Life

Beyond the sheet, develop: **family**, **where you live** (see [[The Districts]]), **2–3 people who matter** (future NPCs the campaign can put in harm's way), **what this summer was supposed to be** (so there's something to lose), and **why your kid said yes to the dare** — the campaign opens on a dare to spend the night locked inside the abandoned mill grounds (see [[Session 1 — The Dare]]). Talk to the other players about group dynamics before Session 0.
