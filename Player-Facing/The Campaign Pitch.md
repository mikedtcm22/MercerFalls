# The Campaign Pitch

Up: [[Player-Facing Materials]]

**The light concept/tone/time-commitment pitch sent alongside the [[Character Creation Guide]]. Establishes the genre, the cadence, and the title — which is given *without explanation* so players discover its meaning through play.**

> [!note] Status: provisional
> The exact pitch language is still being sharpened per-recruit (see the pre-campaign timeline). The content below captures what the pitch must convey, drawn from the design docs.

## What the Pitch Conveys

- **Concept** — You play kids in Mercer Falls, Ohio, summer 1996, a dying rust-belt town. It's the real world, with one thing wrong underneath it. Tonal touchstones: *Buffy the Vampire Slayer*, *Stranger Things* (deliberately diverged), *Persona*.
- **Tone** — Small-town American horror in a quiet register: Stephen King's slower passages, the opening of *Stranger Things*, literary-short-fiction prose. Slow-burn dread, not splatter.
- **Time commitment** — Designed around a **1.5-hour standard weeknight session** (Tuesday 6:30–8:30pm CDT in the v0.1 plan), short enough for adults with jobs and families. **Session 1 runs longer (3–4 hours)** as the onboarding session. Weekly cadence; the episodic format tolerates a missing player.
- **Real-time scheduling assumption** — A recurring weekly slot; players are framed as co-investors, not customers (see [[DM-OS — Architectural Principles]]).
- **The title** — ***Embers of the Gathering Hollows*** appears in the pitch **without explanation.** Players are meant to discover what it means through play; its connection to what the PCs carry (see [[The Embers]], DM-side) should land as a late-Act-1 meta-realization, never as a stated fact at character creation.

## The Atmospheric Teaser (companion piece)

A ~100-word "Mercer Falls feels like…" prose teaser was drafted for recruitment (T-4 in the pre-campaign timeline) — the first writing in the campaign's voice, doubling as a tonal taste and a recruitment-conversion tool. Working draft direction: the mill closed in '91, but the chain on the gate is *new* and nobody knows who locks it; the diner stays open because there's nowhere else to go; the new Sycamore houses are the same five floor plans in four colors; "the summer feels like it's holding its breath."
