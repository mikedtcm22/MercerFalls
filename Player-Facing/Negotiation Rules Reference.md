# Negotiation Rules Reference

Up: [[Player-Facing Materials]]

**The player-facing one-pager for the structured social-encounter system (adapted from Draw Steel). The DM-side disposition tracking and campaign application live in [[The Negotiation System]].**

This is the rules reference for the social half of [[The Two Modes of Play]]: when conversations have stakes, they run on this framework instead of a single roll. Per campaign plan, the one-pager is formally handed out when the system is revealed around Episode 6–7.

## When to Negotiate

Only when an NPC has genuine internal tension — they *want* to consider helping but have real reasons to hesitate. Low-stakes asks stay single rolls (a Lie/Intimidate/Flirt test). Negotiation is for high-stakes asks of important NPCs: an item, a service, an ally, a plot-turning piece of information.

**Limits:** Negotiation is not mind control. You can't talk an NPC into acting wildly out of character. What you *can* do is make behaving differently feel *in* character. Threatening violence exits negotiation entirely.

## The Four Stats

The DM assigns each NPC four temporary things:

- **Interest (0–5)** — eagerness to deal. Starts 1–4. Hits **5** → best final offer; hits **0** → ends with no deal (and hostility).
- **Patience (0–5)** — willingness to keep talking. Each NPC starts above 0; hits **0** → next offer is final. Sharing the NPC's native language can raise starting patience.
- **Motivations** (≥2) — appeal to one (without lying or hitting a pitfall) for an easier roll to raise Interest. Each can be appealed to **once**.
- **Pitfalls** (≥1) — using one auto-fails and drops both Interest and Patience by 1.

The twelve possible motivations/pitfalls: **Benevolence, Discovery, Freedom, Greed, Higher Authority, Justice, Legacy, Peace, Power, Protection, Revelry, Vengeance.** The same trait is a motivation for one NPC and a pitfall for another. Research beforehand (diaries, friends, reconnaissance) reveals them.

## Making Arguments

One hero makes each argument (the table can strategize first). An argument needs a *justification* for why it's true. Roll outcomes:

**Appeal to a fresh motivation (easier test):**
- ≤11: Patience −1
- 12–16: Interest +1, Patience −1
- 17+: Interest +1, Patience unchanged
- Great roleplay can auto-count as 17+.

**No motivation or pitfall (harder test):**
- ≤11: Interest −1, Patience −1
- 12–16: Patience −1
- 17+: Interest +1, Patience −1
- Natural 19–20: Patience unchanged.

**Caught in a lie:** extra Interest −1. **Pitfall used:** auto-fail, Interest −1 and Patience −1. Reusing a spent motivation: Patience −1, no Interest change.

## The Offer (by current Interest)

- **5** "Yes, and…" — everything asked, plus a bonus.
- **4** "Yes." — everything asked.
- **3** "Yes, but…" — what you want, plus a small extra ask.
- **2** "No, but…" — a lesser substitute offer.
- **1** "No." — rejected, no counter (may press for a better deal if patience remains).
- **0** "No, and…" — refuses and turns hostile.

When Patience hits 0 or Interest hits 5, the offer is final. Heroes may walk away anytime.
