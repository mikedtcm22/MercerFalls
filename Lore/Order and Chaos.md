# Order and Chaos

Up: [[Lore & Cosmology]]

**The campaign-spanning cosmology, locked in and DM-private: Order built [[The Dungeon]] to contain Chaos, and chose to do it by eating the choice out of everyone nearby.**

> [!danger] DM-only — none of this is exposed to players in Act 1
> The deeper symmetry surfaces gradually across Act 2 through texture beats, not exposition.

## The Two Forces

- **Order** is a cosmic force whose project is a clockwork existence — predictable, regular, frictionless. *Order does not create; Order arranges.*
- **Chaos** is a cosmic force whose project is unbridled, undirected churn — endless conflict that can only end in death.

The two are eternally opposed, and at their scale **both are existential threats** to ordinary mortal life.

## How the Dungeon Came to Be

In deep history, Chaos gathered itself into a coherent entity capable of being released into the world. Order saw the release coming and built the dungeon as a **containment system** to imprison that entity and keep its release suppressed. That contained Chaos is [[The Entity]] — the PCs' "benevolent" patron.

## The Agency-Eating Mechanism

Order reasoned that Chaos arises from beings making uncontrolled, unpredictable **choices**, and that the way to suppress Chaos in the local field around the dungeon was to **drain choice itself out of the surrounding population**. The hollowed mill workers, the missing persons, the citywide apathy (see [[The Reality Filter]]) — this is the containment system working as designed. As a side-benefit, the harvested agency feeds the suppression directly. From Order's perspective this is elegant engineering. From any humane perspective it is monstrous. See [[Hollowing and Agency-Loss]].

## The "Shitty Solution" Thesis

**Order isn't evil.** Order has a real problem and built a real solution. The solution is just morally bankrupt — and it was shaped by people who never had to live inside it. The campaign argues, through the PCs' embodied position, that the right answer to "Chaos arises from agency" is **not** "remove agency" but a third thing the PCs must articulate at level 20. This is the heart of the [[High Concept]]: agency is the answer.

## Creativity Is Chaos-Aligned

A clean expression of the frame: to make something genuinely new is to introduce information the universe didn't previously contain — a Chaos property. Order arranges rather than creates. [[The Nightmare Engine]] is exactly this: the dungeon takes the wild profusion of a population's dreamings and fearings (Chaos) and imposes structure on it — a *filing system for fear*. The PCs' embers sit uneasily here, granting small impossible abilities the structuring logic can't quite catch.

## The Y2K Expiration

The dungeon was designed around a specific cosmological window. The containment field naturally weakens to nothing at the close of the millennium — **December 31, 1999** — by Order's design. Order was confident that by then the agency-harvest would have solved the problem or produced a tolerable outcome. That date is the day the bars come down: either Chaos breaks loose, or Order has won, or the PCs have found a third way. See [[The Countdown Structure]] and [[Cosmology Seeds]].

## The Cosmological Mixture

| Item | Composition |
|---|---|
| [[The Entity]] itself | Pure Chaos |
| **PC [[The Embers\|embers]]** | ~80% Chaos / 20% Order |
| Rival Party embers (success path) | ~80% Order / 20% Chaos |
| [[Wesley Crane\|Wesley]]'s relic & Evil Party anti-embers (failure path) | ~100% Order |

The PCs' mixed composition gives them late-game flexibility — they aren't captured by either side, which is what lets them hold the third position.
