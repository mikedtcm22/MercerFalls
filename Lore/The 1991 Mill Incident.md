# The 1991 Mill Incident

Up: [[Lore & Cosmology]]

**A mass hollowing event at [[The Mill]], publicly remembered as a gas leak that killed seven. The "seven died" is the aboveground death toll, not a dungeon casualty count — and survivors are still in town.**

## The Public Story

Mercer Steel Works closed in **1991** after a sealed industrial accident killed seven workers. Official cause: **gas leak**. The mill has been sealed ever since. This is the version in every record, the paper, and the town's memory.

> [!danger] DM-only — what really happened (late-campaign reveal)
> The mill was built into the bluff that contains the dungeon's main south entrance — its lower works extend down into bedrock and physically transition into [[The Dungeon]]'s upper levels (no clear line where "industrial basement" stops and "something older" begins). The 1991 "accident" was workers **stumbling into the dungeon proper through the mill's lower works** and being **hollowed** — not killed. See [[Hollowing and Agency-Loss]].

## What Actually Happened

A group of workers in the mill's **sub-basement** broke through into the dungeon — investigating an unusual sound, half-real, half-imagined (the river's enclosed channel above plus something deeper). Per the source, the precise trigger is **provisional** (demolition, a basement breakthrough, an unscheduled exploration).

**All of them were hollowed.** All reconstituted at the dungeon entrance and walked back out through the mill's south gate, **past coworkers who saw it happen and don't remember it now** — [[The Reality Filter]] handling the witnesses.

## "Seven Died" = the Aboveground Toll

The seven aboveground deaths in the days that followed were the hollowed workers whose **safer-instinct was gone** and who walked into mill machinery, fell from heights, or did one of the other things that mill work doesn't survive without intact attention (see [[Hollowing and Agency-Loss]] — death comes later, aboveground). The cause of death was genuinely the accident; the foul play happened upstream, in the dungeon.

## The Survivors Are Still in Town

The **other** hollowed workers had safer jobs to return to. They are the ones who **shrug** when asked about the mill now. Mercer Falls' missing-persons numbers **undercount** the real damage — some casualties never got reported missing because they came home, just emptier.

PCs investigating the 1991 list will find some of the names by walking up to them — **[[Henry Voss]]** is canonical (the gas-station counter; *no warmth, the ember has gone out, ash behind his eyes*). Additional named hollowed cases (Pete, Ellen, Carl) are tracked in [[Hollowed Townspeople]]. See also [[Mercer Falls, Ohio]] for the town context.

## Investigative Hooks

- [[The Mill]]'s administration offices hold payroll records, photo files, and the **redacted 1991 incident report** — investigable after Session 1.
- The mill's **south gate keystone** is dungeon-stone; hollowed workers' walks home came out through this gate, and residue accumulates there once PCs can perceive it (mid-Act 2). See [[The Wrongness Layer]].
- A Filter-broken 1991 survivor is a cosmology breadcrumb — may say something that doesn't fit the benevolent-patron frame (see [[The Entity]], [[The Reality Filter]]).
