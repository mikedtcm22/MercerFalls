# The Entity

Up: [[Lore & Cosmology]]

**The PCs' "benevolent" patron — the source of [[The Embers]] — is the very thing [[The Dungeon]] was built to contain. Their level-20 patron is the BBEG.**

> [!danger] DM-only — the campaign's central secret
> The PCs experience the entity as a strange, interventional ally. The simplification "benevolent ally" works fine for Act 1. The truth surfaces gradually, with the explicit reveal in Act 2 or 3.

## What It Is

The entity is the **contained Chaos** — or a fragment, or an emanation of it; exactly what it is can stay flexible (see [[Order and Chaos]]). The load-bearing fact: the being the PCs treat as a benevolent ally is the cosmic-scale Chaos that Order gathered the whole containment system to imprison. It is **pure Chaos** in composition.

## It Wants Out

Sensing weakness in the containment field around 1996, the entity has been able to **extrude pieces of itself into the world** to cultivate proxies — the embers and the [[The Keystone Tablet]]. Helping the PCs is helping itself toward release. Every Filter-break, every ember-power, every death-mitigation is the entity getting more leverage in the world through them. Their growth has been **Chaos-shaped** (at 80% intensity — see [[The Embers]]).

## The Surfeit Cost

When an ember-bearer would be hollowed, the entity pays the dungeon **from its own substance** to spare most of the bearer's agency (the bearer loses only one day). This payment accrues on a hidden counter — working name **Surfeit**. At thresholds the dungeon gets incrementally stronger: [[Wesley Crane]] unlocks new sigil-class abilities, [[The Reality Filter]] strengthens around specific events, and the entity's interventions become subtly more strained. The entity absorbs this long-term cost because it judges the PCs worth the investment toward its own release. The PCs don't see the counter; they may notice bad delve-streaks make the dungeon push back harder.

## The Tells

The entity's nature leaks through over Act 2:

- The **tuning-fork sense** (see [[The Embers]]) starts feeling slightly *off* — too sharp, too eager, too directive. That's the entity's grip starting to show.
- Filter-broken hollowed mill workers may say things that don't fit the benevolent-patron frame — e.g. "the other one, the one that wants us to scream forever" (see [[The Reality Filter]], [[The 1991 Mill Incident]]).
- A Mercer document references the dungeon being built to contain something the PCs eventually recognize as their entity.

## The PCs' Eventual Crisis

When the PCs grasp that their patron isn't benevolent, the choice is: keep accepting help (and become [[Wesley Crane|Wesley]]-shaped vessels) or refuse it (and lose their primary advantage). **The right answer is neither** — find, name, and embody the third way against both cosmic forces. That articulation is the campaign's victory condition.
