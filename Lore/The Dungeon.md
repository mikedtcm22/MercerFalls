# The Dungeon

Up: [[Lore & Cosmology]]

**The dungeon is a machine, not a mind. It has been running over a century. It eats agency.**

This distinction is load-bearing. The dungeon is sophisticated — its creators were cosmic-scale beings building a containment system for a cosmic-scale problem (see [[Order and Chaos]]) — but it is not, in itself, intelligent. It is a tool. Like any sufficiently complex tool, it needs a user to operate it well; without one it is a static, formidable, predictable deathtrap.

## Wesley Is Its Current User

Most of what the PCs read as the dungeon "thinking" — its responsiveness, escalation, seeming to know what they're doing — is [[Wesley Crane]] operating it through abilities granted by his anti-ember relic. The gradient-shuffle (monster relocation, content shuffling, deep-floor layout reshuffles) is Wesley's housekeeping. Before Wesley, [[Elias Mercer]] ran the machine; Mercer built [[The Mill]] over the dungeon's south entrance as cover for his work, and was consumed by it the same way Wesley is being consumed now.

## The Daily Reset Is Intrinsic

[[Order and Chaos|Order]] built the dungeon with an **automatic nightly refresh** — it runs whether Wesley acts or not. What Wesley *adds* on top of the reset is the gradient-shuffle. When he's active, deep floors come back rearranged; when he's incapacitated, the shuffle stops and deep floors come back static. This is a late-Act-1 forensic signal for tracking Wesley's status without seeing him (see [[Forensic Delving]], [[Floor Reset and the Intelligence Gradient]]).

## "Death" Is Hollowing

When a person is "killed" in the dungeon, they don't die there. The dungeon doesn't want their life; it wants their agency. At the moment of "death" the deciding part is taken, the body dissolves into motes that drift up and vanish, then reconstitutes at the entrance and walks home on autopilot. **Time-loss is the symptom; agency-loss is the mechanism.** Full treatment in [[Hollowing and Agency-Loss]].

## Residue

Hollowing leaves **residue** — a thin, unstable shimmer of the agency the dungeon hasn't finished consuming, fading over hours to days.

- Fresh, dense residue → someone was hollowed here within a day or two.
- Faint, fading residue → it's been a while.
- Civilian residue is significant (full agency). **Ember-bearer residue is minimal** (the ember intercepts the transaction). Wesley leaves **none**.
- **Residue persists through reset.** Rule of thumb: *chalk and glow persist; footprints and blood are gone.* Sigils, chalk-cartography, glyphs, and residue survive; drag marks, blood, spent supplies, corpses reset.

A chamber thick with residue is unambiguously where a *civilian* got hollowed. It is also the source of Wesley's "Gathered Dust" ritual ingredient — harvested fresh civilian residue (see [[The Three Ritual Items]]).

## The Dungeon Has Mass, Reads Glyphs, Reacts to Patience

- **Mass.** Each floor is wide as well as deep — a maze of paths, dead ends, side rooms, with a path of least resistance. Exhaustive exploration depletes resources; the strategic question is which side rooms to spend Stretches on.
- **Glyphs.** Doorframes and walls bear glyphs hinting at what lies beyond (DC 15 Investigation). Matching glyphs to outcomes decodes a private dungeon-language — a knowledge resource that **persists across delves and TPKs**. A cultist's notebook of translated glyphs is a major score.
- **Patience-punishment.** The "Take Your Time" Activity stays available, but triggers a wandering-monster roll **with disadvantage**. This is one of the few places the *machine itself*, not Wesley, applies pressure — it's baked into the design and runs on its own.

## Surface Footprint (brief)

A roughly circular region ~half a mile across, centered between the mill bluff and [[Mercer Lake]]. Three entrances: Mill (south), Mansion/[[The Mercer Gate]] (east-southeast), Lake (north). Upper floors 1–5 are **fragmented arcs by sector** and don't connect; the system unifies at floor 6. Full detail in [[The Dungeon's Surface Footprint]].

> [!danger] DM-only
> The dungeon is Order's containment system for the Chaos-entity ([[The Entity]]). Agency-eating is the containment working as designed (see [[Order and Chaos]]). The containment field expires at the close of the millennium (Y2K).
