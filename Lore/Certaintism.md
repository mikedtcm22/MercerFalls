# Certaintism

Up: [[Lore & Cosmology]]

**[[Gern]]'s family's kooky, Scientology-flavored sci-fi religion. It is accidentally *right* that the hollowing is real and observable — and comprehensively *wrong* about what it is: it has the whole [[Order and Chaos]] war backwards, dressed up as a galactic pest-control epic. It is the engine of Gern's arc — a boy raised to (unknowingly) serve Order, powered by Chaos, misreading the entire board — and a gift-wrapped recruiting handle for [[Wesley Crane]].**

> [!danger] DM-only
> The *existence* of Certaintism is player-facing (it's Gern's upbringing). Everything below — that it's Order's gospel with the labels swapped, and what's really happening — is behind the screen. The whole point is that the faith is **true about the phenomena and cartoonishly wrong about the frame.**

## Register — what it sounds like
Scientology by way of a 1970s paperback with a ringed planet on the cover. Past lives, a perfected cosmic authority, a hidden alien infestation, self-improvement-as-cosmic-hygiene, ranks, jargon, maybe a meter. A homeschooled kid quizzed on it at the kitchen table is inherently a little funny and a little sad. **The comedy is the gulf** between the schlocky space-opera frame and the actual cosmic-horror truth underneath; the horror is that the schlock keeps turning out to be *real*.

## Cosmology (in-doctrine)
- **The Concordance** — the perfected intelligence/civilization that eons ago brought the galaxy into **True Alignment**: everything Settled, resolved, in its correct fixed state, at rest. The authority you align *to*. *(Actually: [[Order and Chaos|Order]].)*
- **the Snarl** — the enemy. A swarm-like infestation of "unaligned particulate" that gets into a person and eats their Alignment from the inside, dragging them back down into raw churn. *(Actually: [[Order and Chaos|Chaos]] — i.e. [[The Entity]].)*
- **the stack** — you are not a soul; you're a *stack* of past-life imprints, most of them snarled.
- **the Kernel** — a core of pure Alignment the Concordance issued you, buried down in the stack. The practice is getting it **locked**.
- **auditing / calibration / running the Ladder** — the practice: burn the Snarl out of your stack, lock your Kernel, read as Aligned so the Snarl can't get a grip. (Optional prop: an **Alignment meter**, the E-meter analog, that "reads" how snarled you are.)
- **Locked / Settled / Aligned** = the goal-state: safe, holy, finished. **loose / snarled / unmade** = the failure-state: erasure.

## What it gets RIGHT (the real phenomena)
- **The hollowing is real.** People really are emptied and reduced. Doctrine calls it being **Snarled** — an **Unmaking**, a **nullprint event.**
- **The residue is real.** A hollowed person leaves a seared, human-shaped outline burned into the ground — arms slightly open, a person gone to a stain. Doctrine calls it a **nullprint**, and it is literally illustrated in the family scripture (**"Figure 9"**), captioned as a hazard placard: *this is what the Snarl leaves.* (In-world this rhymes with the ash-stains ringing the [[The Buzzsaw Room|Buzzsaw]] platform and the mark [[Wendell Pace]] leaves in [[The Pillared Reach]].)
- **The walk-home is real.** A hollowed body that still gets up and moves (the [[Hollowing and Agency-Loss|autopilot walk home]]) has a doctrinal slot: *an emptied stack running on residual imprint.*

## What it gets WRONG (the inversion — the actual truth)
Two inversions, stacked — which is why nobody sees through it:
1. **Valence backwards.** Certaintism frames being Snarled as *the enemy winning* — erasure, to be feared and warded off. In truth the hollowing **is** agency-theft — but committed by **Order's containment machine** ([[The Dungeon]]), not by chaos. The thing to fear is real; the culprit is the opposite of the one named.
2. **Sides backwards (the big one).** The **Concordance is Order** — the force that actually wants to end agency (fixity, Alignment, everything Settled and unable to change). **the Snarl is Chaos** — change, questioning, the churn of a living will. Certaintism **worships the agency-killer and demonizes the agency-giver.** Gern's parents are, without any idea, preaching for the enemy.

So Certaintism is **Order's gospel with the labels swapped**, wearing a rubber alien mask so the real metaphysics is invisible. Structurally it preaches the [[The Cast and the Permanent Cast|Permanent Cast]] as salvation: *get Locked, get Settled, never have to carry yourself again* — the cast that never comes off, sold as heaven.

## The low-residue misread (the instrument that points exactly wrong)
The load-bearing hook, and the cruelest part:
- **The fact:** ember-bearers — the **PCs** — leave **almost no nullprint** when they hollow (Chaos is *shielding* them from full erasure). **NPCs leave a full one.**
- **The doctrinal read:** a clean, complete nullprint = a citizen so Aligned the Snarl had to consume them whole. Someone who barely leaves a smudge must be a **partial** — one of *the compromised* — so riddled with Snarl there's almost nothing Aligned left to burn a clean cast.
- **The result:** Gern, reading strictly by the book, looks at his own party and sees **late-stage Snarl carriers.** The diagnostic is real and points *precisely* backwards — the faint mark that doctrine reads as "eaten alive by the enemy" is in fact the enemy-of-Order *protecting* them.

## Gern's arc (the trap)
- **The seed — the Wendell moment (Ep1):** in [[The Pillared Reach]] Gern watches [[Wendell Pace]] hollow and leave a nullprint — **Figure 9, made real.** First read: *my parents were right — and it's worse than they said.* The faith lands as **true and frightening**, not aspirational.
- **Two false self-readings** (both wrong, both rich, play them against each other):
  1. *"The Snarl's in me — I'm one of the compromised, I'm turning."* → dread and shame (rhymes with Gern's belonging/worthiness wound).
  2. *"I've been issued a field-Kernel to hunt the infestation from inside the compromised."* → a commissioned exterminator; **zeal.** The more dangerous read.
- **His fire betrays the frame.** The embers are warmth, reaching, *change* — pure Snarl signature by the doctrine. A faith-literate, honest kid eventually notices *his own gift smells like the enemy.*
- **The truth he can't see:** his power comes from his gospel's *devil* (Chaos), which is the side actually **protecting** agency; the Concordance he was raised to serve is the agency-killer. His third-position arc: neither the faith nor the fire is telling him the whole story.
- **The Wesley handle:** [[Order and Chaos|Order]] would *love* a Chaos-bearer who believes his Chaos-fire is a holy weapon *for* the Settled order — aimed 180° from the truth. [[Wesley Crane]] can affirm the "exterminator" reading and recruit Gern to fight for the very thing that made him. See [[The Cast and the Permanent Cast]] (the Rival pressure-point/saving-grace generator) and [[The Negotiation System]].

## Ep1 seeds
1. **The Wendell nullprint** ([[The Pillared Reach]]) — the Figure-9 recognition; the one planted touchpoint in the Ep1 crawl. Deliver it privately (script below).
2. *(Optional second touch)* **[[The Cataloguing Office]]** — the century-old operation *filing* everything it finds, the plate-camera quietly exposing a plate of the party, can read to a Certaintist kid as the Concordance's **Accounting/auditing** at work: reality keeping its books. A quiet second rhyme if you want it; skip it to keep Ep1 light.

## Open dials (decide when it matters, not now)
- **Certaintism's actual connection to Order** — (a) *pure kook coincidence* that happened to describe real things; (b) a *garbled downstream echo* — someone once witnessed the real phenomenon (a past bearer, a [[The 1991 Mill Incident|1991 survivor]], [[Elias Mercer]]) and built a faith on the wreckage of what they saw; (c) *genuinely Order-touched* — seeded to cultivate humans who *want* to be Settled. Each cranks Gern's stakes differently; ambiguous is fine for a long while.
- **Jargon depth** — currently a moderate apparatus (stack / Kernel / auditing / nullprint / partials, optional meter). Dial up toward full E-meter-and-ranks, or down to a light dusting.
- **Who else is Certaintist** — Gern's family at minimum; open whether it has any other footprint in Mercer Falls.

## Running the recognition beat
Hand it to Gern's player privately (note card, whisper, DM channel):
> *You've seen this exact shape before. It's Figure 9. Your mother had you copy it out of the family scripture until you could draw it from memory — a person, arms a little open, gone to a stain on the floor. The caption underneath was always the same: "A nullprint. This is what the Snarl leaves."*

Then let him react in front of the others and **explain nothing.** Don't confirm or deny. The horror is the ambiguity — *were they right?* — so leave it hanging.
