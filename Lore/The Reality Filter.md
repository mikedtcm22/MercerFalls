# The Reality Filter

Up: [[Lore & Cosmology]]

**Normies in Mercer Falls perceive the wrongness — they just can't muster the will to follow up on it. The Filter is not a perceptual veil; it's a deficit of follow-through.**

## How It Actually Works

People mostly *see* what happens. The breadcrumb is that **no one ever follows up.** A Fireball in the town square is rationalized as a gas main, nobody calls anyone, nobody investigates, and the witnesses don't return to the memory the next day. The bigger the event, the weirder the rationalization — accepted instantly, then abandoned.

> [!danger] DM-only — what the Filter *is*
> The Filter is the dungeon's effect on the town, generalized. A century of agency-eating (see [[Hollowing and Agency-Loss]], [[Order and Chaos]]) has left Mercer Falls with a citywide deficit of follow-through. The stuckness is mechanical — the containment system working as designed (see [[The Dungeon]]). It's why missing-persons stats stay buried, why nobody reopened the mill question, why the town feels stuck.

## Spell Rationalization

| Spell | Observer perceives |
|---|---|
| Burning Hands | Hairspray + lighter / gas-stove flare |
| Sleep | Long day, dozed off |
| Healing Word | Headache just went, weird |
| Mage Hand | The wind, probably |
| Charm Person | I had been thinking about that anyway |
| Fireball | Gas main blew (no follow-up call to the gas company) |

## Item Rationalization (Visibility Gradient)

People see the item; they just don't act. The bigger and weirder, the more strained the rationalization, but the Filter holds.

| Tier | Item | Rationalization |
|---|---|---|
| Invisible | Pocket knife, walking stick, hunting bow | None — normal |
| Easy | Hunting rifle, machete, hatchet, hockey gear | None, in rural Ohio |
| Moderate | Sword, mace, crossbow, leather armor, biker jacket | "Replica," "costume," "movie prop" |
| Strained | Greataxe, halberd, chain shirt, plate breastplate | "LARPing," "Renaissance Faire" — holds but witnesses feel off |
| Maximal | Full plate, exotic dungeon weapons, anything visibly inhuman | Filter working hard; vague unease afterward, can't name what they saw |

The gradient is a tone-and-pacing dial: sword-and-light-armor PCs are nearly invisible in town; a paladin in full plate is making a *choice* every outing. Smart play is the gear stash (see [[Gear and Items]]).

## Who Is / Isn't Subject

| Who | Filter applies? |
|---|---|
| Ordinary adults | Yes — full Filter |
| Young children | Yes, but weakly — may stare, point, gossip; doesn't translate to adult action |
| Authority figures (cops, teachers, principals) | Yes — adults under the Filter |
| The PCs' parents | Yes, but more strained — stronger follow-through pressure, weaker results |
| **Other PCs** | **NO** — they see weapons as weapons |
| **[[Wesley Crane]]** | **NO** — ember-equivalent bearer |
| The rival party (post-equinox) | NO — same reason |
| Hollowed adherents | NO — Filter broken in both directions |
| Filter-broken NPCs (mid-game) | NO |

**Parent edge case:** parents are Filtered but have stronger social follow-through with their own kid. Flagrant home gear-flaunting strains it — "is everything okay at school?" conversations, Filter-permeable enough to express concern but not to act.

## Why the PCs Are Immune

The PCs are immune because [[The Entity]] has touched them — the [[The Embers|embers]] specifically reinforce the bearer's capacity to act.

## Filter-Breaking

Mid-game (Levels 4–10), PCs may learn to **break the Filter for individual NPCs** — a major story-power that recruits allies who can actually act on what's happening.

> [!danger] DM-only — a cosmology breadcrumb
> Filter-breaking is a late-campaign clue about [[The Entity]]'s nature: hollowed mill workers, when finally Filter-broken, may say things that *don't fit* the "benevolent patron" frame. [[Russ]] at [[Panels]] is the strong candidate for the first significant Filter-break — already Filter-touched, half-aware his sister's death didn't add up and that some back-room "antiques" are weirder than he can say.

## The 1991 Historical Implication

The [[The 1991 Mill Incident]] was a **mass hollowing, not a mass death**. Workers stumbled into the dungeon, all hollowed, all reconstituted at the entrance and walked back out past coworkers who saw it and don't remember now. The "seven died" are the ones whose hollowed selves walked into machinery or off heights; the rest had safer jobs and are still in town — PCs can find names on the 1991 list by walking up to them at the gas station (see [[Henry Voss]]).
