# Lore & Cosmology

Up: [[Embers of the Gathering Hollows — Overview]]

The campaign's hidden metaphysics — the load-bearing secrets the players uncover over twenty levels. Almost everything here is **DM-only**: the dungeon is a machine [[Order and Chaos|Order]] built to contain [[The Entity|Chaos]], the agency-eating is Order's morally bankrupt "solution," and the PCs' benevolent patron is the very thing the dungeon was built to imprison. Two notes are **mixed** — [[The Reality Filter]] (player-perceivable rules with a DM-only true cause) and [[High Concept]] (the player-facing pitch wrapped around the hidden frame). The campaign's argument resolves here: **agency is the answer**, the third position against both cosmic forces.

## The Notes

- [[High Concept]] — the elevator pitch and the themes; *agency is the answer*. **(mixed)**
- [[The Dungeon]] — a machine, not a mind: eats agency, Wesley is its user, intrinsic daily reset, residue, reads glyphs, punishes patience.
- [[Order and Chaos]] — the cosmology: Order built the dungeon to contain Chaos; agency-eating as Order's "shitty solution"; the Y2K expiration.
- [[The Entity]] — the contained Chaos that is the PCs' "benevolent" patron; it wants out; the hidden Surfeit cost.
- [[The Embers]] — fragments of the entity in the PCs' chests; fire vocabulary, 80/20 composition, tuning-fork sense, ember-chamber, class acquisition.
- [[The Nightmare Engine]] — the dungeon curates monsters from local culture; the player research mechanic; Russ as research hub.
- [[The Reality Filter]] — normies perceive but don't follow up; spell/item rationalization; Filter-breaking. **(mixed)**
- [[Hollowing and Agency-Loss]] — death = agency-theft, not death; the dissolve, walk home, and lost day; time-loss is the symptom.
- [[The 1991 Mill Incident]] — the mass hollowing remembered as a gas leak; "seven died" is the aboveground toll; survivors still in town.
