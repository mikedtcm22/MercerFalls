# The Embers

Up: [[Lore & Cosmology]]

**Fragments of cosmic substance, granted by [[The Entity]], that lodge inside the PCs — granting their D&D abilities and dampening the dungeon's hollowing-toll. Player-side they are never named; only fire vocabulary.**

## What They Are

The embers are extrusions of [[The Entity]] — the force that opposes [[The Dungeon]]'s hunger and intervenes in a limited way. The PCs experience the entity as benevolent; the truth is more complicated (see [[Order and Chaos]]). The simplification "benevolent ally" works fine for Act 1.

## Player-Facing Language: Fire Vocabulary

DM-side these are **embers**. Player-side they are *never named*. Use a small, consistent vocabulary — **warmth, flicker, kindle, smolder, catch, banked**. Avoid "blaze," "inferno," "flame" (too big/bright). An ember is *small, dim, persistent, and alive* — never roaring up, never going out.

- **Body-coded, not pocket-coded.** Put the warmth *inside the bearer* — "behind your sternum," "in your chest." Never an external glowing object. The body-coding sharpens the eventual ember-realization.
- **Order vs. Chaos inflection.** Order-side relics (Wesley's; failure-path anti-embers) read *steady, banked, low and even — a coal burning long without consuming itself.* The PCs' embers pick up *flicker, catch, spark, uneven warmth.* Same vocabulary, different rhythm — a forensic payoff when a PC notices their relic *feels different* than Wesley's should.
- **Hollowed people, reversed.** [[Henry Voss]] at the counter — *no warmth, the ember gone out, ash behind his eyes.* Same substance, present vs. depleted.

The eventual realization that the PCs have been carrying the embers of the title is designed to land as a **meta-moment** in Act 1 or beyond. See [[High Concept]].

## How the Embers Work

When an ember-bearer would be hollowed, the ember offers the dungeon **alternative payment** from the entity's own substance in exchange for sparing most of the bearer's agency. The bearer loses **one day** (see [[Hollowing and Agency-Loss]], [[Death, TPK, and the Lost Day]]); the dungeon receives compensation. The hidden cost is the **Surfeit** counter — see [[The Entity]].

## Ember Composition (DM-Private)

The embers are not pure Chaos. They sat in the adjacent chamber within the dungeon's field for over a century; a small amount of Order influence intermixed.

| Item | Composition |
|---|---|
| [[The Entity]] itself | Pure Chaos |
| **PC embers** | ~80% Chaos / 20% Order |
| Rival Party embers (success path) | ~80% Order / 20% Chaos |
| Wesley's relic & Evil Party anti-embers (failure path) | ~100% Order |

The 80% Chaos is what the entity spends to pay the dungeon; the **20% Order still extracts the lost-day toll**. Wesley pays no day-cost (pure Order — nothing in him to charge; his cost is radicalization-hollowing). The mix gives PCs late-game cosmic flexibility and implies a **slow radicalization** — each hollowing nudges them toward rigidity/dogma, a dampened version of Wesley's arc. See [[Order and Chaos]].

## The Ember-Chamber

The embers are placed by the entity in response to a specific opportunity, in a chamber that **opens once**, for the chosen people. It sits in a pocket adjacent to the dungeon — safer, calmer, cleaner than the dungeon proper — and **closes permanently** after the embers are taken. This is why no prior dungeon victim ever found embers: the chamber didn't exist for them. See [[The Ember Chamber]].

## Eligibility

The PCs are not uniquely fated. The entity has cultivated contact with **6–12 eligible kids** touched by the dungeon (near-misses, Mercer Falls bloodlines, other markers). The PCs are simply the ones who entered when the entity was ready. The bullies who locked them in could have been the protagonists.

## Class-Appropriate Acquisition

Each ember requires a class-flavored interaction — flavor puzzles ("oh, that's me"), not hard puzzles:

| Class | Acquisition |
|---|---|
| Fighter | Physical commitment — lifting, breaking through, accepting a wound |
| Wizard | Intellectual recognition — reading a glyph, seeing a pattern |
| Cleric | Devotion/empathy — speaking a name, healing something hurt |
| Rogue | Cunning — sleight of hand, deduction, seeing what others don't |
| Bard | Expression — a song, a story, a joke that lands |
| Warlock | Spoken willingness — accepting that something will be different |
| Druid | Connection to growth — moss, a root, the alive thing in the dead place |
| Sorcerer | Nothing — the ember recognizes something innate |
| Ranger | Attention — naming a track, identifying a sound |
| Paladin | An oath, spoken aloud |
| Monk | Stillness — a held breath, a centering |
| Barbarian | Release — a shout, an outburst |

Accept the *spirit* of the action. When taken, embers are **absorbed into the PC** (not inventory), cannot be removed, and go **inert if a PC dies outside the dungeon** (permanent loss).

## Embers as Tuning Forks

Embers subtly stir near the dungeon and near concentrations of dungeon-stuff (a creature in town, a touched place, another bearer) — stronger near other embers. Communicate narratively, no dice — a small warming or flickering in the chest. A soft danger sense. **Late campaign the warming may feel *off* — too sharp, too eager** — an early warning the patron may not be what it seems (see [[The Entity]]).

## The Entity's Other Gifts

The [[The Keystone Tablet]] is granted through the same channel — it warms in the hand the way embers warm in the chest, in the same vocabulary. A cosmology breadcrumb. The campaign treats its discovery (Episode 1–2) as the entity's second beat: *it gave you embers; now it gives you the tool to survive long enough to matter.*

## Other Ember-Bearers

A parallel NPC party of dungeon-touched kids emerges as a counterpart over Act 1. What kind they are depends on PC choices (see [[Wesley Crane]], [[The Other Party (Rival or Evil)]]). Either way they have ember-equivalents and survive the dungeon at PC-comparable rates.
