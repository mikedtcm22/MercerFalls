# The Watcher

Up: [[Bestiary]]

**An eyeless, sound-hunting predator pulled from a B-movie creature feature — the dare-night chaser and the first monster the PCs research and kill.** Episode 1's antagonist, encountered as a young in the dungeon (Session 1) and an adult topside (Session 2).

## Concept and source
A [[The Nightmare Engine|Nightmare Engine]] creature: the dungeon curated it from a B-movie creature feature with cultural reach in [[Mercer Falls, Ohio]]. **The source film is TBD** — lock the title, decade, plot, the exact film moment Russ describes, and one or two production-trivia "tells" before play (prep ref top-priority item). [[Russ]] at [[Panels]] is a fan and identifies it on sight when the PCs describe it; the canonical kill condition comes straight out of the film's climax.

Eyeless humanoid, ~6'4", emaciated, dirt-colored skin, vestigial gills along the neck (a [[The Dungeon|dungeon]]-native anatomy tell — a [[Cosmology Seeds|cosmology seed]] the PCs can't yet read). Native to the dungeon; got out years ago and has lived in the woods around [[The Mill]], picking off animals and the occasional un-missed drifter.

## Statblock target (provisional)
- ~25 HP, +5 to hit, 1d8+2 damage
- **Advantage** when the target makes any sound on its turn (movement, casting, speech)
- **Disadvantaged / staggered** when its attention is split across multiple sound sources
- Perfectly still until a noise, then sudden directed movement toward the source

## How it dies
A **boss fight killable by ordinary means** — not a pure silence-puzzle (see the revised win-condition in the chase doc). Its sound-tracking is an *exploitable weakness*: misdirection (a thrown bottle, a transistor radio, a kid pretending to be lost) commits it to the loudest source; a party that **splits its sound** can cut it down. The optional [[The Keystone Tablet|stone of quietude]] artifact (a Silence-like effect) makes the fight easier but is never required. The film's stated kill — total silence in a confined space — applies hardest when it's cornered.

> [!danger] DM-only
> **Two forms.** The *young Watcher* lives in the Pillared Reach (Floor 1, Room 13) — the nest; it dies in [[Session 1 — The Dare]] and releases spores that **mark one PC's voice**, lighting that PC up acoustically. The *adult* mostly ranges outside the dungeon now; when the kids flee down the hatch it reads them as food heading to the nest and lets them go — then tracks the spore-marked voice into town across [[Episode 1 — The Watcher]] Session 2. The dark irony (for the table to appreciate later): the kids killed the very young the adult thought it was feeding.
>
> **Not Wesley's.** Per [[The Pattern Recognition Arc]], Wesley fingerprint level is *none*; the Watcher was already loose. Chalk markings near the mill are background from his Tally Stone delve.

## Aftermath
Body in dawn light: dirt-colored skin, eyeless face, vestigial gills. PCs may take a tooth (made of something that isn't bone) as a trophy/sample. The sheriff later files it as "bear." No one follows up — the [[The Reality Filter|Filter]] at work.
