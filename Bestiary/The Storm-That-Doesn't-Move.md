# The Storm-That-Doesn't-Move

Up: [[Bestiary]]

**A localized weather phenomenon over the spot where Mike Tinsmith died — residue spillover from Wesley's Floor-2 harvest, made visible at scale. Episode 5's monster.** Lightly sketched — design when prepping [[Episode 5 — Storm-That-Doesn't-Move]].

## Concept
A storm-cloud over [[The Fishing Dock]] that **doesn't move with the wind**. Rain falls only in a circle ~40 feet wide. The water within the circle is *wrong*:
- Fish that swim in surface dead
- People who enter feel briefly hollowed — a moment of "wait, why did I come here?" — before the [[The Reality Filter|Filter]] restores them

This gives the PCs their first sustained look at **agency-loss as a force, not just a state** of certain townspeople — groundwork for the eventual mechanism reveal (see [[Hollowing and Agency-Loss]]). A [[Cosmology Seeds|cosmology seed]].

> [!danger] DM-only
> **The cause.** The [[The Three Ritual Items|Gathered Dust]] that [[Wesley Crane]] harvested from [[Mike and Doug Tinsmith|Mike Tinsmith]]'s hollowing chamber on Floor 2 is escaping back up through Mike's death-site — **residue spillover**. Mike's hollowing is the bizarro-plot cornerstone of this window (late July). Wesley fingerprint level: **clear** — Mike was a community center volunteer, and the investigation surfaces it. This is where **Wesley's name first enters the PCs' mouths** as more than a friendly background figure.

## Resolution
Disperse the storm either by:
- **Clearing the source-chamber on Floor 2** (the residue's origin point), or
- A **localized cleansing at the dock** — which requires materials only available through dungeon delving anyway
