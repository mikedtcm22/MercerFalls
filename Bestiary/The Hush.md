# The Hush

Up: [[Bestiary]]

**An eyeless, sound-hunting predator pulled from a B-movie creature feature — the dare-night chaser and the first monster the PCs research and kill. Built like an ear, not an eye.** Episode 1's antagonist, met as a juvenile in the dungeon ([[Session 1 — The Hush|Session 1]]) and an adult topside (Session 2).

*(Canon name as of the v0.2 redesign; previously filed as "The Watcher" — kept as an alias and the likely in-world film title.)*

## Concept and source
A [[The Nightmare Engine|Nightmare Engine]] creature: the dungeon curated it from a B-movie creature feature with cultural reach in [[Mercer Falls, Ohio]]. **The source film is TBD** — lock the title, decade, plot, the exact film moment [[Russ]] describes, and one or two production-trivia "tells" before play. Russ at [[Panels]] is a fan and IDs it on sight from a description; the canonical kill condition comes straight out of the film's climax. Native to [[The Dungeon|the dungeon]]; an adult got out years ago and has lived in the woods around [[The Mill]], taking animals and the occasional un-missed drifter.

## What it looks like (v0.2 — locked)
Not a humanoid. A **wolf-shaped front** — a head carried forward — but with **no eyes and a blank where the face should be**; great **translucent listening-fans** sweep back like ears. Its **hide eats light** (matte, swallowing — the reason moonlight "won't land right" on it). The body is **spider-economical and multi-limbed**, not radially symmetric yet able to move any direction. It tracks sound in every direction and its limbs drive any direction, so **getting behind it gains nothing — it is never flanked**, even though it visibly *has* a behind. Faster in a straight line than the kids, and tireless.

## The hunt grammar (three states)
- **Amble (calm):** front-first, mundane; a glimpse reads as a stray dog. Deniable.
- **Spin-and-click (searching):** spins in place, legs steering independently, head swinging late, fanning slow echolocation clicks — no fix yet. *The window* to freeze or misdirect.
- **Snap-and-charge (found you):** snaps front-to-bearing and runs, clicks speeding out of the sweep. Run on the snap.
When hunting it **decouples facing from travel** — pours sideways or backward without turning.

## Voice-mimicry (the introduction hook)
It plays back sound it just heard — a friend's voice, or a PC's own name — from the wrong place in the dark; timbre right, affect wrong. Nobody should walk toward the fake voice; it sends a powerless party running on their own. Lead with the voice, then the shape.

> [!danger] DM-only
> **Two forms.** The **juvenile** lives in the **Pillared Reach** (Floor 1) — the nest; it dies in [[Session 1 — The Hush|Session 1]] and, cornered, releases a **defensive spore** that leaves a lingering acoustic **mark** on one PC's voice. The **adult** mostly ranges outside the dungeon now; on dare night it chases the kids on the grounds but **breaks off at the hatch — it hears [[Ricky Vance]]** (alone, easier) and takes him instead (the [[Session 1 — The Dare]] cold-open hunt; Ricky survives raw). Across Episode 1 Session 2 the adult then tracks the **spore-marked voice** into town. Dark irony for the table to clock later: the kids killed the very young the adult thought it was feeding.
>
> **Defensive spore** (both ages; rare for an adult — only when cornered/hurt): a faintly visible burst; CON save or poison + the lingering acoustic mark.
>
> **Not Wesley's.** Per [[The Pattern Recognition Arc]], Wesley fingerprint level is *none* — the Hush was already loose. Mill-area chalk is background from his [[Wesley's Sigils|Tally Stone delve]].

## How it dies
On **dare night it cannot be meaningfully fought** — no eyes, no front, it shrugs off kids' weapons; Session 1 is a **chase**, not a battle (flee to the blast furnace, down the hatch). In **Episode 1 Session 2**, once the PCs research it (via Russ / the film's climax) and arm up, it becomes **killable by ordinary means** — its sound-tracking is the exploitable weakness: misdirection (a thrown bottle, a transistor radio, a kid playing lost) commits it to the loudest source, and a party that **splits its sound** can cut it down. The film's stated kill — **total silence in a confined space** — bites hardest when it's cornered. The optional **stone of quietude** (a Silence-like artifact) makes the fight easier but is never required; it is deliberately **not** in the Pillared Reach (so the party can't neutralize the juvenile with it) — placed elsewhere in the dungeon (location TBD) as a tool they earn toward for the *adult* topside climax. See [[The Keystone Tablet]] / [[Gear and Items]].

**Statblock target (provisional):** ~25 HP, +5 to hit, 1d8+2; **advantage** vs. a target that made sound on its turn; **staggered** when its attention splits across multiple sound sources. Adult and juvenile blocks both still TBD.

## Aftermath
Body in dawn light: the blank eyeless face, the light-eating hide gone slack. PCs may take a **tooth made of something that isn't bone** (dungeon-stone material) as a trophy/sample — a [[Cosmology Seeds|cosmology seed]] and the recurring-material clue in [[The Pattern Recognition Arc]] (called back in [[Episode 3 — The Click]]). The sheriff files the body as "bear." No one follows up — the [[The Reality Filter|Filter]] at work.
